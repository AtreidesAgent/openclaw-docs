## Episode Summary
Stepan Simkin, founder of Squads, discusses the company's evolution from crypto multisig wallets to Altitude—a stablecoin-powered business banking platform. The episode covers the stablecoin stack, why existing banks can't compete on efficiency, and how traditional finance business models depend on inefficiencies that stablecoins eliminate.

---

## Themes

### #stablecoins - Infrastructure as Moat
Squads exists at the "programmable account/programmable wallet" layer—closer to the chain than custody providers (Anchorage, Fireblocks) or regulated fiat layers (issuers, orchestrators, card providers). Simkin's thesis: "the closer you are to the source, the more valuable you are."

**Stack view:**
- **Regulated fiat layer:** Issuers (Circle, Tether), orchestrators, card providers
- **Custody layer:** Anchorage, Fireblocks, Coinbase
- **Programmable layer:** Squads/Altitude—account infrastructure directly connected to blockchains

**Long-term north star:** End-to-end stablecoin settlement without intermediaries. "I should be scanning a QR code and doing a single transaction on-chain... instead of doing all this sort of jiu-jitsu with seven different vendors in the middle."

### #institutional - Two-Customer Architecture
Squads serves two distinct customer bases through shared infrastructure:

**1. Squads (Multi-sig):**
- Crypto-native protocols and companies
- Treasury management with advanced permissions/workflows
- Self-custody with recovery options
- Store of value with on-chain controls

**2. Altitude (Business Banking):**
- Traditional businesses accessing stablecoin rails
- Crypto startups with CFOs/heads of ops who don't touch Ledger
- Corporate cards, accounting, invoicing, mass payouts
- Non-crypto businesses in offshore jurisdictions, law firms, service providers

**Growth pattern:** Crypto startups matured and added traditional finance staff—"we grew with them." Altitude emerged to serve these operational needs.

### #payments - FX Hedging & Payout Orchestration
Altitude's multi-currency thesis: businesses need both USD treasury and local currency obligations. Key feature—just-in-time FX execution:

- Hold USD treasury, earn yield
- Pre-hedge for known obligations (payroll, vendors)
- Convert at optimal rates in advance
- Payout in local currency when due

**Why not rely on orchestrators:** "You don't want to find out what the FX is going to be at the moment when you're actually doing the payout. You want to hedge that in advance."

**Expansion priority:** USD, EUR live; GBP, SGD, BRL, MXN planned as demand emerges.

### #institutional - Neobank Competitor Dynamics
Existing banks and neobanks are moving into stablecoin services but face structural disadvantages:

**Incumbent constraints:**
- Must honor legacy tech stacks
- Already have licensing/regulatory status but move slowly
- Business models depend on inefficiency (float, T+3, correspondent banking fees)

**Competitive opportunity window:** Not forever."Whoever treats stablecoins seriously today and invests in the buildout... will be the most interesting competitors."

**Barbell outcome:** "You're either going to have these giant banks or really cutting edge and innovative fintechs."

### #macro - Currency Dominance & Technology Adoption
Dollar dominance is structural—"we still live in a dollar-denominated world"—but technology adoption will drive diversification:

**Stablecoin thesis:** "It's a movement not about dollars, it's a movement about just better, more efficient technology."

**Timeline:** "It will take maybe 5 to 10 years to fully get there, but it's just a natural evolution."

**Regional variations:**
- US/EU: Dollar treasury + yield optimization
- LatAm/EM: Dollar savings + local currency payouts
- Some economies: Potential dollarization at scale

**Regulatory barriers:** Currency controls in weak-currency countries may prevent open dollarization despite technical feasibility.

### #DeFi - Programmability as Differentiator
Traditional banking UX versus stablecoin-native possibilities:

- **Instant everything:** Deposits, withdrawals, staking unstakes in seconds
- **No T+1/T+3:** "That alone is a huge appeal"
- **Agent-ready:** Future AI agents managing treasury, yield optimization, payout scheduling
- **Composable:** Internet-native capital markets accessible from business accounts

**Key insight:** Internet-scale programmability enables features impossible on legacy rails—"all the things we obsessed over in web2 and SaaS, now we're getting to do that in financial infrastructure."

### #payments - Inter-Company Settlement
Enterprise treasury management opportunity:

- Multinationals with 40+ entities
- In-house banks managing inter-company transfers
- Quarterly royalty payments to IP holding companies (e.g., Irish subsidiaries)

**Stablecoin value prop:** "Settle those obligations internally 24/7 instantly and for free instead of moving money through the banks."

**Sales motion:** Map existing banking costs (SWIFT fees, account maintenance) to stablecoin alternatives—"here's how much you're paying now."

### #policy - Regulatory Strategy
Altitude's license approach: "minimum viable regulatory stack" rather than comprehensive licensing.

**Questions they're solving:**
- Which licenses needed per jurisdiction
- Self-custody vs. custody arrangements
- Compliance without legacy bank infrastructure

**Geographic strategy:** Start with USD/EUR; expand to GBP, SGD, then BRL/MXN based on demand signals.

## Notable Predictions

1. **5-10 year migration:** Full transition to stablecoin settlement rails across major economies
2. **Neobank displacement:** Fintechs not adapting will be displaced by stablecoin-first companies
3. **Barbell banking:** Either giant scale banks or hyper-innovative fintechs; middle squeezed out
4. **Currency controls as friction:** Regulatory, not technical, barriers will limit EM dollarization
5. **Inter-company first:** Enterprise treasury stablecoin adoption will start with internal settlements, not external payments
