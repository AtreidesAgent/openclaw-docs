# 2021 Year in Review: Community Engagement

**Published:** Tue, 28 Dec 2021 19:19:30 +0000
**URL:** https://neonewstoday.com/general/2021-year-in-review-community-engagement/
**Tags:** Events, General, AxLabs, bNEO, BOWL, CAT, COZ, FLM, GM, Nekoin, Neo Live, NeoSPCC, NEXT, NNT, NoBug, O3, REFU, SFI, TMN, TTM

Neo achieved a significant milestone this year with the launch of N3, but 2021 still carried the scars of the global pandemic that took hold in 2020. While the blockchain industry witnessed the cancellation of almost all in-person events in 2020, 2021 saw the slow return of conferences, meet-ups, and international travel. However, offline events were still much less frequent than pre-pandemic times.

Just as the year before, circumstances did not allow for Neo-led in-person community events in 2021, such as DevCon or the community assembly. It was not until November that contributors from the Neo ecosystem were able to finally share a stage again. Flamingo Finance maintainer Odd Røland and NGD Zürich lead Lili Zhao joined a panel at Coin Telegraph Epicenter, where Zhao also delivered a talk on N3. It marked the first time in nearly two years that members from different groups within Neo were able to gather.

Despite the lack of in-person events, the community remained connected. The focus for the first half of 2021 was squarely on shipping Neo N3. Once this milestone was achieved in August, the level of community engagement sharply increased as Neo began work to attract new developers, projects, and users. In the months that followed, Neo held dozens of AMAs, interviews, podcasts, video appearances, DevLog sessions, and competitions.

Neo Frontier Launchpad and Technical Workshops

The launch of the Neo Frontier Launchpad event was a major initiative by Neo Global Development, executed with the support of the community. The Launchpad hackathon competition was designed to encourage developers to start new projects on Neo N3, and gave birth to up-and-coming projects such as Rentfuse, Humswap, TOTHEMOON, Lyrebird, and NekoHit.

NGD offered more than $100,000 in prizes, with three major prizes awarded to the best NFT, DeFi, and General projects. Major prize winners also received incubation support through the Neo EcoBoost program.

There were two phases of the Launchpad: Planathon and Development. The Planathon phase of the event ran from May 24 through June 7 and allowed teams to submit their project plan and receive feedback. Participants who submitted the best plans were granted “multipliers” between 1.1x and 1.5x, applied to the team’s final prize.

The month-long Development phase of the Launchpad event kicked off on June 12. While participants were building their projects, the Neo developer community was readily available to provide technical support. NGD hosted a series of workshops throughout the month, including introductions to Python contract development, DeFi, NeoFS, NFTs, and governance on N3.

Following the Launchpad event, NGD showcased the top 13 projects, with each giving live online demonstrations of their work over the span of a week. Of these top projects, many were accepted into the Early Adoption Program, which offers US $10 million in grants for qualified teams and individuals. The program’s goal is to increase overall participation in the Neo ecosystem.

Neoverse

Another major community engagement initiative was the Neoverse campaign. Launched to celebrate the release of Neo N3, Neoverse is an NFT collectible project where participants can open blind boxes, find random fragments, and then combine them to mint special edition Neoverse art NFTs.

27,000 blind boxes were made available, with 9,000 distributed through airdrops and collaboration with community projects. The remaining 18,000 were sold on the Neoverse website for 2 GAS each.

A total of nine fragments could be collected, each representing a different aspect of Neo N3, such as distributed storage, smart contracts, and oracles. Upon collecting all nine, a user could burn them to mint a Neoverse collectable art NFT. Three rarities of Neoverse collectibles were available – N, E, and O, each with three different designs. Holders of these NFTs are granted special VIP privileges within the Neo ecosystem, such as access to special drops and events.

Neo Live

Neo Live is an AMA series hosted by NGD on the official Neo Telegram channel. The series began in 2019 and serves to connect the community with subject matter experts, developers, and dApps in the Neo ecosystem. The community is invited to submit questions for guests, and the best questions are rewarded with tokens. A short quiz is also held at the end of every session, giving the community further opportunities to win prizes by answering questions fastest and most correctly.

The Neo Live series had a slow start at the beginning of 2021, but by September had picked up frequency with 19 Neo Live sessions held over the last 16 weeks of the year. Guests included projects who were new to the ecosystem, such as members of the N3 Early Adoption program and Frontier Launchpad winners, and existing sponsored community members. Many of the transcripts are located at www.neonewstoday.com.

Guests included NGD and Neo Foundation employees, NGD Enterprise, community developers, project founders, and industry consortia.
