## Analysis

### Summary
Dylan delivers his most pointed critique of the Neo ecosystem to date, systematically cataloguing its failures — developer retention crisis, zero dApps beyond Flamingo, co-founder infighting, poor communication, and lack of top-down leadership — before presenting a concrete three-pillar plan to fix Neo: native USDT/USDC support, RWA onboarding, and fixing Flamingo Finance's liquidity and bridging infrastructure. He estimates the entire fix would cost less than 5% of the foundation's treasury.

### Topics Covered
- Developer retention crisis: N3 launched at the bull market peak, failed to attract/retain developers
- No dApps beyond Flamingo; Neo N3 is effectively "the Flamingo chain"
- DeFi activity near zero; Flamingo TVL collapsed from $1.6B to $2.6M
- Co-founder dispute: Da vs. Eric fighting over ~$400M treasury access
- Poor communication and the absence of a roadmap before Eric's return
- Top-down vs. bottom-up leadership: community can only do so much without foundation support
- Core developers begging for a unified direction; debates without implementation
- CenterPoint events organized by Dean at Neo News Today — produced ideas but no enforcement power
- Dean's governance proposal for a Strategy and Reserve Board
- Non-Fungible Items tech as Neo's best booth attraction — never invested in
- GrantShares team folding due to lack of top-down support
- Stablecoin thesis: presented to Da Hongfei in October 2023; stablecoin market doubled since then
- Tron comparison: similar architecture to Neo but 100M active addresses and $84.5B stablecoin cap
- RWA thesis: market grew from $556M (2021) to $24.36B (2026); enshrines chains against death
- Avalanche RWA example: grew from $300M to $1.3B TVL despite poor UX
- Fix Flamingo: enhanced token incentives, multi-tiered staking rewards, lower fees, decentralized bridging
- Flamingo building FLM Fun on BNB chain because Neo is "useless" for it
- Eric's proposed changes to gas yield distribution criticized — don't remove governance voting incentives
- Third-party bridge solutions: Wormhole, Layer Zero, SquidRouter, ThorChain, Maya Protocol
- Estimated cost for all three pillars: $15-20M (2.5-5% of foundation treasury)
- Spoon OS credited as positive AI initiative but needs N3 support
