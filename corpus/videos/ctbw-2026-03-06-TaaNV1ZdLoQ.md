## Analysis

### Summary
A walk-and-talk with Connor Holliman, on-chain recruiter at BASE (Coinbase's L2), discussing the value of IRL recruiting at crypto conferences, bear market hiring dynamics, and a practical playbook for getting hired in crypto. Connor outlines three ways to stand out: building (even non-technical mini apps), creating content, and trading/research. They also discuss handling rejection in the interview process and the importance of resilience and recruiter relationships.

### Topics Covered
- Connor's role as on-chain recruiter at BASE, part of a five-person BASE/CDP recruitment team
- IRL recruiting vs. online sourcing — meeting candidates in person is a "game changer"
- Comparing ETH Denver 2026 (bear market vibes) to DevConnect Buenos Aires (more hopeful)
- Bear market as a "purge" filtering bad projects; resilient builders thrive in the next bull
- Bear market hires tend to stay in their jobs longer than bull market hires
- Three ways to stand out for crypto jobs: (1) Building — apps, mini apps, using BASE documentation; (2) Creating content — written or video to brand yourself; (3) Trading/research — DeFi summer era research as a career catalyst
- Handling interview rejection: connect with recruiters, get resume feedback, improve interview skills
- Connor's recruitment philosophy: helps candidates even when they're not the right fit
- BASE Batches international accelerator program — looking for startups, robotics (sponsored by Virtuals), and student projects
- LinkedIn vs. X for job networking — Connor prefers X for direct communication
- Connor training for a half Ironman after completing a marathon
- BASE open roles at base.org/jobs and coinbase.com/careers

### Guest Perspectives
- **Connor Holliman** (On-chain Recruiter, BASE/Coinbase): Second bear market hiring cycle; finds bear market hires more resilient and longer-tenured. Three key ways to stand out: (1) Build something, even a simple app using BASE's docs for non-technical builders; (2) Create content to brand yourself — video or written — showing industry knowledge beyond the scope of the role; (3) Trade and research to build deep domain knowledge (how he personally entered the space during DeFi summer 2019-2020). Actively helps candidates who aren't the right fit improve their resumes and interview skills. Currently recruiting for BASE Batches accelerator with tracks for startups, robotics (Virtuals-sponsored), and students. Prefers X over LinkedIn for direct candidate communication.
