## Analysis

### Summary
Dylan speaks with Chase Devens, a research analyst at Messari, during the week of the FTX collapse in November 2022. The conversation covers Messari's mission to provide transparent crypto market intelligence, Chase's entry into blockchain during COVID quarantine in 2020, the multi-chain thesis and cross-chain interoperability challenges, how FTX's collapse impacts the industry's regulatory outlook, and emerging technology trends like ZK snarks and decentralized social protocols.

### Topics Covered
- Chase's entry into crypto: spring 2020 during COVID quarantine via a college friend introducing him to DeFi
- Messari's role: providing standardized, contextualized blockchain data for informed decision-making
- The University of Wisconsin pipeline: Messari's "Decoding Web3" tour for college recruitment
- Ethos of decentralization at Messari: transparency, censorship resistance, user-owned future
- Research process: analyst expertise areas, customer feedback, team debates, objective stance
- Multi-chain thesis: 2021 discovered multi-chain world, 2022 learned its dangers ($2B+ in bridge exploits)
- Cross-chain approaches: Axelar (MPC validators), Nomad (optimistic with fraud proofs), LayerZero (oracle-based)
- IBC (Cosmos) as the gold standard but resource-intensive; Succinct Labs building ZK proof of consensus
- Thorchain for L1-to-L1 atomic swaps: capital inefficient but viable for niche use
- FTX collapse impact: destroying institutional confidence, creating demand for proof of reserves
- SPF's DC presence: initially viewed as crypto's advocate, revealed as potentially self-serving
- Proof of reserves: using blockchain transparency to verify exchange solvency
- Predictions for 2023: ZK snarks implementations going live, decentralized social (Lens Protocol)
- Undercollateralized lending based on social reputation via Lens

### Dylan's Takes & Opinions
- Dylan described the FTX collapse week as "decades happen in weeks" and admitted doing "a complete 180 on what I thought SPF was doing."
- He views proof of reserves as a positive outcome: "we can use our own technology of crypto and open verification to actually serve the world better."
- Dylan expressed that "there are decades where nothing happens and then there are weeks where decades happen."
- He noted the cross-chain space needs to be understood from first principles: messaging systems are the foundation, bridges sit on top.

### Guest Perspectives
**Chase Devens (Research Analyst, Messari):** Chase argued that the cross-chain problem must be approached from first principles: chains need messaging systems first, bridges second. He views oracle-based cross-chain solutions (LayerZero's approach) as promising because oracles are already embedded in DeFi applications. He expressed cautious optimism about ZK snarks going live in 2023 after 12-18 months of incredible development. He described the FTX collapse as potentially setting back institutional confidence for years but saw the silver lining in proof of reserves and transparency demands. On Messari's ethos: "code is the regulator" for DeFi protocols; centralized entities are where regulation should focus.

- **Messari** - Crypto market intelligence provider

- Cross-chain interoperability as the most critical unsolved infrastructure problem

- The multi-chain thesis: inevitable but fraught with security challenges
- ZK technology as the most promising technical trend for 2023 and beyond
- Centralized entities (not DeFi protocols) as the source of crypto's biggest failures
