## Analysis

### Summary
Episode 57 features Tyler Adams (COZ CEO), discussing lessons learned running an OG Web3 developer community since 2017, how COZ expanded beyond pure development into hardware (NFIs), wallets (Neon), and regulatory compliance. The conversation covers COZ's early days as one of the first legal DAOs, tips for first-time crypto conference attendees, Neon Wallet cross-chain swapping data, Neo Bond program successes, and COZ's plans to support "vibe coders" building with AI-assisted development tools.

- COZ genesis story: Tyler found Antshares while researching consensus algorithms at Seagate
- COZ as one of the first legal DAOs — prototype for Colorado's DAO corporate structure
- Peak COZ: ~400 contributors (most small PRs); ~50-60 regular contributors
- Scaling challenges with the vote-and-reward contribution model
- How COZ expanded roles beyond developers: hardware (NFIs), wallets (Neon), regulatory compliance
- Tips for first-time crypto conference attendees
- Neon Wallet cross-chain swapping data and adoption
- Neo Bond program successes: Foundation standing with token holders
- COZ's plans to support "vibe coders" — developers using AI-assisted tools
- The evolution of developer communities in blockchain
