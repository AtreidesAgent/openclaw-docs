## Analysis

### Summary
Dylan hosts Alan and Christopher from the Quai Network for a deep conversation about proof-of-work economics, censorship resistance, and the SOAP (Subsidized Open Market Acquisition Protocol) merge-mining model. The episode explores Quai's first year post-mainnet, its innovative approach to offsetting mining emissions through merge mining with Bitcoin, Litecoin, Dogecoin, Ravencoin, and Bitcoin Cash, and the broader cypherpunk values that underpin the project.

### Topics Covered
- The persistent value of censorship resistance in 2026, citing Uganda's internet shutdown and X/Cloudflare outage
- Quai Network's elevator pitch: scalable proof-of-work for peer-to-peer electronic cash
- Cypherpunk values: financial sovereignty, privacy, decentralization
- Lessons learned one year after Quai's mainnet and TGE launch
- SOAP (Subsidized Open Market Acquisition Protocol): merge mining to offset token emissions and create buyback pressure
- Merge mining mechanics across multiple algorithms (CucPal, SHA, Scrypt, potentially RandomX)
- The Qi (Chi) flat coin and its relationship to mining difficulty
- Two-week lock on mining rewards as an economic buffer
- Proof-of-work economics and why small PoW projects struggle with sell pressure
- Christopher's background: Lido, Bankless, Opolis, and the Austin blockchain community
