## Analysis

### Summary
A short, punchy episode at ETH Denver's Build Station with Armand, a serial hackathon participant sponsored by DRPC (Decentralized RPC Provider). They discuss the economics of traveling to hackathons worldwide, how persistence and refusing to quit are key to winning bounties, his approach to AI tooling (uses it but doesn't trust autonomous agents), and his current hackathon project — MaritimeDAO, a cargo ship tokenization platform.

### Topics Covered
- Armand's hackathon lifestyle — sponsored by DRPC for two years, supplemented by bounty winnings
- DRPC — decentralized RPC provider with 100+ chains, plus analytics tooling (Core and Cloud products)
- The economics of serial hackathon participation (flights, hotels covered by sponsor; bounties as bonus)
- Key to winning hackathons: persistence when documentation is bad and others quit
- Idea generation challenges — asking sponsors for RFPs and idea validation
- Building 100+ projects across hackathons without reusing code
- Using AI tools but refusing to use autonomous cloud bots ("I don't trust AI")
- Current project: MaritimeDAO — cargo ship tokenization platform
- Programming language versatility — Solidity, TypeScript, JavaScript, Rust, Cairo, 60+ languages
- ETH Denver hackathon internet issues at the new venue
- Spanish language learning advice: "A Latin couple"

### Dylan's Takes & Opinions
- **AI adoption is becoming universal**: Asks "Do you think we're at a place where everybody should be learning how to do AI and not just developers?" — probing whether AI literacy is becoming a baseline skill.
- **Cloud bots as a conversation topic**: Asks directly "Are you running a cloud bot right now?" showing his interest in how builders use AI agents.

### Guest Perspectives
- **Armand** (Serial hackathon hacker, DRPC ambassador): Has been sponsored by DRPC for two years to attend hackathons globally. Has built over 100 different projects.
