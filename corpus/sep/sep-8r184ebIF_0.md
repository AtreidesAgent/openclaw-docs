## Analysis

### Summary
Dylan speaks with Chad Barraford, a core developer at THORChain, about the importance of decentralized cross-chain swapping, how THORChain's liquidity pool model differs from atomic swaps and bridges, the protocol's economic security model via RUNE, THORChain's Savers product and forthcoming lending protocol (zero interest, no liquidations, no expiration), and the Archeo project for decentralized blockchain data access.

### Topics Covered
- Chad's origin story: software engineer at RStudio (R programming language tools) for 6 years; bought first Bitcoin October 2017, quit in December 2017
- Met THORChain co-founders in 2019; spent 9AM-midnight 7 days a week for 1.5 years building the codebase
- Why THORChain matters: centralized exchanges as the only way to move value between chains is "fundamentally broken" and should "offend all of us"
- How THORChain differs from atomic swaps: person-to-pool vs. person-to-person; always-available counterparty, no cancellations, no waiting
- How THORChain differs from bridges: native swaps vs. wrapping tokens; no one-to-one representative tokens on other chains
- RUNE as the intermediary asset in liquidity pools: BTC→RUNE→ETH swap path
- THORChain's economic security model: RUNE deterministically accrues value as more assets are deposited
- Savers product: realistic yields (4% BTC, 6% ETH) backed by actual swap fees, not manufactured yield
- Comparison to BlockFi: BlockFi had more BTC than Liquid Network and Lightning Network combined at just 0.02% APY
- Forthcoming lending protocol: zero interest, no liquidations, no expiration; structurally different from MakerDAO/Aave/Compound
- When collateral drops below debt value, it's actually beneficial for the network
- Archeo project (with John from ShapeShift and Michael Kirkland): decentralized blockchain data access to replace Infura
- Infura's centralization problems: can disable contracts (Tornado Cash), collect IP/wallet associations
- The cycle of forgetting core principles: each new cohort must relearn lessons from Mt. Gox, FTX, Celsius, Terra/Luna
- DeFi's true purpose: financial empowerment, not degen gambling or chasing unsustainable yields

### Dylan's Takes & Opinions
- Dylan has been a THORChain fan "for years" since BEPSwap testnet days and was "a little nervous" about this interview because of how passionate he is about the project.
- Dylan quit his government job as an urban planner in October 2018 "at the very bottom of the bear" to cover the NEO blockchain full-time.
- Dylan was "burnt by FOMO" missing out on OHM and Magic Internet Money's high APRs, making THORChain's realistic yields refreshing.
- Dylan recalls 2017 when "atomic swaps" were the future everyone was talking about, and is surprised the industry doesn't celebrate THORChain's achievement of native L1-to-L1 swaps more.
- Dylan mentions a NEO core dev who had trouble understanding how THORChain differs from bridging — used this as a prompt for Chad to explain.

### Guest Perspectives
**Chad Barraford** (Core Developer, THORChain):
- "I take personal offense" that centralized exchanges are the only way to move between chains — "that should offend all of us as a cryptocurrency community."
- THORChain is "one of the most technically complex technically ambitious projects in DeFi if not in larger crypto."
- Savers yields will naturally come down as more capital enters — "we can't promise 20% fixed rate like Terra UST; that's nonsensical."
- The lending protocol will make "everything else look like antiquated overnight" — zero interest, no liquidations, no expiration, cross-chain Bitcoin collateral.
- "The point of DeFi isn't to degen people and make them millionaires... it's to create financial primitives that allow people to empower them to accomplish what they want."
- Each crypto cycle brings new people who haven't learned from Mt. Gox, FTX, Celsius — "they have to learn it the hard way as we all did."
- Archeo addresses a critical missing infrastructure piece: decentralized blockchain data access without centralized gatekeepers like Infura.
