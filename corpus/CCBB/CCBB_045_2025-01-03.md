## Analysis

### Summary
First CC&BB of 2025. Dylan and Edge reflect on the importance of unplugging over the holidays, celebrate the social layer of longstanding developer communities in Neo, discuss the LRB Fund distribution for former Liar Bird token holders, explore rebuilding trust after 2022's collapses (SBF, Celsius, Terra Luna, Do Kwon extradited), mark Bitcoin's 16th Genesis Day / Proof of Keys Day, and address quantum security concerns from Google's Willow chip announcement.

### Topics Covered
- Both hosts unplugged for the holidays — celebrating maturity in work-life balance
- The social layer of blockchain: longstanding developer communities still building in Neo (COZ, Nash referenced from 2017)
- The "three lifetimes of blessings" fiasco from November 2017 as ecosystem history
- Will 2025 finally be the year longevity matters to speculators? Edge says not until "ossification" at both tech and app layers
- 2024's killer use cases: Pump.Fun and Polymarket (gambling and prediction markets)
- LRB Fund distribution: former Liar Bird token holders can claim assets by sending tokens to distribution address
- Algorithmic stablecoin viability: Edge sees over-collateralized algorithmic stablecoins as a middle ground
- Rebuilding trust: Do Kwon extradited to US (Jan 2, charged with $40B fraud), Celsius founder pleading guilty, CZ's sentence
- Edge divides retail into two groups: those who want to get rich and those who don't care about blockchain
- Bitcoin Genesis Day (16 years): first block mined January 3, 2009 with UK bank bailout headline
- Edge's theory: Satoshi was probably British based on white paper language
- Thought experiment: what happens if Satoshi's Bitcoin are moved? Edge: "they'll get moved eventually because quantum secure keys means migrating addresses"
- Google Willow quantum chip: Edge says we won't worry about quantum threats this decade but eventually old keys will break
- Neo's quantum security claims from the 2017 white paper: Edge calls it "definitely a buzzword" but acknowledges the system is designed for easy signature scheme swaps

### Dylan's Takes & Opinions
- Proud of the team's ability to unplug: "I'm pretty proud of us for having the ability to step away for a week."
- On whether longevity will matter in 2025: "is this the year that blockchain enthusiasts finally start to give a shit about the longevity of the networks?"
- On trust rebuilding: sees government enforcement (SBF jailed, Mashinsky pleading, Do Kwon extradited) as necessary but not sufficient — Edge points out small-scale scammers remain the bigger threat.
- On Bitcoin's Genesis Day: amused that the UK gets the blame via the bank bailout headline in the first block.
- On quantum security: acknowledges Neo was ahead of the curve with dual-token staking model but accepts Edge's reality check that the 2017 white paper's quantum claims were "a buzzword."
- Bitcoin ATM anecdote: highest activity is in the poorest US neighborhoods (food deserts), which he sees as evidence crypto is reaching the unbanked. Edge counters it may be more like scratch card gambling.

### Guest Perspectives
- **Edge** — On longevity mattering: "before we get there, there has to be some sort of ossification" at both tech and application layers. On the LRB Fund: comparable to Terra Luna — "I was open-minded to the possibility that an algorithmic stablecoin could work, but it has to be robust enough." On trust: divides retail into those wanting to get rich and those who don't care about blockchain — until phishing/social engineering scams are solved, "there will never be trust in blockchain." On Satoshi: "if indeed was one person, was probably British" based on white paper language. On quantum: "it's far from a solved problem. And we are far, far, far away from any of these real-world practical keys being broken." On Neo's staking model: "we had the luxury of being a nobody when Anshes was launched, so we could take that sort of risk."
