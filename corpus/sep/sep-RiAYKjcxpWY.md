## Analysis

### Summary
Dylan speaks with Joshua McDougall, president of Slow Ninja, about his decade-long journey building games across Bitcoin, Litecoin, Ethereum, Lightning Network, EOS, and now Cosmos SDK. The conversation covers the philosophy of building fun games first (not blockchain games), the "5X" model that adds exchange to the classic 4X strategy genre, and how Structs will use faction-based governance tied to validators to ensure the game outlives its creators.

### Topics Covered
- Josh's discovery of Bitcoin in 2011 and attempts to "break it"
- Building CoinDroids on Bitcoin/Litecoin (2014): shooting money at each other as a teaching tool
- Tamagotchi-style game on Ethereum; value leakage to miners as a problem
- Lightning Network's invoice requirement making it unsuitable for gaming
- Failed EOS development experience
- Discovery of Cosmos SDK: working demo in three weeks
- Structs as a 5X game (explore, expand, extract, exterminate + exchange)
- Forking Secret Network for encrypted random number generation
- IBC and interchain accounts enabling composability without leaving the game
- Faction-based governance tied to validators (called "reactors")
- Game persistence: as long as one faction runs a validator, the game continues
- Community-contributed art, moderation, and the "penis palace" problem
- C4 non-profit for blockchain education and standards
- Anti-crypto bias in gaming communities and advertising platforms

### Dylan's Takes & Opinions
- Dylan shares his experience at a Consensus demo booth where hundreds of people lined up to scan QR codes and interact with blockchain, reinforcing that experiential onboarding works.
- Dylan appreciates that Structs' approach of hiding blockchain complexity aligns with how "people don't care if Postgres powers World of Warcraft."

### Guest Perspectives
**Joshua McDougall (President, Slow Ninja):** Spent a decade learning what doesn't work across multiple blockchains before finding Cosmos SDK as the right fit. Prioritizes making a fun game above all blockchain features. Believes games should "trick people into having fun before they realize what's actually happening under the hood." Sees IBC composability as the killer feature - using existing Cosmos ecosystem DEXes and NFT marketplaces rather than rebuilding them. Plans faction-based validator governance so the game persists without Slow Ninja's involvement.

### People & Projects Mentioned
- **Slow Ninja** - Blockchain gaming studio building Structs
- **Structs** - 5X strategic game of interstellar enterprise on Cosmos
- **CoinDroids** - Earlier Bitcoin/Litecoin-based game used at Defcon conferences
- **C4 (Cryptocurrency Certification Consortium)** - Non-profit Josh co-founded for blockchain education
- **Cosmos SDK / IBC** - Framework powering Structs' blockchain
- **Secret Network** - Forked for encrypted random number generation
- **Osmosis** - Cosmos DEX that Structs plans to integrate via IBC
- **Stargaze** - Cosmos NFT marketplace for potential asset trading
- **Defcon** - Security conference where CoinDroids was demonstrated
- **Defcoin** - Intentionally worthless token used for educational purposes

### Recurring Themes
- Building fun experiences first, blockchain features second
- The decade-long iteration required to find product-market fit in blockchain gaming
- Education through play as the most effective onboarding mechanism
- Composability and interoperability (IBC) as blockchain gaming's killer feature
- Decentralized game persistence through validator-based governance
- Anti-crypto sentiment in gaming communities as a distribution challenge

### Content Ideas & Future Topics

- Follow-up on Structs' testnet launch and faction formation.
