## Analysis

### Summary
Dylan speaks with Rebecca Liao, co-founder and CEO of Saga, a protocol that allows developers to spin up L1 blockchains (called "chainlets") as easily as launching a smart contract. The conversation covers the current sentiment of the blockchain space post-EthCC, why the industry needs new users, Saga's focus on gaming and entertainment verticals, the evolution of web3 gaming from play-to-earn to genuine entertainment experiences, Saga's gasless and publisher model for indie game developers, and Rebecca's political career advising on foreign policy and tech policy.

### Topics Covered
- Rebecca's background: corporate lawyer, AI startup (Globality, backed by SoftBank), first crypto startup (Skuchain for DeFi/SMB liquidity), political campaigns (Clinton 2016, Biden 2020)
- Saga's value proposition: L1 to launch L1s with infinite horizontal scalability, gasless transactions, interoperability-first
- Chainlets as analogous to cloud server instances that elastically scale
- Why individual chains matter: avoiding congestion, gas fee variability, and shared block space limitations
- dYdX as an example: 18 months to migrate from Ethereum to Cosmos even with significant engineering resources
- EthCC 2024 sentiment: unprecedented complaining, recognition that the industry needs new users and has over-indexed on infrastructure
- Bipartisan crypto policy: Rebecca's argument that crypto must work with both political parties regardless of personal feelings
- Web3 gaming evolution: from play-to-earn (2021) to genuine entertainment and gasless experiences
- Why big AAA studios won't save crypto gaming - change comes from grassroots indie developers
- Saga Origins: publishing mature-rated, provocative, expansive games that push the envelope
- Indie Summer: onboarding web2 indie developers who recognize blockchain's creative freedom
- Saga's anti-grant philosophy: providing users and go-to-market support rather than cash grants
- Airdrop strategy: continuous drops through "Power Level Over 9000" campaign to build sustained community engagement
- Entertainment beyond gaming: user-generated content, cross-platform IP sharing, creator economy

### Dylan's Takes & Opinions
- Dylan noted that stable coins on Tron serving developing countries represents a different use case than what crypto Twitter discusses.
- He expressed that crypto was bipartisan until recently: "up until a few years ago the crypto industry as a whole was bipartisan."
- Dylan admitted being "a little cynical or callous" after expecting web3 gaming to pop off in 2021 and seeing the Reddit backlash about blockchain being environmentally hazardous.
- He observed that "nobody's ever happy with what they get with airdrops" and finds the continuous airdrop model interesting.

### Guest Perspectives
**Rebecca Liao (Co-Founder & CEO, Saga):** Rebecca articulated that technology adoption succeeds by solving two problems: vanity (desire to feel loved and respected) and convenience (making life easier). She argued that crypto has found product-market fit for cryptocurrency/tokens and stable coins but not yet for consumer applications. She positioned Saga as anti-establishment, championing indie game developers over AAA studios that have "failed gamers." She rejected grants as a primary developer incentive, arguing that providing 50,000 monthly active users is more valuable than a $50,000 grant. She called out the crypto industry for "recirculating money amongst ourselves" rather than attracting new users.

### People & Projects Mentioned
- **Saga** - L1 to launch L1s protocol with gasless transactions
- **Saga Origins** - Saga's game publishing arm for mature-rated titles
- **Skuchain** - Rebecca's first crypto startup (DeFi for SMB liquidity), co-founded with Zi Manan Witcher
- **Globality** - AI procurement startup Rebecca worked at, backed by SoftBank
- **dYdX** - Referenced for 18-month migration from Ethereum to Cosmos
- **Cosmos SDK** - Foundation Saga is built on
- **Roblox, Fortnite** - Referenced as examples of centralized UGC platforms that blockchain could decentralize
- **Angelic, Zeroverse, Luca** - Games building on Saga
- **Stand with Crypto / Coinbase** - Dylan's DC lobbying trip for pro-crypto legislation

### Recurring Themes
- The critical need for new non-crypto-native users to sustain the bull market
- Gaming and entertainment as the bridge to mass adoption (vanity + convenience)
- Indie developers as the true innovators, not AAA studios or big institutions
- Gasless and infrastructure-abstracted blockchain experiences for end users
- Bipartisan crypto policy as a practical necessity
- Continuous community building through sustained airdrops rather than one-shot events
- Stable coins as crypto's killer app for developing economies

### Content Ideas & Future Topics
- Rebecca mentioned entertainment beyond gaming (film, TV, music, books) as a next-cycle opportunity worth exploring.
- The web2 indie developer migration to web3 via Saga could be followed up on.
