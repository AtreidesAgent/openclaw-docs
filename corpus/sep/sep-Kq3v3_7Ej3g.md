## Analysis

### Summary
Dylan interviews James Weybourne, the Chief Design Officer at COZ (a Neo ecosystem development group), about the psychology behind good UX design, his award-winning work in health technology, collaborative design processes, and the barriers to blockchain mainstream adoption. The conversation bridges James's 25-year career in web and UX design with the unique challenges of designing for the emerging blockchain space.

### Topics Covered
- James's background: self-taught web design from 1998, graphic design at university, early Flash and HTML
- Creative blocks and overcoming them through research-driven UX processes
- User-centered design: personas, emotional drivers, cognitive load reduction
- Award-winning health technology work: cystic fibrosis platform (gold at industry awards), NHS flu vaccination campaign
- International health tech: working with Kenya's Ministry of Health on cancer funding for rural communities
- Collaborative design workshops: design studios, Miro boards, cross-functional team participation
- Transition from health tech to blockchain (via COZ and Tyler Adams)
- UX as the biggest barrier to blockchain mainstream adoption
- Web3 branding evolution: "Bitcoin not blockchain" to "blockchain and tokens" to "Web3"
- Digital wallets as the "passport to Web3" and outgrowing the "wallet" term
- COZ council membership and bringing process-driven management
- Remote vs. in-person design workshops post-COVID

### Dylan's Takes & Opinions
- Dylan shares his parallel career arc: graduate degree in urban planning, working for government agencies, then finding his calling in crypto content creation.
- He describes feeling like an "imposter" in blockchain because he's "just a writer and not a developer" — but acknowledges his growing role as a trusted voice in the Neo ecosystem.
- Dylan draws a parallel between UX design and his experience as a charity fundraiser: "make it easy for other people to say yes."
- He argues his role as a planner was to "convert engineer speak into human speak," mirroring the UX designer's challenge.

### Guest Perspectives
**James Weybourne (CDO, COZ):** James argues that UX is a group activity, not a solo endeavor — developers, product owners, designers, and even users should participate in design studios together. He believes blockchain's biggest adoption barrier is the complexity of the technology (private keys, wallet addresses) that doesn't fit current mental models. He cites Steve Krug's "Don't Make Me Think" principle as central to good UX. On the future, he predicts wallets will evolve beyond mere crypto storage into comprehensive Web3 passports encompassing digital identity and NFTs.

### People & Projects Mentioned
- **COZ** - Neo ecosystem development group; James is CDO and council member
- **Tyler Adams** - COZ founder who recruited James into blockchain
- **Neo** - Blockchain ecosystem; multiple products designed by James (N3 brand, website, Dora explorer)
- **Neon Wallet** - Desktop and mobile wallets designed by James
- **GrantShares** - Neo community funding mechanism
- **Dora** - Blockchain explorer in the Neo ecosystem
- **Steve Krug** - "Don't Make Me Think" author; UX design influence
- **Jeff Raskin** - Apple Mac software designer; UX influence
- **Alan Cooper** - UX design author and influence
- **Central St Martin's** - London design school; James's colleague's background

### Recurring Themes
- UX as the key to blockchain mass adoption
- Psychology and cognitive load in design
- Collaborative, research-driven design processes
- The evolution of career paths into blockchain
- Digital identity and the wallet as Web3 passport
- Imposter syndrome in blockchain (both Dylan and James discuss this)

### Content Ideas & Future Topics
