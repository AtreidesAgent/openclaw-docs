## Analysis

### Summary
The final Crypto Talk and Blockchain Walk of ETH Denver 2026 with Joshua Lapidus, a SporkDAO board steward who has been involved with ETH Denver since volunteering in 2020. They discuss the event's logistics, the new venue's first year, the impact of the Trump administration on international attendance and visa issuance, the parasitic side event economy, and why this was "the most American ETH Denver" ever. Joshua shares that at least 50% of attendees are always new to ETH Denver and crypto, making the onboarding experience critical.

### Topics Covered
- Joshua's ETH Denver journey: volunteer (2020) → meta steward (2021) → steward (2022) → SporkDAO board
- SporkDAO board creation in 2021 — the DAO that oversees and operates ETH Denver
- Event planning cycle: a few weeks to decompress, then immediately start on next year
- The "ETH Denver is dead" narrative: FOMO, jealousy, AI bot psyops, and clout-chasing Elon bots
- New venue's first year — went better than the first year at the previous venue (2023)
- Attendance metrics: ~12K unique ticket redemptions, ~44-45K turnstile, 25-30K including side events
- Estimated $250M economic impact on Denver area (hotels, flights, Ubers, restaurants, venue spend)
- Trump administration's impact: reduced visa approvals, international attendees afraid of ICE, "the most American ETH Denver ever"
- DevConnect Buenos Aires announcement — crowd booed the possibility of America as a host location
- Side event culture resolving itself this year — most people stayed in the main venue
- 200+ side events this year vs. 600 the previous year — money is tight in bear market
- Thematic stage organization so attendees could sit at one stage for hours (e.g., AI stage)
- 50%+ of ETH Denver attendees are always new — first-time crypto and Ethereum exposure
- The importance of a positive first impression for onboarding — no shilling, no gambling traps
- The ETH Denver skateboard merch held in customs, arriving on the last day

### Guest Perspectives
- **Joshua Lapidus** (SporkDAO Board Steward): The "ETH Denver is dead" talk is driven by FOMO, AI bots, and clout chasers — "everyone who's in the room is enjoying themselves." This year was "builder focused, intimate, high quality, high signal, low noise — all the tourists left the building." The biggest external factor was the Trump administration reducing visa approvals and creating fear among international travelers, making this "the most American ETH Denver ever." Attendance was down but the experience was better — thematic stage organization let people deep-dive topics without running between rooms. Critical stat: 50%+ of attendees are always new to ETH Denver and often new to crypto entirely, making the quality of their first impression essential — "this is our one shot at giving a first impression of what our ecosystem is about." The new venue worked well in year one, with adjacent buildings available for expansion if needed. Economic impact estimated at ~$250M for Denver.
