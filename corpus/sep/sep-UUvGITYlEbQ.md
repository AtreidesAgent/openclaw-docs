## Analysis

### Summary
Dylan speaks with Aaron Kaplan, founder and co-CEO of Prometheum, about building the first fully regulatory-compliant digital asset exchange and custodian in the United States. The conversation covers Aaron's conviction since 2013 that digital assets are securities, the six-year journey to obtain a special purpose broker-dealer license, why existing crypto exchanges lack proper compliance, and how Prometheum aims to provide the market infrastructure that enables institutional adoption of digital assets under existing securities laws.

### Topics Covered
- Aaron's background: financial services, securities law, writing a no-action letter to the SEC about trading Bitcoin in 2014
- Early work on blockchain-based in-game asset ownership (pre-NFT era, 2015)
- The DAO Report of July 2017 as the catalyst for founding Prometheum
- Why it took six years to build a compliant exchange (regulatory pioneering, technology from scratch)
- Prometheum's structure: parent company (tech), Prometheum ATS (trading), Prometheum Capital (special purpose broker-dealer for custody)
- Difference between Prometheum and Coinbase: proper securities licensing and tech stack
- Aaron's view that digital assets have always been securities and regulatory clarity has always existed
- SEC enforcement progression: lending → staking → custody → exchanges
- The SPF/FTX collapse: institutions going through "a glass box of emotions"
- The path to institutional adoption through compliant trading and custody
- Why claims of "innovation leaving the US" are self-serving narratives from non-compliant actors
- Prometheum's intent to list all digital assets that are securities (everything except Bitcoin per SEC guidance)

### Dylan's Takes & Opinions
- Dylan compared Aaron's experience to the Zoolander "crazy pills" scene - Aaron has been calling digital assets securities since the ETH ICO while the industry ignored him.
- Dylan acknowledged doing "a complete 180" on SPF after the FTX collapse, having initially viewed him as crypto's advocate in DC.
- He noted that prior to this conversation, he "didn't necessarily grasp the importance of the special purpose broker-dealer license."
- Dylan described the conversation as "a little bit of a light at the end of the tunnel" amid regulatory doom and gloom.

### Guest Perspectives
**Aaron Kaplan (Founder & Co-CEO, Prometheum):** Aaron firmly believes the federal securities laws are the best regulatory framework for digital assets and that the SEC has been clearly communicating this since the 2017 DAO Report. He argued that existing crypto exchanges lack both proper licenses and appropriate technology stacks for securities processing. He contended that the "regulatory clarity" argument is a self-serving narrative from non-compliant actors. He positioned Prometheum's special purpose broker-dealer approval as proof that a compliant path forward exists in the US. He predicted a National Market System for digital assets will develop similarly to equities, with more approved entities expanding the pie.

### People & Projects Mentioned
- **Prometheum** - First SEC-compliant digital asset exchange and custodian in the US
- **SEC / Gary Gensler / Chairman Clayton** - Regulatory figures driving securities compliance for crypto
- **FTX / Sam Bankman-Fried** - Referenced as example of non-compliant centralized entity failure
- **Coinbase** - Contrasted with Prometheum's compliance approach; received Wells notice
- **Binance** - Received letters from CFTC
- **Terra/Luna** - Referenced as cross-over between technology and financial service provider
- **New York BitLicense** - Referenced as early state-level crypto regulation (2014)
- **MiCA** - European regulation that Aaron views as not yet implemented and behind US progress

### Recurring Themes
- Securities law compliance as the inevitable framework for digital assets in the US
- The tension between crypto industry narratives and regulatory reality
- Centralized entities (not DeFi protocols) as the source of all major crypto collapses
- Institutional adoption requiring compliant infrastructure for trading and custody
- The US maintaining its position as global market leader despite short-term regulatory friction
- The importance of investor protection over financial service provider convenience
- Building through regulatory uncertainty with conviction

### Content Ideas & Future Topics
- Dylan expressed interest in following which assets Prometheum lists first, especially given the SEC's position that everything except Bitcoin is a security.
- The development of a National Market System for digital assets could be a future episode topic.
