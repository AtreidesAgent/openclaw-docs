## Analysis

### Summary
Dylan interviews Grace Gui, CMO of Jsquare (a crypto VC firm), as part of the Women in Blockchain series. Grace shares her journey from a legal career in China to the Neo ecosystem to venture capital, discusses the differences between the 2018 and 2022 market cycles, the evolving VC investment landscape, and the inherent inclusivity of the blockchain industry's international and pseudonymous culture.

### Topics Covered
- Grace's background: law degree, paralegal work, transition to crypto over AR/VR
- China's September 4, 2017 crypto ban and its personal impact on Grace's career transition
- Grace's role at Neo Foundation: overseas marketing, global developer community events (DevCon)
- GrantShares origins: Grace's involvement in creating a public funding mechanism for Neo developers
- Jsquare vs. DFG: Jsquare focuses on NFT/GameFi/metaverse; DFG is more general (Ledger X, Circle, CoinList, Polkadot)
- Market cycle comparison: 2018 (ICO-driven retail) vs. 2022 (institutional/VC-driven with DAO groups)
- DAO-based investing as a new coordination mechanism
- Layer 1 foundations vs. VCs: different goals and risk management strategies
- Current trends: DAOs, GameFi evolving beyond play-to-earn, metaverse, DID (decentralized identity)
- The crypto space as naturally gender-neutral and inclusive
- Pseudonymity and anonymity as equalizers in blockchain

### Dylan's Takes & Opinions
- Dylan shares his own crypto origin: first exposed to Bitcoin in 2012-2013 via a meme about a price crash, then not thinking about it again until 2017.
- He notes that Neo's DevCon 1 in San Francisco was his first-ever crypto event.
- He believes in a "multi-chain future" and the importance of seed funding mechanisms like GrantShares.
- Dylan describes missing working with Grace and their collaborative history at Neo News Today.

### Guest Perspectives
**Grace Gui (CMO, Jsquare):** Grace brings an international perspective, arguing that crypto's appeal lies in its cross-border, cross-cultural nature — "people from different countries sharing ideas and building something." She distinguishes between 2018's retail-driven ICO boom and 2022's more institutional/VC-driven market, noting that fundraising has matured from public sales to structured private rounds. She views DAOs as evolving the way we work — freelancing, freedom, and merit-based contribution. On women in blockchain, she sees the space as naturally inclusive and doesn't think gender should be a focal point: "it's so natural to me to have female colleagues around me."

### People & Projects Mentioned
