## Episode Summary
Raj Parekh, Portal founder and now head of stablecoins and payments at Monad, discusses his path from Visa to crypto原生 builder. The episode covers Visa's early Libra experience as a catalyst, why Portal built stablecoin payments infrastructure, the DAO acquisition by Monad, and the future of multi-rail stablecoin settlement across both permissionless and corporate blockchain environments.

## Themes

### #stablecoins - Portal as Infrastructure Layer
Portal is a "stablecoin developer platform" enabling companies to move money fast with stablecoins—boring use cases from payroll to freelance platforms to neo-banking. Unlike issuers or custodians, Portal sits at the infrastructure layer: APIs for stablecoin settlement rails.

**Customer base:** WorldRemit, Bitso, PayPal, and early-stage companies. Portal enables "boring use cases with stablecoins"—not DeFi speculation but fiat replacement applications.

**Acquisition logic (July 2025):** Monad Foundation acquired Portal—Raj now has "two jobs": Portal as standalone platform AND building Monad ecosystem stablecoin/payments infrastructure.

### #payments - Visa DNA Applied to Blockchains
Raj spent 7 years at Visa across crypto, stablecoins, product, risk, security, and AI—\"deeply knowledgeable on end-to-end workings of payment systems.\" Key insight: Visa, SWIFT, DTCC all started as \"credibly neutral organizations\"—consortiums of banks agreeing on standards. This is the model for durable blockchain payment systems.

**Libra as accelerant:** Visa was an early Libra member. When that \"bold ambitious vision\" failed, it became an \"accelerant across fintech and payment organizations\"—realization that major organizations were exploring crypto seriously.

**The mainframe problem:** \"Visa being like a 60 to 70 year company—if the next 60 to 70 years is also like this [sending wires through mainframes], something's wrong.\" Blockchains as open-source payment systems are the natural evolution.

### #institutional - Enterprise vs Startup Innovation
Raj's view on where innovation happens:
- **Enterprise:** Companies like Stripe, Shopify creating closed-loop improvements (like Amazon's internal payments)
- **Startups:** Fill "blind spots\"—specific verticals like B2B payments, supply chain, trade finance, global payroll where large orgs won't build bespoke workflows

**Stablecoin adoption curve:** "Will span these organizations whether they build it themselves or work with providers\"—inevitable convergence, just timing and modality questions.

### #macro - Permissionless vs Corporate Chains
Raj is skeptical of permissioned blockchain bets after seeing \"CBDCs and permissioned blockchains chatter\" at Visa for close to a decade. Cites credibly neutral consortium model (Visa, SWIFT origins) as the durable pattern.

**Validator thesis:** Banks and FIs will become blockchain validators—\"members of Swift OR they'll become a validator for a blockchain\"—providing governance and controls. Multi-rail ecosystem: \"six or seven debit processing rails\" exists in TradFi; blockchains will mirror this heterogeneity optimized for fees, speed, use case.

### #stablecoins - Issuer Dynamics: USD vs Local Currencies
Critical distinction between USD stablecoins (Circle, Tether) and emerging local currency stablecoins (BRL, MXN, etc.):

**USD stablecoins:** Hit \"escape velocity\" with network effects, distribution, 10-year head start

**Non-USD stablecoins:** Must be evaluated differently—\"can't think of it in dollar terms because the demand base and supply base is just very, very different.\"

**Success factors for local stablecoins:**
- Political leverage/government relationships (especially in unclear regulatory landscapes)
- Built-in distribution partners (\"not enough to just say cool we have a stablecoin\")
- Strategic thinking about yield, DeFi interplay, circulating supply KPIs
- Clear distribution strategy across FI/fintech space

**Warning:** Can \"tell the ones that are thinking about this strategically versus the ones that are seeing the opportunity for what it is today.\"

### #DeFi - Payroll as On-Ramp
Portal's "boring use case\" thesis: Payroll is the Trojan horse for stablecoin adoption. Companies with global workforces need efficient cross-border payments—stablecoins solve this better than correspondent banking. Neo-banks and freelance platforms are early adopters.

### #policy - Regulatory Strategy
Working with issuers who have \"political leverage\" and government relationships critical in markets with unclear regulatory landscapes. Stablecoin issuance is as much political/regulatory game as technical one.

## Notable Predictions

1. **Multi-rail future:** Blockchains will mirror TradFi's heterogeneous payment rails—optimized routing based on fee, speed, use case
2. **Validator transition:** Banks/FIs become blockchain validators like current SWIFT members
3. **Local stablecoin fragmentation:** Non-USD stablecoins require different success metrics than USD—government partnerships critical
4. **Global-by-default:** Stablecoins make being global "a lot easier and faster\"—advantage to new companies vs. legacy enterprises with compliance overhead
5. **Conservative innovation:** Big companies will innovate on stablecoins, but startups will dominate bespoke vertical workflows
