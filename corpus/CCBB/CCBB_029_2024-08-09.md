## Analysis

### Summary
Episode 29 features Guil Sperb Machado from AxLabs/Bain Labs joining the conversation. Edge discusses account abstraction progress and passkey/YubiKey security, Dylan highlights NeoPod ambassador program early successes with new and familiar faces competing, and Guil shares his perspective on the Neo X launch — pleased that the technology "just worked" but wanting more developer questions and engagement. Ghost Market's new Lucky Buy feature is also covered.

### Topics Covered
- Edge's abstract wallet tinkering on N3 — demo shown to Dylan
- YubiKey and passkey security: advantages over authenticator apps and SMS 2FA
- Passkeys as the same technology powering abstract wallet work
- NeoPod ambassador program: 204 participants, mix of known and new names in top 10
- 50/50 split between recognized community members and newcomers in the leaderboard
- Guil (AxLabs/Bain Labs) on Neo X launch: technology worked smoothly, bridge security holding
- Guil surprised by fewer developer questions than expected — wanting more engagement
- Need for open-sourced Neo X node binaries and documentation
- Ghost Market's Lucky Buy feature: dice-roll mechanic for subsidized NFT purchases
- Yuga (Coinbase engineer) account hacked this week
- The importance of passkey-based 2FA over authenticator apps

### Dylan's Takes & Opinions
- On the abstract wallet demo: "pretty freaking cool, man" — excited about the potential.
- Dylan is "quite bullish" about NeoPod results, noting that ~50% of top 10 leaderboard names are completely new to him, suggesting the program is successfully onboarding newcomers.
- He sees NeoPod V2 as significantly better than V1 because it extends across all social platforms rather than being insulated in Discord, and creates a reinvigoration of community energy.
- On the broader ecosystem, Dylan described a multi-pronged bullish case: new projects on Neo X + renewed community engagement through NeoPod + "if we have another year plus run of a bull market, maybe just maybe we can revive" Neo's narrative.
- On Ghost Market's Lucky Buy: appreciates the degenerate gambling appeal — "we want meme coins. We want like degenerate gambling type stuff. That's why we're on crypto right now."

### Guest Perspectives
- **Edge** — Working 50/50 on N3 (abstract wallet) and Neo X (indexing). Described the abstract wallet UX: "you'll just download the app, give a username, do your biometric sign in, and then that's it." Explained passkeys as superior to all other 2FA methods because they can't be intercepted via man-in-the-middle attacks. On NeoPod, he noted a flood of new Discord accounts when it launched, uncertain whether they're Reddit lurkers or truly new community members. Hoping to get an abstract wallet prototype ready "for sharing maybe with a few insiders in the near future."
- **Guil Sperb Machado** — AxLabs/Bain Labs contributor to Neo X and the native bridge. Surprised and pleased that the technology "just worked" — bridge functioning, blocks producing, contracts deploying smoothly. However, expected more developer questions and feature requests (e.g., bridging generic messages/data, extending JSON RPC, NeoFS from Neo X). Acknowledged the node binary/source code release is pending — CI/CD pipeline ready but documentation needs finishing. Gave kudos to Michael for excellent work on Neo X at Bain Labs.
