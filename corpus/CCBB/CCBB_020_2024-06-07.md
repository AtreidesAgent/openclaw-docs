## Analysis

### Summary
Episode 20 covers GameStop trading halts as a case study for why blockchain markets are superior, dives into improving crypto UX through MEV resistance, account abstraction, and chain abstraction on Neo, discusses the Ansem vs. Hayden (Uniswap) debate over crypto populists vs. elitists, and examines celebrity coins as a problematic trend. Dylan and Edge also discuss Michael's EAD Zurich presentation on Neo X architecture.

### Topics Covered
- GME (GameStop) trade halts and the case for 24/7 blockchain markets
- Roaring Kitty's return and the history of the GME short squeeze saga
- MEV resistance on Neo X and its benefits for average users
- Account abstraction, passkeys, and reducing onboarding friction
- Chain abstraction and intent-based architecture as the next composability layer
- Neo X's relationship to Neo N3 and the native bridge
- Michael's EAD Zurich presentation on Neo X as a sidechain
- The Ansem vs. Hayden Adams (Uniswap) debate: crypto populists vs. elitists
- Celebrity coins and the zero-sum nature of crypto token pumps
- Gitcoin and quadratic funding (prior episode reference)

### Dylan's Takes & Opinions
- Dylan strongly advocates that blockchain's 24/7 operation and absence of trade halts represents a fundamental advantage: "the blocks don't stop minting. These markets don't stop. These chains don't stop. It's a totally fair and free market."
- He hopes crypto UX will improve enough that retail can participate "without being so exposed to the scary idea of losing your seed words."
- On chain abstraction, Dylan envisions a future where users simply state an intent (e.g., swap $100 to Bitcoin) and "the blockchain world made it so" — without caring about which chain processes it.
- He noted that Neo N3 already has capabilities for account abstraction (passkey-based wallets) that Ethereum struggles to emulate, and hopes teams will explore this more.
- On the Ansem/Hayden debate, Dylan resonates with Coby's point that "when you're hoping for a crypto pump, you're hoping to dump on some parcel that also bought into that dream" — fundamentally different from GME where retail was sticking it to hedge funds.
- He acknowledged the problem even extends to gas generation on Neo: "we print gas for ourselves by having our Neo and voting with it, and then we dump that gas on people that bought gas because they want to use the network."

### Guest Perspectives
- **Edge** — Provided the GME historical recap (from $4 in Dec 2020 to $80 in Jan 2021, Roaring Kitty's congressional testimony, his return in 2024 with a $100M+ position). Edge explained MEV resistance, account abstraction, and chain abstraction in technical but accessible terms. He highlighted that Neo N3's verification scripts make account abstraction "trivial" compared to Ethereum where you'd need a contract per user. He also discussed Michael's EAD Zurich talk, noting Neo X will use gas from N3 (not generate its own), making the two chains "inseparably" linked. Edge pushed back that the real issue is the broader token economy dynamic where early investors always have privileged positions. Referenced the Agorik/Smart Economy Podcast episode on chain abstraction.
