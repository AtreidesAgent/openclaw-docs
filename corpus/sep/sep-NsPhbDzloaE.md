## Analysis

### Summary
Dylan interviews Zac Williamson, CEO and co-founder of Aztec, a privacy-first Layer 2 built on Ethereum. The conversation traces Aztec's journey from a 2018 DeFi credit startup (CreditMint) to becoming a ZK-powered privacy infrastructure layer, covers the Noir programming language, the alpha build testnet learnings, and why Aztec is committed to decentralization from day one — unlike most other L2s.

### Topics Covered
- Zac's background: physics PhD at Oxford, particle physics research at CERN
- Origin of Aztec: CreditMint (disintermediation of credit default swaps) pivoted to privacy infrastructure
- Getting "privacy pilled" — realizing ZK technology enabling confidential computation on blockchain was transformative
- Aztec's early products: ZK.Money (shielded transfers), Aztec Connect (private DeFi bridge)
- Noir: general-purpose ZK programming language based on Rust syntax, designed to be more than an Aztec-only tool
- Alpha build testnet: 10,000+ contracts deployed, key apps (Anon for anonymous social media, ZK Passport)
- Commitment to decentralized sequencer network from day one — "non-negotiable"
- Why other L2s don't decentralize: no incentive (users don't care, decentralizing raises costs and lowers TPS)
- Aztec's unique incentive: privacy network MUST be decentralized to maintain neutrality
- Programmable privacy: apps choose which parts are shielded vs. transparent vs. selectively disclosed
- Regulatory positioning: building for compliance, not avoiding regulation
- Tornado Cash, Samourai Wallet shutdowns — Aztec differentiates through programmable compliance
- Token distribution: commitment to not being "decentralized on paper" — real community control
- ZK Passport (self.xyz): proving citizenship/age from passport chip without revealing other data
- Noir's network effect: external developers improve the language and libraries for everyone
- 2025 prediction: "2024 was the year of scaling, 2025 is the year of privacy"

### Dylan's Takes & Opinions
- Dylan calls this his "8th year in the blockchain and crypto space," establishing his veteran status.
- He notes that as "a web3 user and just a human in general, I cannot wait to have more privacy tools."
- He raises the Tornado Cash and Samourai Wallet precedents, asking whether these attacks change Aztec's approach.
- Dylan admits he actually followed a URL from Zac's conference presentation slide — prompting a humorous exchange.

### Guest Perspectives
**Zac Williamson (CEO, Aztec):** Zac argues that privacy is not just desirable but necessary for web3 to provide meaningful services beyond token mixing. He positions Aztec's programmable privacy as fundamentally different from full-privacy chains — allowing apps to build compliance into their logic. He is emphatic about decentralization: "if me as the co-founder want something to happen on the network, I've got to convince the community." He sees Noir as essential to Aztec's success because it creates a positive network effect beyond the Aztec ecosystem. He references Mina as trailblazers but notes their L1 choice limited them, while Aztec benefits from Ethereum's composability as an L2.

### People & Projects Mentioned
- **Aztec** - Privacy-first L2 on Ethereum; ZK-powered programmable privacy
- **Noir** - General-purpose ZK programming language built by Aztec
- **CreditMint** - Aztec's predecessor startup (credit default swap disintermediation)
- **ZK.Money** - Aztec's shielded transfer product
- **Aztec Connect** - Private DeFi bridge (deprecated)
- **Anon (anonymous social media)** - Built with Noir during alpha build; proves token ownership for anonymous posting
- **ZK Passport / self.xyz** - Passport verification via phone chip with selective disclosure
- **ZK Email** - Now has Noir integration for email-based proofs
- **Mina Protocol / Ted Kids** - ZK L1 trailblazers; referenced positively
- **Tornado Cash** - Privacy mixer facing US regulatory action
- **Samourai Wallet** - Bitcoin coinjoin wallet shut down
- **CERN** - Where Zac did particle physics research

### Recurring Themes
- Privacy as the missing piece for web3 mass adoption
- Programmable privacy vs. full privacy chains
- Decentralization as a requirement, not an option, for privacy networks
- ZK technology moving from scaling to privacy applications
- Building for regulatory compliance while maintaining network neutrality
- Open-source languages creating positive network effects
