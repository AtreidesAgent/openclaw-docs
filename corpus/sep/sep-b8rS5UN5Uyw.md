## Analysis

### Summary
Dylan interviews Ian Rogers, Chief Experience Officer at Ledger, about his pioneering career spanning the early internet, digital music, luxury retail, and now crypto hardware wallets. The conversation explores Ian's experience as the first webmaster for BeastieBoys.com, his tenures at Yahoo Music, Beats (acquired by Apple), and LVMH, and his philosophy on self-custody as the fundamental value proposition of cryptocurrency. Ian draws rich parallels between the early internet era and the current state of blockchain adoption.

### Topics Covered
- Ian's background: Indiana upbringing, skateboarding culture, computer science at Colgate
- Being the founder/webmaster of BeastieBoys.com and potentially running the first web design company
- Parallels between the early internet (1994-95) and current crypto adoption challenges
- Career progression: Yahoo Music, Beats/Apple, LVMH, then Ledger
- Expat life in Paris and perspective gained from leaving the California tech bubble
- The fundamental question: "If not self-custody, why crypto?"
- Ledger's positioning as the ultimate security device provider
- Network states and crypto's role in building alternative governance
- Technology transformation across decades: internet, music, luxury, crypto
- The concept that "there are no new human problems, we just hire new technologies to solve old problems" (Christensen framework)
- Vibe coding as "punk rock" -- democratizing technology creation
- Fitness and longevity as a parallel passion (skating, running, calisthenics at 50+)

### Dylan's Takes & Opinions
- Dylan relates to Ian's early internet story by sharing that when he was in the Bay Area (2015-2018), "crypto still wasn't cool" and talking about it met "eye rolls and skepticism" from the existing VC model.
- Shares that his own escape from a career he didn't love (urban planning, stuck in a cubicle) mirrors Ian's trajectory of following passion over conventional paths.
- Draws a skateboarding connection, mentioning Tom Penny who left America for a chateau in France, and nostalgia for old Flip skate videos.
- Connects Ian's career pattern to a broader thesis: history doesn't repeat but rhymes -- these "fringe" technologies consistently become mainstream.

### Guest Perspectives
**Ian Rogers (CXO, Ledger):** Posed the provocative question "If not self-custody, why crypto?" arguing that if you don't appreciate self-custody, you should just celebrate Visa and PayPal instead. Drew extensive parallels between early internet skepticism and current blockchain skepticism, noting that every "impossible" internet use case (video streaming, e-commerce) eventually became reality. Emphasized that blockchains are specifically good at issuance and trading applications -- "if it doesn't involve those two things, you should probably use a SQL database." Described vibe coding as "punk rock" in that it takes technology "off the stage and into the hands of the people."
