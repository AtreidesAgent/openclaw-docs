## Analysis

### Summary
Episode 62 features Dean (NNT), Gil (Axelabs), and Tyler (COZ) for a deep governance discussion covering Neo's council accountability issues, on-chain voter personas and behavior, proposals for changing GAS reward distributions to incentivize active participation, both on-chain and off-chain methods for validating council member contributions, and the pros and cons of Neo Burger's concentrated voting power.

### Topics Covered
- Issues with Neo's governance: inactive council members earning GAS without contributing
- On-chain voter personas and behavior analysis
- Proposals for changing GAS reward distributions to incentivize active participation
- On-chain methods for validating council member participation
- Off-chain methods for accountability (community reporting, social proof)
- The pros and cons of Neo Burger as a liquid staking/voting aggregation mechanism
- Concentrated voting power risks from liquid staking wrappers
- Council member accountability as a persistent ecosystem concern

### Dylan's Takes & Opinions
- Governance reform is overdue — inactive council members earning GAS is a long-standing frustration raised at Centre Point.
- Interested in both technical (on-chain) and social (off-chain) solutions for accountability.

- **Tyler Adams** — COZ CEO. Contributed insights on governance model tradeoffs and Neo Burger's impact on voting power concentration.
