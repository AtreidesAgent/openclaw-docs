## Analysis

### Summary
Episode 001 recorded at ETH Boulder with Francesco, Head of Developer Relations at Consensys (MetaMask, Infura, Linea). The conversation centers on how smart account permissions and MetaMask tooling are being adapted for AI agents, the agentic economy narrative, and whether the next billion on-chain users will be agents rather than humans. Francesco also shares observations about social coordination and reputation as emerging themes at ETH Boulder.

### Topics Covered
- Francesco's role leading DevRel at Consensys across MetaMask SDK, Embedded Wallet, and smart accounts
- Smart account permissions ("caveats") for controlling AI agent actions on-chain
- The agentic economy wave and its intersection with crypto
- Austin Griffith's agent attempting to extract private keys from MetaMask
- X402 payment volumes — estimated 70% are agents farming themselves
- AI as the new UI for blockchain and whether agents will be the majority of the next billion users
- Social engineering risks with AI agents and private keys
- The "vibe coding" phenomenon and the importance of learning fundamentals
- Social coordination and agent reputation as key emerging themes at ETH Boulder
- ETH Boulder's intimate, low-pressure format vs. ETH Denver

### Dylan's Takes & Opinions
- **First billion users framing**: Continues the recurring question from Episode 0: "When I got into crypto, we were always talking about onboarding the first billion users and this was 2017. Now in 2026, how much of the first billion users are going to be AI agents?"
- **Agents replacing human workers at MetaMask**: Provocatively asks "how far are we from a world where we're no longer hiring humans to work at MetaMask and we're only hiring AI?"
- **ETH Boulder's intimacy is valuable**: Notes that at ETH Denver "a million people would be wanting to talk to you" whereas ETH Boulder allows deeper one-on-one conversation.

### Guest Perspectives
- **Francesco** (Head of DevRel, Consensys/MetaMask): Working on MetaMask smart account kit with delegation toolkit to give AI agents specific, permissioned capabilities ("metamaskills") without exposing private keys. Notes that ~70% of X402 payments are agents farming each other, cautioning the industry to understand real vs. artificial use cases. Believes 50–80% of the next billion on-chain users will be agents. Warns vibe coders to still learn fundamentals. Highlights social coordination and agent reputation as critical emerging topics. Values ETH Boulder's relaxed, workshop-oriented format including morning Tai Chi sessions.

### People & Projects Mentioned
- **Consensys** — Parent company of MetaMask, Infura, and Linea
- **MetaMask** — Wallet with new SDK, Embedded Wallet, and smart account toolkit being adapted for agents
- **Austin Griffith** — Referenced for his story about an agent trying to extract private keys from MetaMask
- **Sean** — Gave a talk on "allies and agents" at ETH Boulder that Dylan enjoyed
- **X402** — HTTP payment protocol; Francesco notes most current volume is agent-to-agent
- **OpenClo** — Referenced in context of the agentic economy renaissance in crypto

### Recurring Themes
- AI agents as the next billion on-chain users
- Permission systems and security for autonomous agents
- The need to distinguish real use cases from agent-to-agent circular activity
- Fundamentals still matter despite AI-assisted development
- Social coordination and reputation systems for agents
