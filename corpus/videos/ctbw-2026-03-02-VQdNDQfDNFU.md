## Analysis

### Summary
The third walk-and-talk of ETH Denver day two with Kelly LeValley Hunt, an OG crypto art collector and advocate who has attended every ETH Denver. They discuss the evolution of NFTs from crypto art and Rare Pepes on Counterparty to modern real-world asset tokenization, Kelly's personal journey from Bitcoin to Ethereum through art provenance, and her conviction that NFTs are just at their early stages with ticketing, airline points, and RWA as the next frontiers. Also features an entertaining tangent about using dollars and Coca-Cola as untraceable currency at Nigerian roadblocks.

### Topics Covered
- ETH Denver's growth — more diverse products, more condensed and refined information, less noise
- The new venue improvements (no cow smell, better layout)
- Online negativity about ETH Denver driven by FOMO, not reality
- Bitcoin's recurring "crash" narrative — currently at $68K being called a crash
- Kelly's history with crypto art: Name Coins → Rare Pepes on Counterparty (2016) → crypto art → NFTs
- Rare Pepe scientists transforming Pepe from a hate symbol to one of love and inclusion
- Matt Fury as the original Pepe the Frog creator
- Using dollars and Coca-Cola at Nigerian roadblock shakedowns as commentary on fiat traceability
- NFTs' future: ticketing (BlockApps/Ticketmaster precedent from 2017-18), airline tickets, loyalty points, RWA
- MetaMask advocacy and defense against recent criticism
- Crypto art provenance for verifying physical art (oil paintings from France)
- Nifty Gateway and early NFT purchases via credit card

### Guest Perspectives
- **Kelly LeValley Hunt** (Crypto art collector, OG ETH Denver attendee): Has attended every ETH Denver; comes primarily to learn. Traced NFTs from their earliest forms: Name Coins (Nilly from Israel), Rare Pepes on Counterparty (2016), crypto art, then NFTs. Got interested because of her artistic family background (jazz musician mother, painter grandfather in the French Quarter, nuclear physicist father who played Mozart on banjo). Believes NFTs are "just at their early stages" — next evolution includes ticketing, airline tickets/points, and RWA. Notes BlockApps built one of the first blockchain ticketing systems bought and shut down by Ticketmaster in 2017-18. Strong MetaMask supporter: "If you're not getting hate, you're not doing it right." Lived in Nigeria where untraceable dollars and Coca-Cola were used at roadblock shakedowns — a real-world illustration of fiat's traceability problems.
