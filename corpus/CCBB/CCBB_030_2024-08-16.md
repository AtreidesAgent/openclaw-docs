## Analysis

### Summary
Episode 30 covers the third Poly Network exploit which impacted NEO and GAS holders and inflated prices on Flamingo Finance, the resulting arbitrage opportunity, new dApps emerging on Neo X (Astero Neo, Carrot Swap), the Neon 3 multi-chain wallet launch, and Dylan's on-the-ground experience at Rare Evo in Las Vegas — a Cardano-focused conference featuring Charles Hoskinson, Elijah Wood DJing, and insights into the Cardano ecosystem.

### Topics Covered
- Third Poly Network exploit — hacker inflated purchasing power, bought N3 assets, bridged to centralized exchanges
- 15-20% price divergence on Flamingo creating an arbitrage opportunity (with bridge-reopen risk)
- 24-hour Flamingo volume spiking to ~$12M vs. normal $500K-$1M
- Poly Network bridge shut down; wallets frozen; exchanges contacted
- New dApps: Astero Neo (DEX + forthcoming Neonauts NFT), Carrot Swap racing to launch
- Neo Dashboard flagging odd behavior with Astero Neo's WGAS contract naming
- Neon 3 multi-chain wallet launch — supports Ethereum and Neo X accounts
- Native bridge integration planned for Neon Wallet
- Neo X Secure3 bridge audit results (mostly informational/lows, one medium)
- Rare Evo VIP party: Charles Hoskinson shaking hundreds of hands, Elijah Wood DJing vinyl
- Cardano ecosystem observations: die-hard NFT/gaming communities, DAO funding mechanisms

### Dylan's Takes & Opinions
- On the Poly Network hack: "we've bore witness to our third Poly network exploit" — noted this is the first time Neo-side contracts were targeted.
- He identified an arb opportunity but clearly warned about the risk: "you're definitely running the risk of not knowing when a third party is going to reenact an architecture."
- On Astero Neo's contract naming issue, Dylan erred on the side of trust: "interpersonal experience dictates that generally like malicious actors don't reach out and say hey, I'm willing to hop on a video chat with you."
- He was impressed by Charles Hoskinson personally greeting ~250-300 people at the VIP party: "to see a founder walking around and shaking people's hands was just amazing."
- Elijah Wood DJing vinyl at the event was "totally surreal" — Dylan learned it's actually Wood's favorite pastime.
- He plans to write a Twitter thread summarizing Rare Evo insights.

### Guest Perspectives
- **Edge** — Noted this is the first Poly Network exploit targeting the Neo side. Concerned about the Secure3 crowdsourced audit model: "if you were a grey hat looking for an opportunity, you might not want to turn in the best vulnerabilities that you find." Prefers known entities like Red4Sec. On the investigation: "there were like hundreds and hundreds of transactions" requiring custom software to crawl. Confirmed Neon 3 already supports Ledger migration and multi-chain accounts (Ethereum, Neo X). Mentioned COZ is working on integrating the native bridge directly into Neon Wallet.
