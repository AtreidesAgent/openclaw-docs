## Analysis

### Summary
Episode 25 discusses Edge's chain abstraction research for deploying contracts across both Neo N3 and Neo X, the community poll results on the Salt Bound Token (which led to shelving the project), the importance of on-chain reputation systems, Neo Dashboard's new exchange tracking tool showing NEO migrating off centralized exchanges, OPSEC best practices after multiple high-profile account hacks, and the broader challenge of user retention in the Neo ecosystem.

### Topics Covered
- Edge's chain abstraction research — writing contracts once for both Neo N3 and Neo X
- Salt Bound Token poll results: ~40% want it, ~15% don't, ~10% unsure, ~35% uninterested — project shelved
- On-chain reputation systems as a potential public good
- NNT micro grants for ecosystem builders
- Neo Dashboard's new tool tracking NEO held on centralized exchanges — significant outflows from Binance (-11.75%) and OKX (-7.5%)
- NEO migration off exchanges as a potentially bullish signal
- OPSEC: protecting crypto holdings, hardware wallets, seed phrase security
- Account hacks: Da Hongfei (Dod) and Michael O'Rourke (Pocket Network) both compromised
- YubiKeys and hardware security recommendations
- Personal heuristics for detecting scams — excitement + urgency = red flag
- EthCC Brussels — people getting mugged at the conference

### Dylan's Takes & Opinions
- Dylan was disappointed the SBT didn't gain stronger support but recognized the "negative potentials probably outweigh the likely positives." He pivoted interest toward reputation tools as a potential public good.
- On NEO leaving exchanges: "Is this bullish to see hundreds of thousands of Neo being migrated off of exchanges?" — explored multiple hypotheses (accumulation, gas yield farming, whale activity).
- He admitted to being "the odd man out" among decentralization-maxi friends, appreciating "some of the levels of centralization and ease of activity."
- On security, Dylan implored listeners to scrub post history of token holdings, be cautious wearing crypto swag in public, and shared his personal scam heuristic: "anytime I get excited, I now take a step back and ask myself, why am I getting excited?"
- He called himself a "whale for an American" given limited exchange access for US NEO holders.

### Guest Perspectives
- **Edge** — Exploring chain abstraction tooling to write contracts in one language and compile for both Solidity (EVM) and Python/NeoVM. Noted the challenge of picking the right origin language given trade-offs in parsability and developer experience. On the SBT, Edge confirmed it's going to the "icebox" but pivoted to broader on-chain social tools and reputation systems. On exchange outflows, he suggested following the paper trail to see if NEO is going to voting, bNeo, or just sitting in new addresses. Edge provided OPSEC advice: the only secure 2FA is security-key based (passkeys); authenticator apps are vulnerable to man-in-the-middle attacks; SMS-based 2FA is the worst due to SIM swapping. Recommended YubiKeys as a valuable personal investment.

### People & Projects Mentioned
- **Neo Dashboard** — Built a tool aggregating NEO holdings across centralized exchanges with 7-day change metrics; tracking ~10 exchanges including Binance, OKX, KuCoin, Bitfinex
- **Flight Engineer** — Community member who suggested the SBT should integrate with other ecosystem projects before launch
- **Da Hongfei (Dod)** — Had his X/Twitter account hacked
- **Michael O'Rourke** — Co-founder of Pocket Network; same attack pattern used on his account
- **Vitalik Buterin** — Previously SIM-swapped, losing control of his Twitter account
- **Matt Lysing** — Recent Smart Economy Podcast guest on crypto content creation
- **Max Hillebrand** — Upcoming guest; CEO of ZKSnacks (Wasabi Wallet), discussing Bitcoin privacy
- **Kurt Hemecker** — CEO of Mina Foundation; interviewed about zero-knowledge on layer ones
