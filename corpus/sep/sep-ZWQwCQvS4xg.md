## Analysis

### Summary
Eli Cohen, general counsel at Centrifuge, discusses the platform for tokenizing and integrating structured credit markets on chain. The conversation covers the different types of RWAs and their tokenization processes, the shifting US regulatory landscape under the new administration, how Centrifuge tokenizes treasury bills and DeFi yield funds, the CFG token's role in DAO governance, and the relationship between Centrifuge's labs company and its DAO.

### Topics Covered
- Eli's career path: DOD, US ambassador office, banking, SGX, CME Group, Block.one/Bullish, then Centrifuge
- Educating APAC regulators about Bitcoin futures in 2017-2018
- Two categories of RWAs: public securities tokenization vs physical goods fractionalization
- Centrifuge's focus: bridge between TradFi asset managers and DeFi investors
- The securitization process explained simply (pooling assets to diversify risk)
- Anemoy: BVI-regulated fund entity issuing tokenized treasury bills as NFTs on Centrifuge
- The investor token as a securities token representing fund shares
- Centrifuge DAO structure: CFG governance token holders vote on protocol changes
- KF (labs company) vs CNF (Cayman nonprofit foundation) vs DAO relationships
- Partnerships with Circle, Coinbase attestations, Morpho lending markets, Steakhouse Financial
- Why Centrifuge moved from Ethereum to Polkadot (gas fees, flexibility)
- Biden administration as "pretty much a disaster" for US crypto regulation; optimism for new admin
- Stablecoin legislation as the most likely first bipartisan win
- New asset types beyond T-bills: AAA-rated higher-yield TradFi products incoming

### Dylan's Takes & Opinions
- Stated he's "generally and you probably don't hear this in day-to-day life" excited when lawyers join the podcast — appreciating the "different kind of reality" and matter-of-fact perspective.
- Reflected on surviving both bull/bust cycles AND an adversarial political regime, saying he "looked myself in the mirror and kind of patted myself on the back" — congratulating anyone who built through that double challenge.
- Called Bitcoin and stablecoins the "two killer use cases that crypto has delivered since Satoshi delivered the white paper."

### Guest Perspectives
**Eli Cohen** brought a seasoned regulatory lawyer's perspective spanning multiple jurisdictions. He was blunt that the Biden administration "was pretty much a disaster" for US crypto — "hard to imagine it could have been much worse" — citing enforcement actions and Choke Point 2.0. He was optimistic about Commissioner Peirce, Acting Commissioner Pham, and Senator Lummis. He provided a clear explanation of securitization: "you take a group of assets, put them together to diversify risk" — demystifying a TradFi concept. He noted that the CFG governance token holders are largely unknown to the team ("we don't know who they are") and personnel decisions at the labs company are not subject to DAO control.

### People & Projects Mentioned
- **Centrifuge** — platform for tokenized financial products; Polkadot parachain
- **Anemoy** — BVI-regulated fund entity; issues tokenized treasury bills
- **CFG** — Centrifuge governance token on Polkadot
- **KF** — Centrifuge's labs company (Switzerland-based)
- **CNF** — Cayman nonprofit foundation for protocol legal needs

- **Steakhouse Financial** — long-term partner; assists MakerDAO with RWA investments

- **Circle / USDC** — Anemoy has direct Circle relationship for stablecoin conversion
- **Coinbase** — attestation service used for lending market KYC
- **Commissioner Peirce** — SEC commissioner praised as "the right voice"
- **Commissioner Pham (CFTC)** — acting commissioner; praised
- **Senator Lummis** — praised; worked with Senator Gillibrand on stablecoin bill

- **MakerDAO** — referenced for Steakhouse Financial's RWA advisory work
