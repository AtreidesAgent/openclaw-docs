## Analysis

### Summary
Episode 27 marks the first CC&BB since the Neo X mainnet launch — a "genesis" episode introducing Neo X block height reporting. Dylan and Edge discuss the soft launch approach, the rapid contract deployment (50+ in <24 hours), the absence of VC token unlocks as a differentiator, MEV resistance as the key narrative for Neo X, Solana surpassing BSC in market cap, and whether ETH ETFs will drive on-chain adoption.

### Topics Covered
- Neo X mainnet launch — block height 14,785 on first reporting
- Ecosystem partners retweeting the launch: Cassette, NeoLine, NeoBox/Pixooty, COZ, Axelabs, For The Win Network
- Ed (For The Win Network) as a three-generation Neo builder (Legacy, N3, X)
- Current state of Neo X: bridge live, governance and MEV resistance forthcoming
- Pros and cons of a bare-bones "soft launch" approach
- 50+ contracts deployed on Neo X in less than 24 hours (including Tornado Cash)
- Old L1 tokens and the challenge of lower highs in subsequent cycles
- Community bag holders as potential sell pressure equivalent to VC unlocks
- Solana surpassing Binance Smart Chain in market cap
- Dylan's personal EVM renaissance through BASE/MetaMask
- MEV resistance and staking as Neo X's key differentiators
- Discovering the NEO token as a yield-bearing asset
- ETH ETF launch: Day 1 +$106M, Day 2 -$133M, Day 3 -$152M — parallels to BTC ETF
- CarrotSwap deploying imminently; For The Win's first product live

### Dylan's Takes & Opinions
- Dylan is "a fan of how the launch went" — preferring the cautious approach of ensuring each building block works before adding complexity.
- He expressed excitement about the EVM compatibility: "for the longest time, I was staunchly anti-Ethereum" but had a "personal renaissance" through BASE, and now sees Neo X as capturing that same plug-and-play experience.
- On old tokens: "all the darling coins from the 2013 cycle, from the 2017 cycle, from the 2021 cycle, they all kind of have lower highs in the subsequent bull markets."
- He pushed the narrative that Neo and Gas have no VC unlock schedule as a differentiator but acknowledged the "community of bag holders" risk.
- On Solana vs BSC: "you can only bang your head against the table so many times getting mad that the tech that people choose to value isn't the best tech."
- Dylan is "permanently bullish" — comparing the Neo X launch to a "Herculean task" and emphasizing "we have nowhere to go but up."
- On ETH ETFs, he drew parallels to the Bitcoin ETF's rocky first days and told listeners: "go out, touch grass, breathe a little bit, zoom out."

### Guest Perspectives
- **Edge** — Defended the soft launch as the sensible approach: "if you're just looking to make a financial splash" you'd launch with all bells and whistles, but for security and maturity, a controlled environment is ideal. He pushed back on Dylan's dismissal of Solana, arguing it "has its own technologies" with "low-level technical primitives which other blockchains, including Neo, should be studying." He noted it's "bullish for Neo if a chain like Solana, which is another alternative to EVM, is gaining popularity over an EVM clone." On Neo X's narrative, Edge emphasized NEO token discovery as a yield-bearing asset and NEO-to-NeoX bridges enabling gas distribution to new audiences. He envisioned marketing around Uniswap on Neo X offering "a better deal because there won't be someone sandwich attacking you." On ETFs, he sees them as "a catalyst for learning" about on-chain trading risks (MEV, reorgs) and believes Neo's one-block finality and MEV resistance could appeal to TradFi-adjacent users.
