## Analysis

### Summary
Episode 47 features Alex from Cassette (formerly Greenfinch), a decentralized storage dApp built on NeoFS. Alex shares the journey of building on a shoestring budget with day jobs, the remarkably low cost to host all of Wikipedia on NeoFS (~0.036 gas/year for 512GB), the pros and cons of building natively on a fledgling distributed storage network, plans to expose NeoFS to Ethereum via Neo X, and how anyone can now build immutable websites through Cassette. The episode ends with a call to action for community support and feature requests.

### Topics Covered
- Cassette's genesis: Alex discovered NeoFS, got NNT budget to explore, recruited Robin for UI/UX
- Building on NeoFS in the early days: no documentation, direct questions to developers
- Cassette renamed from Greenfinch (embarrassing bird-naming era) — "cassette rhymes with asset"
- NeoFS maturity: from no documentation to stabilized APIs and REST gateways
- The Wikipedia on NeoFS project: user "King" working to store all 300GB+ of Wikipedia
- Cost: 512GB storage costs 0.036 gas per month — ~1 gas stores 30 years of Wikipedia
- Anyone can fund NeoFS storage directly — no intermediary takes a cut (like a "digital charity")
- Cassette development paused when wallet integration wasn't possible — wouldn't ship without trust
- NFT-controlled access to NeoFS assets as Alex's dream feature (e.g., OnlyFans-style gated content, early access music)
- Exploring exposing NeoFS to Neo X (EVM) — potentially giving MetaMask users access to decentralized storage
- Working with Rick Locke (LinkD Academy) on the NeoX-NeoFS bridge
- Building immutable websites via Cassette: HTML upload + NeoNS naming (e.g., wikipedia.neo)
- Shoestring budget reality: both founders have full-time jobs, no foundation funding
- Feature requests drive the roadmap — community should ask for what they want
- Planned features: Raspberry Pi integration, mobile apps, file sync/continuous backup

### Dylan's Takes & Opinions
- "This might be a hidden gem across the broader industry" — doesn't know another team that's made decentralized storage this accessible via drag-and-drop.

Highlighted the potential for NeoFS exposure to EVM via Neo X as a major opportunity.
