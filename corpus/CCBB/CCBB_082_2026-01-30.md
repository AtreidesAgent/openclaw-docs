## Analysis

### Summary
Dylan brings on Cameron (Cameraon), a crypto-specialized CPA from Colorado, for an in-depth conversation about cryptocurrency tax preparation. They cover the implications of the Clarity Act on tax reporting, best practices for avoiding IRS audits, the new 1099-DA reporting requirements, the wash sale loophole, and the pros and cons of using AI for tax preparation — all grounded in years of hard-won personal experience from both host and guest.

### Topics Covered
- Potential Clarity Act implications: commodities vs. securities treatment, mark-to-market eligibility, and staking/mining tax treatment
- How to prepare for future tax law changes (wash sale rules likely going away)
- Best practices for preparing crypto taxes: save 25% of crypto income, use stablecoins for off-ramping
- Tips for using custodial exchanges: only transfer stables to CEXs to simplify 1099-DA reconciliation
- How to avoid getting flagged for IRS audits: reconcile 1099-DA proceeds, review tax software outputs critically
- The pros and cons of using AI for tax preparation (hallucinations, seeking confirmation bias)
- Wall-by-wall tracking requirements for 2025 tax year
- 1099-DA reporting: first year exchanges report proceeds but not cost basis
- The value of hiring a professional CPA vs. DIY with tax software
- Personal war stories: Terra/Luna anchor losses, tax savings in stablecoins gone wrong

### Guest Perspectives
- **Cameron (Cameraon, Dorian Advisors)**: Crypto-specialized CPA since 2017, handles 500+ reports annually. Key advice: save 25% of crypto income into stables immediately. Compared crypto tax prep to changing brake pads — you can DIY but mistakes are dangerous. Biggest client mistake: over-reliance on tax software without verification. On the Clarity Act: wants classification to enable proper treatment but personally prefers ambiguity to keep non-crypto CPAs out. Staking/mining should be treated like farming — not taxed until sold (corn kernel analogy). On 1099-DA: exchanges will report proceeds but not cost basis, creating reconciliation chaos. On wash sales: likely to be eliminated but may trade for staking/mining becoming non-taxable until sold. On AI: "these things hallucinate tax results" — useful for internal prep and research but not for actual filing. "There is no judgment" for clients with embarrassing portfolios.
