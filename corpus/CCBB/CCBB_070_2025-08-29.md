## Analysis

### Summary
Episode 70 covers Flamingo Finance's new LP token collateralization for minting FUSD, Block Spirits activation stats from Blockchain Rio (nearly 5,000 battles over two days), GrantShares deliberations on the Vaultin proposal and CenterPoint 2 travel grants, the ZK DKG security audit for Neo X, SpoonOS Dev Call Season 1 conclusion with 43 winners and $5,500 in prizes, and the dichotomy between patience in software development versus hype cycles in crypto.

### Topics Covered
- Flamingo Finance: new LP token (FLP FUSD/BNEO) approved as collateral for minting FUSD
- FUSD collateral types and interest rates breakdown
- Block Spirits activation stats: 1,939 battles day 1, 2,975 day 2 (~5,000 total)
- War on Bugs update: Whack-A-Bug minigame, NeoFS integration for NFT characters
- Vaultin GrantShares proposal deliberation: 2,300 gas ask, pseudonymous developer, milestone approach
- CenterPoint 2 travel grant applications from global applicants (US, UK, Montenegro, Nigeria, West Africa, Spain)
- SpoonOS Dev Call Season 1 conclusion: 85 GitHub issues, ~200 proposals, 8 winners, $5,500 in gas distributed
- ZK DKG protocol audit completion for Neo X anti-MEV
- The dichotomy between patience in development vs. market hype expectations
- Neo Legacy deprecation reminder: October 31

### Dylan's Takes & Opinions
- **On supporting anonymous builders**: Extended deliberation on Vaultin — ultimately argued in favor: "if this person is indeed being honest...the gamble on paying 2,300 gas to incentivize this person to come build in the ecosystem is worth it." Values the developer's attitude of building rather than complaining.
- **On pseudonymity**: "The genesis of the industry in which we work places such a high value on pseudonymity and anonymity and privacy." Defends the Vaultin dev's choice to remain anonymous.
- **On losing builders**: "Some teams and projects are leaving...if there's an intelligent builder that comes to the ecosystem, we should be kind of taking the risk to support them."
- **On development patience**: Drew an analogy to highway lane expansion — after three lanes, additional lanes yield diminishing congestion relief. Applied this to the fallacy that doubling team size will halve delivery time.
- **On Africa and LatAm**: "Africa and Latin America are two of the most overlooked regions of the world that have some of the hungriest developers and some of the brightest minds."

### Guest Perspectives
- **Guil (AxLabs)**: Agreed with Dylan on supporting the Vaultin proposal — argued the developer likely has legitimate reasons for pseudonymity (e.g., employer restrictions on side projects). Noted GrantShares has taken larger risks on non-open-source projects in the past. Offered to have xLabs vote in support but wanted another member to endorse first.
