## Analysis

### Summary
Episode 19 is a special on-location edition recorded at Consensus 2024 in Austin, Texas, featuring COZ CEO Tyler Adams as a guest. The conversation centers on COZ's guerrilla NFI (non-fungible item) activation at the conference — chip-embedded branded apparel used for lead generation without a traditional booth — and broader observations about the energy, sentiment, and regulatory mood at Consensus 2024.

### Topics Covered
- COZ's NFI-enabled apparel activation at Consensus 2024 in partnership with The Smart Economy Podcast and Wachsman PR
- The guerrilla marketing approach: staffing branded shirts with scannable NFI chips instead of renting a booth
- Iteration and hardware/software improvements in NFI technology since Consensus 2023
- The lack of competing physical-asset blockchain tech at the conference
- Conference sentiment and political tailwinds for crypto in the US
- Plans for distributing NFI-enabled goods to global community evangelists

### Dylan's Takes & Opinions
- Dylan used his NFI shirt chip as a networking tool at a Bitcoin founders event where there was no cell reception, calling it "a really useful way personally for me to do networking." He sees practical everyday utility in the tech.
- He expressed surprise that NFC-enabled blockchain technologies haven't iterated or expanded beyond simple business cards since emerging ~2 years ago: "we really haven't seen anything kind of emerge beyond that."
- Dylan believes the ease of onboarding improves dramatically when people have prior exposure to the tech — citing that ~80% of Consensus attendees had seen the rings from 2023 and could quickly grasp the new activation.
- On conference sentiment, he noted that US legislative wins the prior week created positive energy, and that international builders (Eastern Europe, Asia) are "taking note on the breathability" now that US regulatory hostility is easing.
- He characterized a major unnamed blockchain project's booth engagement tool (a spin-the-wheel) as "stone age type technology" compared to what COZ offers.

### Guest Perspectives
- **Edge** — Co-editor at Neo News Today; joined despite having COVID. Provided Neo N3 network stats (block height ~5,468,225; ~117,810 addresses). Minimal speaking role due to illness.
- **Tyler Adams** — CEO and co-founder of COZ. Described the Consensus activation as part of a monthly pilot series testing physical asset market fit. Explained that each NFI shirt had a scannable button linking to a web app; collecting all sponsor scans entered users into a raffle for an iPad Air. Tyler admitted his own social limitations hampered the guerrilla approach, suggesting it would work better with outgoing staff. He noted that despite expecting more competitors in the physical-asset blockchain space by 2024, COZ is still the only project doing it at Consensus. Tyler highlighted that the entire electronics package and software have been completely overhauled since Consensus 2023 — representing roughly five years of hardware advancement. He expressed motivation to get NFI-enabled goods into the hands of community evangelists globally and to publish a public roadmap for the technology.

### People & Projects Mentioned
- **COZ** — Neo ecosystem development group running the NFI activation at Consensus 2024
- **Wachsman** — Global PR firm partnering on the activation; also funnels guests for the Smart Economy Podcast
- **The Smart Economy Podcast** — Dylan's podcast, co-sponsor of the activation
- **Item Systems** — COZ's NFI technology platform
- **Neo News Today** — Content outlet where Dylan and Edge are co-editors
- **Kaia** — Listener who joined the space (brief mention)
- **Meg Lister / Gitcoin** — Teased as next week's podcast guest to discuss GG20 (Gitcoin Grants Round 20)

### Neo Ecosystem Signals

- COZ is the sole project at Consensus 2024 doing physical-asset blockchain activations — no competitors emerged despite Tyler's 2023 prediction
- The NFI hardware and software stack has been completely overhauled since Consensus 2023, with five iterations of hardware/software configurations in the Paris Blockchain Week shirts alone
- Tyler signaled interest in creating a public roadmap for NFI technology and distributing NFI-enabled goods to global community members for product evangelism
- The activation demonstrated a booth-less conference strategy, testing whether lead generation can work without a fixed physical presence

### Recurring Themes

- The gap between blockchain technology's potential and mainstream adoption at crypto events
