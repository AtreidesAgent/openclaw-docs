## Analysis

### Summary
Episode 36 features Tyler Adams (COZ CEO) and community member Nuke (Cryptorado) discussing the Denver Walls 2024 NFI activation in detail — modular plaque design improvements, the tap-and-redeem beer experience at Ratio Beerworks, onboarding non-crypto-native users, and the potential for a COZ NFI developer toolkit. The conversation also touches on Colorado's local crypto community and real-world use cases for NFI technology like mountain peak geocaching.

### Topics Covered
- Denver Walls 2024: modular bronze plaques with swappable NFI chips and faceplates
- Year-over-year improvements: faster install, better tap-scan reliability (~85% first-tap success)
- Tap-and-redeem process at Ratio Beerworks — lessons learned from ETH Denver pilot
- Dedicated POS tablet for bar staff replacing QR-code admin key approach
- The challenge of onboarding non-crypto-native mural visitors (camera app vs. NFC reader confusion)
- Entirely on-chain quest system — no backend server, all permissionless smart contracts on Neo N3
- Tyler explaining the quest framework: anyone could create redemption offers using the smart contracts
- The potential for a COZ NFI SDK/dev kit — close but hardware supply chain is a bottleneck
- Nuke (Cryptorado) suggesting NFC-enabled mountain peak geocaching for Colorado 14ers
- Local crypto community building in Colorado through Cryptorado events
- The Block Spirits Token 2049 activation (noted as a topic for future discussion)

### Dylan's Takes & Opinions
- Dylan emphasized the challenge of onboarding "the first billion users" — even something as simple as tap-scanning confuses non-crypto users who open their camera app and physically tap the phone against the plaque.
- He sees the Denver Walls activation as "Trojan horsing blockchain and crypto enabled decentralized networks into the mass market through an art vertical."
- On the quest being entirely on-chain: "we're onboarding and Trojan horsing blockchain and crypto enabled decentralized networks into the mass market."
- Dylan encouraged local community members (Nuke, Peter) to propose collaboration ideas that would push COZ toward building out the dev kit — "if there's enough squeaky wheels then maybe we can get the grease."

### Guest Perspectives
- **Tyler Adams** — COZ CEO. Described the modular plaque redesign: swappable NFI chips and faceplates, significantly improved tap-scan reliability, quarter of the install time vs. 2023. Explained the quest system is entirely on-chain with no centralized backend — "if COZ was wiped off the face of the earth, anybody could still go in and create redemption offers." On the dev kit timeline: the SDK is close but hardware supply chain and rapid product iteration (breaking changes monthly) make it risky to release prematurely. He confirmed the quest app could be deployed to new NFIs in ~15 minutes, mostly design work.

Mentioned the Cryptorado community hosts many regular events (lu.ma/cryptorado). Credited Dylan with opening his eyes to the Neo ecosystem.
