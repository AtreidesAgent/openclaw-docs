## Analysis

### Summary
Episode 37 covers Dylan's experience recording 22 Smart Economy Podcast interviews at Permissionless in Salt Lake City, with key themes emerging around regulatory ambivalence, the rise of monolithic chain narratives, Uniswap launching Unichain (an L2 with TEE), the FBI creating a fake AI token to catch market manipulators, and recent Ndapp improvements including wallet connectivity and swap features.

### Topics Covered
- 22 interviews conducted at Permissionless across two days
- Regulatory sentiment shift: ambivalence replacing fear — "it doesn't really matter" who wins the election
- Frederica Ernst (Gnosis co-founder) reframing the conversation: crypto is built to supersede human power structures
- Gnosis Card as a real-world crypto spending product Edge actively uses
- Hadley Stern's career arc: Fidelity Bitcoin mining (2014) → Ethereum infra → BNY Mellon custody → Marinade/Solana
- Institutional money and builders flowing into crypto regardless of regulation
- Uniswap launching Unichain L2 with sub-250ms block times using Trusted Execution Environments
- The TEE debate: Edge dismisses them as "hand waving" — prefers FHE and ZK proofs
- The app chain thesis failing in Cosmos and potentially threatening Ethereum
- Dylan's emerging bullish thesis on monolithic chains as fragmented liquidity becomes a problem
- FBI launching a fake AI token (Next Fund AI) to catch 18 market manipulators
- Prediction markets legally allowed to operate in the US (court ruling this week)
- Ndapp updates: wallet connectivity, gas claiming, vote changing, swaps via Glida integration

### Dylan's Takes & Opinions
- After Permissionless, Dylan feels "bullish on us as an asset class" — seeing builders leaving TradFi for crypto, infrastructure getting built, and expectations of dApp explosions in the next 1-2 years.
- He's "frankly becoming concerned about the future of Ethereum" due to L2 fragmentation and the app chain thesis: "each year goes by and more L2s launch, more activity gets siphoned away."
- Developing a new bullish Neo thesis: "the rise of the monolithic chain" as fragmented liquidity pushes value back to monolithic architectures. "If this plays out, you heard it here first."
- On the FBI's fake token: "they're here. They're on crypto Twitter. They're engaging with us and we don't know who they are."
- Dylan sees prediction markets being legalized in the US as a positive.

### Guest Perspectives
- **Edge** — Dismissed TEEs as insufficient: "the problem with the TEE is that you're going to use someone else's TEE... we have technologies such as fully homomorphic encryption and zero-knowledge proofs." Confirmed FHE general purpose is here ("I was told that yesterday from the good man himself"). On Ethereum infighting, he noted it's "unusual" because the community is "usually this steadfast stoic group." On monolithic chains, Edge agreed there's advantage in Neo's slow-roll approach: "we have the technology now that we could facilitate ZK rollups... but we maybe have an opportunity to see what the solutions are before we start deploying those roll-ups." On Ndapp: highlighted wallet connectivity, gas claiming, vote changing, and Glida-powered swaps as key improvements.
