## Analysis

### Summary
Episode 16, the longest CC&BB to date, features Dylan, Edge, and Guil in a deep conversation about narrative strategy for Neo. They trace Neo's narrative evolution from 2017 (multi-language, finality) through today's EVM-dominated landscape, discuss Dylan's "420 thesis" for Neo success (top-down Hong Kong strategy + bottom-up NeoX launch), and debate why Neo was absent from Forbes' "zombie chains" list. Guil reveals NeoX is targeting Q3-Q4 launch with the team near burnout, and Edge proposes using NeoX to attract EVM devs then luring them to N3's unique features.

### Topics Covered
- Neo N3 statistics (103,164 addresses, block 5,283,148, 400+ contracts)
- Samuari Wallet developers arrested by DOJ; comparison to Tornado Cash
- Stand With Crypto update: lame duck year approaching; toothless 1.5 years for crypto legislation
- Vitalik's privacy pools paper: ZK proof funds didn't originate from unlawful sources
- Narrative evolution: 2017 Neo (multi-language, finality) → failed due to high deploy fees ($15K-$70K)
- Current L1 narratives (TPS, low fees) becoming less effective as L2s/L3s emerge
- User experience becoming the strongest narrative; Base/Farcaster as proof
- Neo was "right place, right time" in 2017 with fewer smart contract options
- Dylan's "420 thesis": top-down (Hong Kong office, Cyberport, stablecoins, RWA, ETFs) + bottom-up (launch NeoX before bull run ends)
- Forbes "zombie chains" list: 20 billion-dollar projects — Neo not even included
- NeoX targeting Q3-Q4 2024 launch; team near burnout; EVM compatibility vs. innovation paradox
- Gil: NeoX is "portal not door" enabling conversations with state-of-art EVM tooling teams
- Edge: attract devs to NeoX, lure back to N3 with unique features (Nexo multisig = free Gnosis Safe)
- Gil: NeoFS accessible from MetaMask via native bridge is technically possible
- First Digital Trust (FDUSD) launched on Sui — hope for Neo stablecoin
- COZ at peak had 400 contributing developers; collapsed with price
- Lower N3 gas fees advocated; gatekeeping "never going to be good for us"
