## Analysis

### Summary
Episode six of the day at ETH Denver with Alisia Painter, representing a Bitcoin L2 (Botanix) at an Ethereum conference. They have a substantive technical discussion about why Bitcoin L2s emerged (Taproot upgrade, ordinals fee explosion, regulatory environment under Gensler), the "loud minority" of Bitcoin ossifiers vs. the "silent majority" who want yield on their BTC, and why Ethereum ecosystem participants should care about Bitcoin adoption because the two assets are intricately correlated. Alisia explains how users can bridge Bitcoin to Botanix's EVM-based L2 to earn yield.

### Topics Covered
- Being a Bitcoin L2 "in the belly of the beast" at ETH Denver — fifth ETH Denver for Alisia
- BTC Denver contingent noticeably absent this year due to market conditions
- Dylan's thesis on why BTC L2s boomed: Gary Gensler's SEC made commodity chains (Bitcoin, Ethereum) the only safe substrate
- Technical catalyst: Taproot upgrade (2021) enabled Bitcoin L2s that were previously impossible
- Ordinals driving Bitcoin base layer fees so high that scaling solutions became necessary
- The "loud minority" of Bitcoin ossifiers vs. the "silent majority" who want DeFi primitives
- Bitcoin's security problem: halving subsidy every 4 years requires fee revenue from on-chain activity
- The correlation between Bitcoin price and Ethereum price — "you can pretend that Ethereum lives in a vacuum, but it does not"
- EVM as the best and most scaled execution environment, even for Bitcoin L2s
- Botanix: Bitcoin bridge, STBTC (staked Bitcoin with pure BTC-in/BTC-out yield), Dolomite lending, Blend vault (~10% USDC yield)
- How the Botanix bridge works: generates a Bitcoin deposit address, send from any wallet, appears in EVM wallet

### Dylan's Takes & Opinions
- **BTC L2 narrative linked to regulatory environment**: Offers his own thesis that Bitcoin L2s boomed because Gensler's SEC made Bitcoin (as a commodity) the safest chain to build on. Identifies Bitcoin and Ethereum as "one of two chains that cannot be stopped by a nation state."
- **Bitcoin security problem is real**: "Every four years, the subsidy is cut in half... If we want to have a secure blockchain layer secured by miners, we're going to have to provide them with fees. And where do fees get derived from? On-chain activity."
- **Everyone is down because Bitcoin is down**: "We're all down bad because Bitcoin is down. 100%. We're all down bad."
- **Ethereum and Bitcoin are correlated**: Agrees that caring about Bitcoin adoption is essential even for Ethereum holders.

### Guest Perspectives
- **Alisia Painter** (Botanix Labs): Building a Bitcoin L2 for three years. Two factors enabled Bitcoin L2s: (1) Taproot upgrade in 2021 removed technical constraints, (2) ordinals caused fee explosion requiring scaling solutions. Describes Bitcoin ossifiers as the "loud minority" who are "holding Bitcoin back" — contrasts with the "silent majority" (like parents DCA-ing into BTC) who intuitively want yield and borrowing capabilities. Key argument: Bitcoin is the largest crypto asset with massive loss of productive capital, talent, and innovation flowing to other ecosystems. Uses EVM because it's the most scaled virtual machine with the most devs and tooling — "how can you argue with the Lindy-ness of the EVM?" Products include STBTC (staked Bitcoin with pure BTC yield), Dolomite lending for USDC borrowing against BTC, and Blend vault for ~10% USDC yield. Emphasizes crypto Twitter is waning in importance as cultural shift occurs within Bitcoin.

### People & Projects Mentioned

- **STBTC** — Staked Bitcoin product on Botanix yielding pure BTC from network activity
- **Dolomite** — Lending protocol live on Botanix for borrowing USDC against Bitcoin
