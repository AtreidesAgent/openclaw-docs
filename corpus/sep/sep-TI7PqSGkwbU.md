## Analysis

### Summary
Dylan speaks with Andreas Brekken, founder of SideShift, a non-custodial exchange supporting 100+ cryptocurrencies, about discovering Bitcoin in 2011, building the JustCoin exchange in 2013, working at Kraken, and launching SideShift in 2019. The conversation covers how exchanges haven't fundamentally changed since Mt. Gox, the beauty of Bitcoin's simplicity, building through bear markets, SideShift's "ephemerally custodial" model, and the team's explosive growth in the current bull market.

### Topics Covered
- Andreas discovering Bitcoin in 2011 and being struck by the simplicity of peer-to-peer money transfer
- Building JustCoin exchange in 2013 as a hobby project (Bitcoin, Litecoin, XRP only)
- Creating the first JavaScript hierarchical deterministic (HD) Bitcoin wallet
- Bitcoin core development pace as a feature, not a bug
- Selling JustCoin to ANX (now OSL) in Hong Kong for Bitcoin; Brock Pierce's interest
- Working at Kraken as a software engineer (recruited by Jesse Powell in an 8-minute phone call)
- Leaving Kraken because Bitcoin gains made working unnecessary
- SideShift's origin: a demo for moving assets between Bitcoin main chain and layer 2 (drivechain)
- SideShift's "ephemerally custodial" model (custodial for seconds during swaps)
- Inventory management: SideShift holds large floats of 100+ tokens, partners help rebalance
- Zero-registration, no-KYC approach to user privacy
- Bear market building: steady user growth, hardware wallet integrations (Trezor, Edge, Ledger)
- Bull market results: volume up 10x, treasury from ~$100K to ~$15M
- The xAI token and fee-sharing model

### Dylan's Takes & Opinions
- Dylan connected with Bitcoin through the lens of banking the unbanked, noting that Western Union fees can eat 25% of remittances sent by migrant workers.
- He compared Bitcoin's peer-to-peer revolution to his teenage experience downloading music on Napster: "the moment of downloading that first song... was so empowering."
- Dylan shared that he went full-time into crypto one month before the bottom of the 2019 bear: "I was like, what am I doing with my life."
- He views building during bear markets positively: "during bull runs it's very hard to pay attention... your desk is located right next to the water cooler."
- Dylan expressed concern about data privacy, citing Ledger's data breach and his own PII being leaked from companies and credit monitoring services.

### Guest Perspectives
**Andreas Brekken (Founder, SideShift):** Andreas emphasized that centralized exchanges have barely changed since Mt. Gox - they still perform all functions (custody, clearing, deposits/withdrawals) under one roof with little transparency. He described Bitcoin's slow, conservative development as the right approach for what may become a global settlement layer. He advocated for the no-registration model arguing that exchange hacks and database leaks of user PII cause immense harm. He shared his bull market advice to his team: "do not get caught up in the Euphoria, don't keep your coins in exchange, don't more than 2x leverage."

### People & Projects Mentioned
- **SideShift** - Non-custodial exchange supporting 100+ cryptocurrencies
- **JustCoin** - Andreas's first exchange, sold to ANX/OSL
- **Kraken** - Major centralized exchange; Andreas worked there under Jesse Powell
- **Brock Pierce** - Called Andreas on Skype to buy JustCoin
- **Jesse Powell** - Kraken founder who recruited Andreas
- **Luke Dashjr** - Bitcoin developer who answered Andreas's early questions
- **Drivechain** - Bitcoin layer 2 project that inspired SideShift's name
- **Trezor, Edge Wallet, Ledger** - Hardware/software wallets SideShift integrated with
- **cify** - SideShift's product accessible to American users
- **Cosmos, Algorand, Aptos, Cardano, Near, Terra** - Chains SideShift supports

### Recurring Themes
- Building through bear markets as the optimal time for focused development
- Bitcoin's conservative development ethos as a feature for a potential global settlement layer
- The multi-chain thesis: embracing many blockchain networks rather than maximalism
- User privacy and the harms of centralized data collection
- The cyclical nature of crypto markets and the importance of conviction through downturns
- Banking the unbanked through permissionless, peer-to-peer financial systems

### Content Ideas & Future Topics
- Dylan mentioned wanting to discuss the xAI token in more detail but ran out of time.
- The cify product for American users was flagged but not explored.
