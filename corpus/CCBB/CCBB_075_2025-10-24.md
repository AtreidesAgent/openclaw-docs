## Analysis

### Summary
Solo episode 75 is a deep dive into Erik Zhang's Neo4 roadmap, which Dylan frames as a pivotal moment for the ecosystem. Dylan argues that RWAs and TVL — not users — are the path forward for Neo, drawing on the explosive growth of on-chain real-world assets in 2025. He also covers the Neo Core developer meeting recordings (now public), the governance portal being developed, and his long-held conviction that Neo should have aggressively pursued native stablecoins years earlier.

### Topics Covered
- Erik Zhang's Neo4 Roadmap: "a blockchain platform for the real world" — RWAs, GATs, stablecoins, DEXs, gaming
- The argument that TVL, not daily active users, is Neo's salvation
- RWA market explosion in 2025: stablecoins, private credit, institutional funds (rwa.xyz data)
- Neo Core developer meeting publicly recorded and shared for transparency
- Governance portal development from CenterPoint 2 outcomes
- Erik's exploration of gas-free transactions for specific use cases (stablecoins, institutional funds)
- RISC-V compatibility for the next Neo VM execution layer
- Erik's acknowledgment that the Legacy-to-N3 migration was a mistake
- Decoupling staking rewards from governance voting
- Gas treasury allocation through parameterized governance
- CenterPoint check-up meeting published on NNT YouTube
- NeoNS domain activity up 190%

### Dylan's Takes & Opinions
- **On TVL vs. users**: "The only two ways that people will care about your project is if you have a ton of users or if you have TVL...clearly the users aren't going to be the savior of the Neo blockchains. So what then? We go after TVL."
- **On RWAs**: "I gave a presentation in October 2023 about how we need to be doing everything we can to open Neo to onboarding native stable coins...that was a total fuck up on the ecosystem's behalf."
- **On Erik's return**: "Somebody who is smart enough to build an entire blockchain architecture from scratch that has never experienced a catastrophic hack...is an intellectual...that anybody should be excited to have on their team."
- **On gaming**: "I'm still not sold on gaming as something that will onboard the masses quite yet...to build a game that people want to play, you have to be a AAA studio."
- **On council treasury**: Dreams of a GrantShares-like DAO for the Neo Council with access to significant funds: "Do you think that if the NEO Council had 20 million dollars that we would have paid whatever we needed to to get tether on NEO?"
- **On the Legacy migration mistake**: Erik acknowledged it was "a mistake to do migration from legacy to N3 that bifurcated our users."

### People & Projects Mentioned
