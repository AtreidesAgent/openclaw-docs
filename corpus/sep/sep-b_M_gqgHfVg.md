## Analysis

### Summary
Dylan interviews Tomasz Stanczak, co-executive director of the Ethereum Foundation and founder of Nethermind, about the EF's leadership transition, its new outlook on empowering builders, and the structural changes being implemented to better serve the Ethereum ecosystem. The conversation covers the EF's approach to DevConnect in Buenos Aires, the co-leadership model with Hsiao-Wei Wang, institutional onboarding, L2 relationships, and the forthcoming "second generation" of the Ethereum platform.

### Topics Covered
- Tomasz's background: traditional finance at CME Group and Citi (FX forwards pricing), transition to crypto in 2016-2017
- Building Nethermind: wrote 80-90% of the early codebase, now one of Ethereum's leading client implementations
- The EF leadership transition: why it happened, the board's preparation, finding the right candidates
- Co-executive director model with Hsiao-Wei Wang and its surprising benefits
- DevConnect Buenos Aires: intentional design choices to include L2s, applications, and use case tracks
- Restructured EF social media approach to highlight builders (4M+ Twitter followers being leveraged)
- New Founders Success team and application support team at EF
- Restructured grant systems focused on speed, introductions, mentoring rather than prolonged deliberation
- The EF's new policy on open source and free licensing for DeFi protocols
- Scaling, censorship resistance, and security as core EF focus areas
- LATAM as an emerging region for blockchain adoption (remittances, stablecoins, payments)
- Institutional onboarding and the L2Beat/Nethermind/Etherealize report

### Dylan's Takes & Opinions
- Dylan described the previous EF Twitter account as "a little off" and noted frustration from the community about poor communication in Q4 of the prior year and Q1.
- Shared a personal anecdote about a friend building the Pistachio app who praised the EF's hands-on approach in reaching out to applications and giving bootstrapping projects free booth space at DevConnect -- projects that normally couldn't afford $10K+ booth fees.
- Noted he's been "really exploring the intersection of LATAM and blockchain" and the different regional use cases (remittances, stablecoins, payments).
- Was wearing his DevConnect shirt during the interview, signaling personal engagement with the event.

### Guest Perspectives
**Tomasz Stanczak (Co-Executive Director, Ethereum Foundation):** Emphasized that the EF's restructuring focuses on "letting people move fast" rather than overthinking grant decisions that "kill the spirit of moving forward." Described the co-leadership model as having surprising advantages, noting that having two leaders creates a natural checks-and-balances system. Revealed that the EF is pushing for open source and free licensing as policy, putting pressure on protocols that aren't fully open source. Noted that the "second generation" of Ethereum is coming -- not just version numbers but a generational shift in the platform's capabilities.

### People & Projects Mentioned
- **Ethereum Foundation** -- Nonprofit supporting Ethereum development, research, and ecosystem growth
- **Nethermind** -- One of Ethereum's leading client implementations, founded by Tomasz
