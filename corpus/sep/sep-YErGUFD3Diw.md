## Analysis

### Summary
Ryan Galvanker, founder of Plaza Finance, discusses building a programmable derivatives platform on Base that splits Ethereum exposure into bond-like (Bond ETH) and leveraged (Lev ETH) tokens. The conversation covers Ryan's transition from TradFi hedge funds to crypto, how traveling to Kazakhstan opened his eyes to blockchain's real-world value, the bifurcated DeFi landscape, and how Plaza brings traditional financial product structures on chain.

### Topics Covered
- Ryan's TradFi background at Bank of America Merrill Lynch, Magnetar Capital, and Adalayia Capital Management
- The aha moment visiting Kazakhstan and seeing economies where banks can't be trusted
- Skip Protocol founders (Magnus and Barry) as the catalyst for going full-time crypto
- The bifurcated DeFi landscape: memecoins/perps on one extreme, stablecoins on the other
- Bond ETH: a $100-par bond backed by ETH paying 10% annual yield in quarterly USDC coupons
- Lev ETH: leverage without liquidation through self-balancing AMM mechanics
- How Bond ETH and Lev ETH create a symbiotic risk-sharing structure
- The 60/40 portfolio concept applied to crypto (stocks/bonds analog)
- Institutional flows coming on chain and the gap between Bitcoin/ETH institutional interest and altcoin performance
- Regulatory uncertainty for decentralized derivatives blocking US access
- Future plans: Bitcoin bonds and leverage, Solana expansion

### Dylan's Takes & Opinions
- Observed that "crypto Twitter is impossible to make happy" but noted the frustration is partly justified because altcoin holders are still underwater while Bitcoin/ETH benefit from institutional flows.
- Expressed that he attended Stand with Crypto's DC event and feels "crypto won this election regardless if you're left or right."
- Noted that Jamie Dimon still says he doesn't like Bitcoin even as banks build on blockchain — finding this amusing.
- Referred to RWA.xyz as a "phenomenal real world asset platform."

### Guest Perspectives
**Ryan Galvanker** brought a structured credit perspective from his time at Magnetar Capital (famous for the 2008 "Big Short" trade). He argued that the vast majority of finance happens between the two extremes DeFi has gravitated toward (memecoins vs stablecoins), and Plaza fills that gap. He was transparent about Bond ETH's risks: if ETH falls 90% in one day, coupon payments would pause (though this has never happened in ETH's history). On leverage, he explained Lev ETH "asymptotically approaches zero" rather than instantly liquidating — preserving the position for long-term believers. He noted that Plaza cannot yet serve US customers due to unclear decentralized derivative regulation.
