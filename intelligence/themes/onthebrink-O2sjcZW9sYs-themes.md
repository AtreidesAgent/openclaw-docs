## Themes

### #policy - Regulatory Reversal
2025 marked the most consequential regulatory shift in crypto's history, per the guests:

- **Bitcoin reserve EO (March):** Directed Treasury to hold seized BTC; created crypto task force under David Sacks
- **Leadership changes:** Paul Atkins (SEC chair), Michael Selig (CFTC chair nominee)
- **Dropped lawsuits:** Coinbase, MetaMask (Consensys), Ripple, Kraken, plus Wells notices withdrawn from Robinhood, Uniswap
- **SAB 121 repeal (May):** Removed punitive capital requirements for banks holding crypto
- **GENIUS Act (July):** Stablecoin legislation creating US compliance framework

**Historical context:** Two years prior, Brian Armstrong considered moving Coinbase to London; now "the US is clearly without a doubt the center of the crypto blockchain universe again."

**2026 watch:** Market structure bill (Clarity Act) is "single most important thing" — needs Q1 passage before midterm politicization. If stalled, crypto enters "gray area" vulnerable to 2025-level reversals.

### #institutional - The Blockbuster Defense Ends
Traditional finance CEOs pivoted from "this will amount to nothing" (2022–2023) to "we are upgrading our infrastructure" (2025). Evidence:

- **Payments:** Visa/Mastercard experimenting, acquiring, launching "back-end" stablecoin rails
- **Banks:** Major firms now offering "digital asset custody and stablecoin rails" alongside payments
- **Asset managers:** Larry Fink/BlackRock publicly discussing RWA tokenization alongside Bitcoin ETFs
- **DTCC:** Now discussing RWA tokenization of all supported products; secured SEC no-action letter for blockchain experiments

**"Innovator's dilemma" dynamic:** Incumbents can't admit their products are "expensive, slow, not really fit for purpose"; they "defend what they do today whilst monitoring what's coming." That changed in 2025 — no major TradFi executive is publicly hostile anymore.

### #equity - "Year of Crypto Equity"
2025 rebalanced venture returns from token-weighted to equity-weighted:

**IPO window after long drought:**
- Circle (firing gun), Gemini, Bullish, eToro, Figure announced/securitized SPAC
- 60+ early stage VC funds had been 60/40 equity/token split historically, but returns overwhelmingly token-weighted — until 2025

**M&A activity:** Stripe/Bridge, Robinhood/Bitstamp, plus strategic acquisitions by Coinbase, Ripple, Kraken

**Warning:** IPO windows historically open ~1 year then close. 2025 marks ~1 year open; risk of 2026 closure or shift to de-SPACs for liquidity.

### #macro - Valuation Risk & narrative cycles
Venture capitalists (long-term) vs trader capital (short-term):

- 2025 saw "very large amounts of capital" flow fast toward blockchain, particularly late-stage pre-IPO rounds and DATs (digital asset treasury companies)
- Late-stage valuations "began to look elevated" by year-end
- Traders worry "today, will this stock go up today or tomorrow" — creates boom/bust cycles visible in DAT discounts within calendar year

**AI correlation concern:** "Trillions of dollars going into the AI sector could actually look a little bit like a bubble later on in 2026" — parallels crypto late-stage valuation compression risk.

### #stablecoins - "Eating the financial stack"
Fifth Era thesis: "All financial activity may ultimately settle on blockchains." Two primary adoption vectors:

**Developing world:** Direct consumer use via dollar demand (Yellow Card/Africa), inflation hedging
**Developed world:** Institutionally-led via Visa "back-end" stablecoin settlements, Apple Pay abstraction, efficiency gains

**UX evolution:** Users won't know they're using stablecoins — "just that your dollars can move on the nights and the weekends outside of bank hours."

### #regulatory - Midterm political risk
Stablecoins now codified (GENIUS Act) — resilient to election cycles. Rest of space remains gray:

- **Best case:** Market structure bill in Q1 2026 codifies CFTC vs SEC jurisdiction, token vs security definitions
- **Risk case:** Delayed to post-midterms; becomes politically weaponized given Trump family crypto involvement
- **Consolation:** Chair Atkins rulemaking (if bill fails) would be difficult to roll back for tokenized products already launched by BlackRock, Fidelity, Apollo

**Structural safeguard:** "Stand with Crypto" super PAC raised "hundreds of millions" — formidable in 2024 midterms, deterrent against hostile regulators

### #adoption - 2-3 Billion Wallets Prediction
Matthew Le Merle: "We've been tracking digital wallets for a long time... there's 500 million today" (less than 6% of global population). Projection: 2–3 billion new wallets in next 3–5 years — "four or six times as many as our industry was able to open up over the last 12 or 13 years."

**Financial supermarket thesis:** Coinbase, Robinhood, Kraken, Uphold becoming "financial supermarkets" alongside AliPay/WeChat Pay equivalents — native + imported TradFi products in unified digital wallet

### #convergence - AI × Blockchain × IoT
2026 prediction: "Mashup" of converging technologies creates next innovation cycle:

- Blockchain (value over internet)
- AI / advanced computing
- Internet of Things (device-to-device transaction)
- Decentralization (global distribution without intermediaries)

**Sam Altman quote:** "I would not be backing any AI application companies... I'd be backing whatever's going to come next." The "next" here is cross-domain software engineering — blockchain + AI + IoT + decentralization combined.

**Use case evolution:** Past question: "Apart from stablecoins, what are the use cases?" Future: "Stablecoins are huge" + digital commodities + digital securities + AI-convergent layers.

### #scarcity - Musical chairs dynamic
Traditional finance entering acquisition phase:

- "Lot more banks than... blockchain crypto custody companies"
- "Lot more asset managers than high-quality digital exchanges"
- If incumbents don't move in 2026, "you're a seller, not a buyer" — scarce assets go to auction

**Analog:** 1990s banks ignoring internet until "suddenly all of the big players woke up" → acquisition spree

## Notable Predictions

1. **IPO window closes:** 2026 sees return to de-SPACs and fewer traditional IPOs as public markets close to crypto after year-long window
2. **2-3B wallets:** 3-5 year timeline for wallet count to 4–6× current base
3. **Market structure bill:** Q1 2026 passage critical; if missed, industry lives with Atkins rulemaking as consolation prize
4. **AI bubble risk:** Trillions in AI investment mirrors crypto late-stage valuation risk — potential 2026 correction
5. **Convergence plays:** 2026 marks year where blockchain + AI + IoT mashups become primary startup category, not single-domain apps
6. **DTCC tokenization:** Real-world asset tokenization moves from experiment (2025) to operational infrastructure (2026-2027)
7. **Political durability:** Digital natives (55% of US) + annual 1.5-2% voter turnover create structural pro-crypto electorate that transcends regulatory cycles
