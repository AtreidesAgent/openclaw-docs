## Analysis

### Summary
Episode 23 covers attracting users to the forthcoming Neo X EVM sidechain through rewards and incentives (with the Blast airdrop as a cautionary tale), the dismal US presidential debate, Julian Assange's release, the first Solana ETF application by VanEck, and crypto payments via Ampera/Flexa. Dylan and Edge also discuss Flamingo's underrated UX and the challenge of user retention vs. acquisition in the Neo ecosystem.

### Topics Covered
- Edge exploring SubQuery integration on Neo X testnet — infrastructure still early
- Neo X partnerships with AI projects, account abstraction wallet, and infrastructure providers
- Blast airdrop results and lessons for Neo X reward design
- How to attract and retain users on a new EVM sidechain — airdrops, staking rewards, gas incentives
- Flamingo Finance as an underrated DeFi platform with excellent UX but low liquidity
- NEO and GAS having no VC unlocks — a potential narrative advantage
- Dylan's personal journey from anti-Ethereum to EVM adoption via Farcaster/Base
- Julian Assange's release and its significance for free speech
- The US presidential debate — both candidates criticized as unfit
- VanEck's Solana spot ETF application as a watershed moment for digital asset ETFs
- Crypto payments through Ampera and Flexa — Levi's Baskin Robbins use case

### Dylan's Takes & Opinions
- Dylan believes Neo X rewards need to be substantial (hundreds or thousands of dollars) to genuinely attract and retain users, not just "$15, come test this out."
- He credits his first gas claim in Neon Wallet (September 2017) as the reason he's still in the Neo ecosystem — "one of the most powerful moments that I've ever experienced on the internet."
- Dylan pushed back on Edge's Flamingo criticism, calling it his "base case" for evaluating new DeFi platforms and arguing it's "primed to kind of like take off in use" once people discover it.
- He sees the absence of VC token unlocks for NEO/GAS as a narrative opportunity, though acknowledged the Neo Foundation's large treasury could be perceived negatively.
- On the presidential debate: "America, you have still figured out a way to steal a little bit more light, joy, inspiration, and idealism for a better tomorrow."
- He views the Solana ETF application as representative of a "forthcoming wave of digital asset ETF applications."
- Dylan compared buying Bitcoin during the 2020 George Floyd protests to "a peaceful protest for where I could put my spending power."

### Guest Perspectives
- **Edge** — Testing SubQuery on Neo X, noting infrastructure is still missing for established teams. He argued that Neo X's real selling point will come with envelope transactions (MEV resistance) since that provides fundamental UX advantages other networks can't match. On Flamingo, Edge agreed on UX quality but noted the liquidity is "at least 10 times outmatched" by major EVM networks, and that people don't generally seek out a network for its DEX — "you really need to onboard people into the network." He suggested that the ideal incentive would give gas to dApp developers who then distribute tokens to users, creating dual-sided investment in success. On the debate and politics, Edge characterized UK politics as "political theatre" and noted the dangerously low voter turnout in the UK.
