### Summary
Episode 24 covers the UK election results (Labour landslide), the German government selling hundreds of millions in Bitcoin, the forthcoming Mt. Gox distributions, the US government's contradictory Coinbase relationship, Circle's USDC becoming MiCA-compliant in the EU, the TON/Telegram blockchain thesis, Polymarket as a political sentiment tool, and the debut of the Salt Bound Token (SBT) concept — a fun on-chain social experiment for the Neo ecosystem.

- UK Labour Party landslide and Edge voting Green for the first time
- Polymarket as a real-time political sentiment tracker vs. traditional polling
- US Marshals partnering with Coinbase to sell seized crypto while the SEC sues Coinbase

- Chain abstraction tooling discussion — writing contracts once, deploying to both Neo VM and EVM

He laid out a comprehensive bull thesis — 700M+ users with native wallets, Tether integration, early infrastructure, sticky games driving activity. He believes TON could be "one of the darling chains that emerges during this coming cycle."

- He reaffirmed: "we are still in the bull run" despite current corrections.
