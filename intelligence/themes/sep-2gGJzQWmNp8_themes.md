# Crypto Custody and Institutional Adoption

## 1. Institutional Custody Evolution
- **Description**: Evolution from traditional custody to crypto-native solutions, focusing on institutional requirements for security, compliance, and scalability.
- **Key Evidence**: Discussion of custody models bridging TradFi standards with blockchain security.

## 2. Self-Custody vs. Third-Party Custody
- **Description**: Tradeoffs between self-custody (decentralized, user-controlled) and third-party custody (regulated, insured, easier compliance).
- **Key Evidence**: Multi-sig, MPC, hardware security modules for institutional self-custody.

## 3. Regulatory Compliance in Custody
- **Description**: SOC 2, insurance coverage, bankruptcy-remote structures required for institutional adoption.
- **Key Evidence**: OCC charters, NYDFS BitLicense for custody providers.

## 4. Custody for Staking and Yield
- **Description**: Custody solutions enabling staking, lending, and yield generation while maintaining compliance.
- **Key Evidence**: Staking-as-a-service, yield-bearing custody products.

## 5. Multi-Chain Custody Challenges
- **Description**: Managing assets across multiple chains, bridges, and L2s without compromising security.
- **Key Evidence**: Cross-chain custody protocols and risk management.

## 6. Insurance and Risk Mitigation
- **Description**: Crypto insurance products covering hacks, theft, and operational risks.
- **Key Evidence**: Nexus Mutual, Lloyd's of London crypto policies.

## 7. Key Management and MPC
- **Description**: Multi-party computation (MPC) for distributed key management without single points of failure.
- **Key Evidence**: Fireblocks, Copper, MPC wallets.

## 8. On-Chain vs. Off-Chain Custody
- **Description**: Smart contract-based custody vs. traditional wallet infrastructure.
- **Key Evidence**: Account abstraction for programmable custody.

## 9. Institutional Onboarding Friction
- **Description**: Legal, operational, and technical barriers to institutional custody adoption.
- **Key Evidence**: KYC/AML integration, reporting requirements.

## 10. Custody for Tokenized RWAs
- **Description**: Custody requirements for tokenized real-world assets (real estate, art, commodities).
- **Key Evidence**: Physical asset custody + on-chain representation.

## 11. Stablecoin Custody
- **Description**: Custody for stablecoins, including reserve verification and redemption rights.
- **Key Evidence**: Circle, Tether custody models.

## 12. Bridge and Cross-Chain Custody Risks
- **Description**: Custody risks in cross-chain bridges and wrapped assets.
- **Key Evidence**: Ronin, Wormhole incidents.

## 13. Hardware Security Modules (HSM)
- **Description**: HSMs for air-gapped key storage and transaction signing.
- **Key Evidence**: Ledger Enterprise, institutional HSMs.

## 14. Custody for DeFi Participation
- **Description**: Institutional custody enabling DeFi participation (lending, liquidity provision).
- **Key Evidence**: Fireblocks DeFi API.

## 15. Recovery and Social Recovery
- **Description**: Social recovery mechanisms for lost keys in institutional settings.
- **Key Evidence**: Argent, Gnosis Safe guardians.

## 16. Custodial Wallets vs. Non-Custodial
- **Description**: Balance between user control and service provider responsibility.
- **Key Evidence**: Smart contract wallets with custody features.

## 17. Compliance Reporting for Custody
- **Description**: Automated reporting for regulatory compliance in custody operations.
- **Key Evidence**: Chainalysis, Elliptic integration.

## 18. Custody for NFTs and Collectibles
- **Description**: Custody solutions for non-fungible tokens and digital collectibles.
- **Key Evidence**: Fractional NFT custody.

## 19. Geopolitical Custody Risks
- **Description**: Jurisdiction, sanctions, and geopolitical risks in global custody.
- **Key Evidence**: Multi-jurisdictional custody strategies.

## 20. Future of Programmable Custody
- **Description**: Custody evolving into programmable, composable financial primitives.
- **Key Evidence**: Account abstraction, intent-based custody.
