## Analysis

### Summary
Ace, co-founder and community representative of UFO Gaming, discusses the project's vision to become a blockchain gaming platform akin to Steam. The conversation covers UFO Gaming's community-first ethos, the team's pseudonymous approach, fair launch tokenomics, the staking ecosystem with plasma points, and the philosophical question of whether gaming teams or blockchain teams make better blockchain game developers.

### Topics Covered
- UFO Gaming's vision: a blockchain gaming platform (the "Steam of blockchain games")
- The team's pseudonymous identity and reasons for it (avoiding distraction, standalone success)
- Fair launch model: 50% token supply burned, no team allocation, everyone buys on open market
- Staking DAP: plasma points earned by staking UFO tokens to mint free genesis NFTs
- Triple token system: UFO (governance/utility), UAP (SuperGalactic in-game token), plasma points
- SuperGalactic: debut top-down shooter game in alpha with 1,500 tester slots
- Plans for NFT marketplace, metaverse v2, and cross-game NFT interoperability
- The philosophical debate: gaming studios entering blockchain vs blockchain teams building games
- Community governance moving toward DAO structure
- Revenue model through NFT marketplace, Yufodgies NFT drop, and VC fundraise

### Dylan's Takes & Opinions
- Stated he was "of that opinion" that gaming studios coming into blockchain would be more successful than blockchain teams making games, but acknowledged "in the blockchain space you never know."
- Praised the "community first ethos" and said "kudos" for building first and seeking compensation later — comparing it to what attracted him to Neo in 2017-2018.
- Noted that covering UFO Gaming would increase as they're part of the Neo EcoBoost program.

### Guest Perspectives
**Ace** argued that a blend of gaming and blockchain expertise is the "perfect storm" — noting UFO Gaming benefits from team members with both backgrounds. He was candid about the pseudonymous approach being partly a competitive strategy: the team didn't want to "piggyback off affiliation" with previous successful projects. He described the fair launch as an intentional choice to avoid team tokens, VC unlocks, and the negative dynamics those create. He compared their approach to Axie Infinity's model: where Axie required buying expensive NFTs to play, UFO Gaming lets players earn genesis NFTs for free through staking.

### People & Projects Mentioned
- **UFO Gaming** — blockchain gaming platform building SuperGalactic and metaverse
- **SuperGalactic** — debut top-down shooter game on Polygon, in alpha
- **Yufodgies** — upcoming NFT collection with in-game utility
- **Axie Infinity** — referenced as comparison for play-to-earn model and NFT cost barriers
- **Neo** — third game planned to be built on Neo blockchain via EcoBoost program
- **Polygon/Matic** — chain hosting SuperGalactic and associated tokens
- **Ethereum** — chain hosting the UFO governance token
- **Fireblocks** — mentioned in passing context
- **Uniswap** — where UFO token was originally launched

### Recurring Themes
- Community-first, build-first philosophy in blockchain gaming
- Fair launch tokenomics vs VC-backed models
- The debate between gaming-native vs blockchain-native game development teams
- Cross-chain and cross-game NFT interoperability as a long-term vision
- Building during bear markets as a strategic advantage

### Content Ideas & Future Topics
- Dylan noted he'd be covering UFO Gaming more as it's in Neo EcoBoost.
- Follow-up on the Neo-based third game when development begins.
- Cross-chain NFT interoperability implementation details.
- Update on the VC raise and team growth.
