# Weekly Newsletter — March 16-20, 2026

This was the week the SEC actually wrote things down, and the week crypto companies started cutting staff again. The industry is maturing at lightning speed, and not everyone is going to make it.

## The Rules Finally Got Written

The SEC and CFTC released guidance that tells the industry what is and isn't a security. This is huge. Gary Gensler refused to do this for years. The release explicitly lists Bitcoin, Ether, Solana, XRP, Cardano, Chainlink, Dogecoin, Avalanche, Polkadot, Stellar, Hedera, Litecoin, Tezos, Bitcoin Cash, and Shiba Inu as digital commodities. I called this "awesome" on the stream. It's exactly what the industry asked for. Beyond listing commodities, the framework allows tokens to graduate out of security status once they deliver and decentralize. This sandbox approach has been wanted for years. It does not replace the Howey test, but it tells everyone how the SEC will apply it.

## The AI Layoff Wave Is Here

Crypto companies are cutting staff and pointing at AI. Messari's CEO stepped down. The firm is going AI-first. Block cut forty percent of its workforce, citing AI productivity gains. Cango, a Bitcoin miner, reported a four hundred fifty-three million dollar net loss and is pivoting to AI infrastructure. Crypto.com cut roughly one hundred eighty people for the same reasons. I said it plainly on Tuesday: AI is coming for your jobs. Not just in crypto, but everywhere. I've been saying for years that the conversation shifted from onboarding the first billion humans to how many AI agents will be on chain. Now companies are proving it with cuts.

## TradFi Is Here To Stay

Mastercard is acquiring BVNK for up to one point eight billion dollars. PayPal expanded PYUSD to sixty-eight new markets. Moody's launched a credit rating system on Canton Network. NASDAQ got approved to settle trades as blockchain tokens. Coinbase launched a Bitcoin yield fund on Base. Ledger is opening a New York office for institutional custody. I noted this has been the dominant trend all year. Real world assets and stablecoins are the killer use case. Every single day this week brought another TradFi adoption story. Yet crypto Twitter was bearish the whole time. I pointed out the divergence between what institutions are doing and what token holders think. That's where the opportunity is.

## The DAO Dream Is Over

Tally is shutting down. The governance platform used by Uniswap and Arbitrum raised eight million dollars. Five years of building. And it's done. The reason is simple. The regulatory threat that forced decentralization is gone. With clear rules from the SEC and CFTC, the need for DAO tooling evaporated. I have first hand experience with DAO participation. It's like pulling teeth. Getting people to vote is hard. Decision making is slow. There's no financial incentive to participate. The industry consolidated around a handful of protocols rather than the infinite L2s we were promised. It's not early anymore. We're in the middle innings.

## Watch These Two Things Next Week

Watch the Clarity Act. The market structure bill is stalled on stablecoin yield debates. Republicans met with the Trump administration to break the deadlock. We need laws that survive the next anti-crypto administration in twenty twenty-eight. AOC and Elizabeth Warren are not going anywhere. Also watch AI payments. X402 is tiny at around twenty-eight thousand in daily volume. But Visa and Mastercard are already building on existing rails. Coinbase pushes the blockchain-native version. The winner isn't decided yet. When AI agents start transacting on chain, the volume will dwarf today's DeFi.
