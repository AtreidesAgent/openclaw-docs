## Analysis

### Summary
An ETH Denver conversation with Ashton Addison, an OG crypto content creator with 1,500+ videos since 2014, on his first ETH Denver despite attending many other major conferences. They discuss surviving four market cycles, the dot-com parallel for understanding crypto's maturation, AI agent payment infrastructure (X402) as the next frontier, stock market tokenization as an inevitable but slow-moving use case, and advice for aspiring Web3 content creators.

### Topics Covered
- Ashton's 12-year crypto journey starting in 2014 when the entire market cap was smaller than a single altcoin today
- Surviving four cycles — 2014 entry, 2017 boom, three crashes plus the current downturn
- The dot-com bubble parallel: 90-95% of projects die, but the survivors (like Amazon) become dominant
- AI agents and payment infrastructure — X402 and how agents won't have bank accounts or operate 9-to-5
- Stock market tokenization as an inevitable but slow development (security token summit in Bahamas 2018)
- The STO era of 2018 and how we still lack a liquid tokenized securities exchange
- Real-world assets and private stock tokenization as stepping stones
- Self-custody and peer-to-peer payments as enduring crypto value propositions
- First ETH Denver impressions — more builders than expected, AI agents as dominant topic
- Content creator advice: find a niche, create around personal interests, it's never too late to start
- 5-10 episodes per week production pace for Crypto Coin Show
