## Analysis

### Summary
A bilingual (English/Spanish) episode at ETH Boulder with Germán, builder of EVVM (Ethereum Virtual Virtual Machine) — a project enabling virtual blockchains to be deployed as smart contracts on any chain, eliminating the need for dedicated nodes. Inspired by Vitalik's vision of cheap gas, EVVM brings cloud computing paradigms to blockchain infrastructure and is already being used by banks through a partnership with Entity Data on Hyperledger Besu.

### Topics Covered
- EVVM — virtual blockchains deployed as smart contracts, like "having your own blockchain without nodes"
- The Web2 infrastructure evolution parallel: dedicated servers → VPS → cloud, applied to blockchains
- Vitalik's 2023 prediction at ETH Global about gas becoming extremely cheap as the catalyst
- Banks as primary EVVM users — deploying private blockchains with permissioned products
- Partnership with Entity Data on Hyperledger Besu for bank consortiums
- Permissionless deterministic bridge connecting private bank EVVMs to Ethereum mainnet
- EVVM treasury on Ethereum testnet pending mainnet approval
- ETH Boulder's relaxed, builder-focused atmosphere
- Upcoming workshops at ETH Denver scaling/privacy and X402 events

### Dylan's Takes & Opinions
- **Banks using EVVM — dystopian or positive?**: Asks directly "Is this sort of like a dystopian use case or are you happy to be seeing this type of user?" Shows his awareness of crypto's anti-institutional roots while being open to pragmatic adoption.
- **ETH Boulder is for builders**: "I saw the end of your talk at a very small conversation, a very intimate conversation, and you get to connect with other people who will provide insights into what you're building."

### Guest Perspectives
- **Germán** (Founder, EVVM): Built EVVM over three years in stealth after hearing Vitalik speak about cheap gas at ETH Global. Envisions blockchain infrastructure following the same trajectory as cloud computing — from dedicated to virtualized to cloud-native. Currently working with banks through Entity Data on Hyperledger Besu, where each bank gets its own EVVM with custom tokens and products, interconnected through permissioned bridges to Ethereum mainnet. EVVM can be deployed in 5 minutes for under $1 on L2 or $5 on mainnet. Appreciates ETH Boulder's no-pressure, builder-first environment.
