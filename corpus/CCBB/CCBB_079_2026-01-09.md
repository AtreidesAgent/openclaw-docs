## Analysis

### Summary
In the first episode of 2026, Dylan delivers a solo deep-dive into the public feud between Neo co-founders Da Hongfei and Eric Zhang over control and transparency of the Neo Foundation treasury. He contextualizes the dispute within Neo's decade-long history, then pivots to a forward-looking three-part growth thesis centered on RWAs, stablecoins, and DeFi as the pillars Neo needs to regain relevance.

### Topics Covered
- History of Neo from Antshares (2014) through the 2017 ICO boom and N3 launch
- The public dispute between co-founders Da Hongfei and Eric Zhang over Neo Foundation treasury control and financial disclosure
- Eric Zhang's return to Neo in Q4 2025 and his push for Neo N4
- The last Neo Foundation financial report (August 2020) and its disclosed assets ($524.3M at the time)
- NGD's official statement assuring operations are unaffected
- What Neo needs to fix: users/TVL, Flamingo liquidity, and native stablecoins
- The three-pillar growth thesis: RWAs, stablecoins, and interoperability/DEX support
- Flamingo Finance's TVL decline from $1.6B to $3.2M
- Centralized bridging problems on Flamingo
- Dylan's open letter to Da and Eric published January 1, 2026

### Dylan's Takes & Opinions
- "There's three sides to every story. There's side A, side B, and the truth" — Dylan repeatedly refuses to take sides in the co-founder dispute, framing himself as a community advocate focused on resolution. `[16:35]`
- Neo's communication failures are partly cultural: "I think that's been a cultural difference" regarding the top-down communication gap between Eastern leadership and Western community expectations. `[0:54]`
- Cardano's survival is attributed to Charles Hoskinson's daily community engagement, held up as an example Neo should follow. `[11:17]`
- "It is fucking ridiculous that we don't have USDC or USDT native on NEO N3" — Dylan has been advocating for native stablecoin integration since October 2023 and delivered a presentation to Da Hongfei directly. `[~37:00]`
- Flamingo's bridging is "terrifyingly centralized" — reliance on a privately controlled wallet for cross-chain liquidity is a fundamental flaw. `[~40:00]`
- The treasury dispute must be resolved by placing funds under council control so the community can direct investment. `[31:48]`
- "NEO as an ecosystem is filled to the brim with people who still give a shit" — despite all problems, the community and developers remain committed. `[~42:00]`
- Thinks long-term: "the only way to win is to think in years and decades, not nights, weekends and months." `[~44:00]`
