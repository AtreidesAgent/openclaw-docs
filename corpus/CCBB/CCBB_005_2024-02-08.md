### Summary
Episode 5 explores the explosive growth of Farcaster and its Warpcast client as the first viable Web3 competitor to centralized social media, the Frames primitive as interactive mini-apps that reduce user friction, and opportunities for non-EVM ecosystems like Neo to build presence on the platform. Dylan also previews the ETH Denver NFI plaque beer redemption event at Ratio Beer Works.

- Neo N3 network statistics (94,114 addresses, block height 4,866,394)

- Warpcast as dominant Farcaster client: 314K total users, 57K monthly active users, ~$5-7 one-time account cost
- Traditional social media problems: Twitter/X censorship, algorithmic manipulation post-Elon, Mastodon's server fragility
