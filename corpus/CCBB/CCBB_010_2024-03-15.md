## Analysis

### Summary
Episode 10 covers the successful Ethereum Dencun upgrade (EIP-4844 proto-danksharding/blobs reducing L2 costs ~10x), a deep dive on MEV classification (Monarch/Moloch/Mafia from Flashbots' Zing), Neo dropping out of the CMC top 100, and the Web3 Labs partnership at Cyberport Hong Kong. Dylan and Edge also reflect on positive directions since the Neo Community Assembly and debate the real-world viability of blockchain for government bureaucracy and real estate.

### Topics Covered
- Neo N3 statistics (97,735 addresses, 95 added this week, block height 5,058,389)
- NCA Hong Kong reflections: MEV resistance as early narrative Neo got ahead of
- MEV classification: Monarch (validator-directed), Moloch (coordination failures), Mafia (sandwich/frontrun/backrun)
- Encrypted mempools and NeoX envelope transactions as Mafia MEV solutions
- Dencun upgrade: hard fork of consensus + execution layers, EIP-4844 proto-danksharding
- Blobs: temporary data storage for rollups (~10x price reduction for L2s)
- Full danksharding still years away (data sampling, fisherman's dilemma)
- Ethereum upgrade smoothness as technological feat across multiple clients
- Blast L2 knocked offline by Dencun while others adapted immediately
- Neo dropped out of CMC top 100 (every top-100 coin now >$1.1B market cap)
- Web3 Labs partnership with Neo at Cyberport Hong Kong: 8 incubated projects
- EVMS project: cross-chain inscription exchange with indexers
- ADAPT project: real estate tokenization
- Government bureaucracy decades behind adopting blockchain
