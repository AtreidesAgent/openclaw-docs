# EP.673 - Mike Cagney (Figure) - Blockchain Capital Markets

**Date:** January 2026 (estimated)
**Video ID:** qWqTbu0Dn_A
**Format:** Guest interview

---

## Episode Summary

Mike Cagney, founder of Figure (recently public), discusses the company's blockchain-native approach to capital markets across credit and equities. The episode explores Figure's "second QIP" equity strategy, stablecoin-driven deposit flight from banks, and their vision for full DeFi vertical integration.

---

## Themes

### #DeFi - The "Promised Land" Strategy

Cagney frames DeFi as blockchain's ultimate value proposition—the elimination of intermediaries including prime brokerage, DTCC, and introducing brokers. Figure's strategy involves creating blockchain-native securities (a "second QIP") that trade on their alternative trading system (ATS) with bilateral settlement rather than centralized custody. He's pitched this to ~150 investors during the IPO roadshow, emphasizing cross-collateralization capabilities between equity and Bitcoin that traditional prime brokerage cannot offer.

**Key claim:** Cagney argues that true DeFi means "no Goldman Sachs warehouse"—assets remain in user custody, with users setting stock lending rates themselves rather than prime brokers capturing the spread.

### #institutional - Two-Track Equity Model

Figure's equity strategy involves parallel securities: the NASDAQ-listed NMS security and a blockchain-native equivalent with identical economic rights but different regulatory treatment. The blockchain security trades on Figure's ATS—described as potentially "the only ATS" enabling bilateral DEX-style settlement without intermediation. Cagney proposes treasury swaps between the two securities to ensure liquidity parity.

**Notable:** Traditional bankers initially pressured Figure to "tone down the blockchain stuff" during the S-1 process, resisting DeFi terminology. Circle's successful public offering reversed this stance.

### #stablecoins - Narrow Banking Thesis

Cagney presents a stark warning to regional banks: stablecoins represent existential deposit flight risk. He cites a Treasury study projecting $6.6 trillion could migrate from bank liabilities to interest-bearing stablecoins. The mechanism involves users moving deposits to yield-bearing stablecoins, then allocating to DeFi lending markets rather than bank balance sheets.

**Prediction:** Cagney argues that "the genie is out of the bottle" post-GENIUS Act. JP Morgan's JPM Coin will outcompete regional banks, driving stablecoin margins toward zero while larger banks dominate through application-layer advantages.

### #policy - Market Structure & Clarity Act

The episode details regulatory friction between NMS (best execution) requirements and DEX bilateral settlement. Cagney explains that blockchain-native exchanges cannot comply with "best bid best offer" routing because bilateral settlement involves asset exchange, not order routing across multiple venues. This creates structural incompatibility between traditional market structure and DeFi.

**Timeline:** While current SEC leadership under Atkins has signaled DeFi enforcement relaxation, Cagney notes Clarity Act passage remains 2–3 years out to codify these frameworks beyond interpretive guidance.

### #Bitcoin - Cross-Collateralization Capabilities

Cagney emphasizes that blockchain-native equity enables seamless collateralization across asset classes—using equity to purchase Bitcoin and vice versa without traditional segmentation. Traditional brokerage accounts (Robinhood, Schwab) cannot offer this integration due to custodial and regulatory silos.

### #VC - Blockchain Value Thesis

Cagney posits three blockchain value propositions in order of power: transactional efficiency, liquidity, and DeFi financing. He argues most current blockchain applications only demonstrate the first two; Figure aims to demonstrate all three across credit (green field) and equity (disruption) markets.

### #macro - Fed Policy & Banking Sector

Cagney connects stablecoin growth to Fed tightening dynamics observed in late 2022, when bank deposits contracted $1 trillion. He warns that a similar or larger withdrawal—$6.6T projected—would force asset sales and potential "financial calamity" absent blockchain adaptation by regional banks.

---

## Notable Predictions

1. **Regional bank crisis:** Without blockchain adoption, regional banks face existential deposit flight as stablecoins mature
2. **Margin compression:** Stablecoin yields will trend toward treasury rates minus minimal spread, eliminating banking's "free deposit" advantage
3. **DeFi demonstration:** Figure aims to demonstrate full vertical DeFi (credit) plus horizontal expansion (equity) by end of 2026
4. **DTCC displacement:** $2B revenue / $50B capital represents "rent" that blockchain can release, but secondary to larger DeFi economic shifts
