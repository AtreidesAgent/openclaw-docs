## Episode Summary
Pelle Braendgaard, co-founder & CEO of Notabene, discusses the evolution of Travel Rule compliance and the launch of Notabene Flow—a B2B stablecoin payment network. The episode covers decentralized identity infrastructure, FATF regulatory history, and how stablecoin payments are positioned to disrupt the $120 trillion B2B payments market.

---

## Themes

### #stablecoins - Swift for Crypto 2.0
Notabene's new "Flow" product targets the $120 trillion B2B payments market with a messaging and compliance layer built atop stablecoin rails. Unlike consumer-focused stablecoin products, Flow is designed for invoice-based B2B workflows: request payment, receive automatic reconciliation. The thesis is that stablecoins solve correspondent banking inefficiencies, but require a messaging layer (like SWIFT) to become operational at enterprise scale.

**Key claim:** "We can now actually build the better Swift. And no, I did not say the better Swift just for stable coins... I'm saying the better Swift and stable coins is part of what makes it better."

### #payments - Pull Payments Architecture
Flow introduces "pull payments" to crypto—a function Braendgaard claims no prior system has solved at scale. This allows businesses to request and authorize payments tied directly to business workflows (invoices, payroll), rather than simple push transfers. Technical infrastructure includes: real-time authorization, trusted network due diligence, fiat/forex conversion, and multi-chain support across blockchains and stablecoins.

**Opportunity:** Reconciliation challenges across chains, wallet address risks, and compliance overhead create friction that Flow aims to eliminate for exchanges, VASPs, and enterprises.

### #policy - FATF Travel Rule & Regulatory History
Braendgaard traces Travel Rule origins to Trump Administration (first term) engagement with FATF—driven by FinCEN seeking global regulatory clarity because "the US actually at that point was the place with most regulatory clarity." The Travel Rule requires transmitting originator/beneficiary information alongside payments—standard in traditional finance (SWIFT, Visa) but logistically difficult for crypto due to the "ledger vs. messaging layer" distinction.

**Timeline:** Notabene founders "were probably a little bit too optimistic" about regulatory clarity initially; full global implementation took ~5 years from 2019 guidance. Compliance rates are "really, really going up" now that major jurisdictions (UK, EU, Switzerland, Singapore, Hong Kong, Korea) have clarified guidelines.

### #institutional - Compliance & Customer Experience
Travel Rule is positioned as a customer safety feature, not just regulatory burden. End users gain verification that funds reach intended destinations (address validation), while institutions build "trusted networks" with real-time due diligence on counterparties. Braendgaard argues early "horrifying" proposals (OP_RETURN with PII on-chain, soulbound tokens, ENS doxxing) misunderstood privacy risks—Notabene's implementation uses decentralized identity with private messaging layers.

**Notable:** Strict liability for compliance officers under Bank Secrecy Act means "it's not a question of am I going to be in compliance, it's just how will I be in compliance."

### #Bitcoin - Self-Custody UX & Risk
Braendgaard acknowledges the anxiety of self-custody crypto transactions even for experienced users—referencing the "white knuckle experience" of copying addresses and the Bybit incident. Flow's messaging layer allows product teams to build safer UX where users avoid direct address handling while maintaining compliance and verification.

### #macro - Global Payments Infrastructure
The $120 trillion B2B SWIFT market represents Flow's target. Braendgaard argues stablecoins solve correspondent banking issues, but still need an "additional layer to operationalize it" at enterprise scale. Notabene has 250+ institutions operationalized with real-time authorization and trusted networks.

### #DeFi - Decentralized Identity Foundation
Notabene's roots in decentralized identity (DIDs, verifiable credentials) trace to core technology now embedded in Apple products, Swiss/European identity systems. The travel rule messaging protocol is "public domain like the internet"—improvement proposals rather than proprietary standards.

## Notable Predictions
1. **Swift disruption:** Stablecoin + messaging layer can capture meaningful share of $120T B2B payments market through superior workflow integration
2. **Global harmonization:** Trump's first-term FATF push created regulatory clarity worldwide; GENIUS Act in US is "final exciting step" for stablecoin adoption
3. **Pull payment emergence:** Crypto payments will shift from push-only to request/authorize workflows (invoices, subscriptions, payroll)
