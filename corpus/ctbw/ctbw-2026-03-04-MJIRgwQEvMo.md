### Summary
Episode five of the day at ETH Denver with David Hernandez (aka Zen Shorts), a creative producer, cultural curator, and analog photographer based between Harlem, NYC and Luquillo, Puerto Rico. They discuss Onchain Creators' content challenges at both ETH Boulder and ETH Denver, the importance of IRL connections over digital ones, David's "Vibe Check" event (annual rave with AI film festival), and his philosophy that the human element must scale alongside the technology for crypto to succeed.

### Guest Perspectives
- **David Hernandez / Zen Shorts** (Creative Producer, Cultural Curator, Onchain Creators): Self-described "recovering D-Gen now a Re-Gen" who leads with human connection over technology. Runs Onchain Creators content challenges that incentivize conference attendees to capture events through their own creative lens. Throwing his 4th annual Vibe Check event featuring an AI film festival and rave. Believes crypto's tech will continue scaling but "if we lose that humanity, it's not going to scale." His biggest operational challenge is CRM — maintaining relationships with hundreds of people across different worlds. Values emotional and relational drip over monetary engagement.
