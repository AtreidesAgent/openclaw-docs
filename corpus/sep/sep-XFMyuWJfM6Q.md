## Analysis

### Summary
Nathan Windsor, co-founder of Landslide, discusses building an IBC-enabled Avalanche subnet that bridges the Cosmos and Avalanche ecosystems. The conversation covers the deep Cornell University roots connecting both ecosystems, the technical differences between Tendermint and Avalanche consensus, and why institutional adoption requires both permissioned and permissionless infrastructure.

### Topics Covered
- Nathan's path from Cornell biology degree to music therapy to crypto
- Occupy Wall Street as a catalyst for self-sovereign finance interest
- Cornell's role as the academic birthplace of both Avalanche and Cosmos founders
- Deterministic (Tendermint) vs probabilistic (Avalanche) consensus mechanisms
- Avalanche subnets and Cosmos zones as multi-chain architectures
- IBC as a transformative bridging protocol ($2.1B in 30-day volume)
- Permissioned vs permissionless blockchain infrastructure for institutions
- SWIFT being sunset and its national security implications
- Landslide's incentivized testnet and upcoming token

### Dylan's Takes & Opinions
- Compared banning crypto/blockchain in the US to "saying we don't want to be a part of SWIFT in 1970" — framing blockchain interoperability as a national security concern.
- Credited Occupy Wall Street as the genesis of his own pivot to graduate school for urban planning, drawing parallels to Nathan's crypto awakening.
- Noted that banks and financial institutions have been "quietly building" on blockchain for years, citing multiple previous podcast guests who dropped Easter eggs about this.

### Guest Perspectives
**Nathan Windsor** provided a unique perspective as someone embedded in both the Cosmos and Avalanche ecosystems since their earliest days. He explained that DeFi must become compliant to become resilient — an unpopular but necessary stance. He emphasized that Landslide's success would be measured by invisibility: users shouldn't even know they're using it. He was forthcoming about Avalanche's lack of a hard cap on validators as a key differentiator and passionately advocated for IBC as the TCP/IP-equivalent protocol for institutional asset transfer.

### People & Projects Mentioned
- **Landslide** — IBC-enabled Avalanche subnet bridging Cosmos and Avalanche
- **Cornell Blockchain Group** — academic origin of Avalanche (Emin Gün Sirer, Kevin Sekniqi, Ted Yin)
- **Ava Labs** — company behind Avalanche; Morgan Ketky, John Woo, Stephen, Lucian, Patrick O'Grady highlighted
- **Cosmos/Atom** — hub of the IBC ecosystem; jkwon and Ethan Buckman as founders
- **dYdX** — major Cosmos client processing $33M in 7 days via IBC
- **Noble** — IBC chain partnered with Circle, processing $64M in 7 days
- **JP Morgan, Citi, Tro Price, SK Planet, Off the Grid** — institutional Avalanche subnet builders
- **Strange Love** — Cosmos stewards helping Landslide (Jack Samplin, Andrew, Alex)
- **Babylon** — providing Bitcoin staking security to Cosmos chains
- **Halborn, Oak Security** — auditing Landslide's contracts
- **ICF (Interchain Foundation)** — supporting Landslide through builders program

### Recurring Themes
- Cross-chain interoperability as critical infrastructure
- Institutional adoption requiring permissioned environments
- Infrastructure invisibility as the ultimate success metric
- Academic origins influencing blockchain ecosystem design
