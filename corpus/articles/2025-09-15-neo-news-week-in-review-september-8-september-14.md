# Neo News: Week in Review – September 8 – September 14

**Published:** Mon, 15 Sep 2025 13:41:54 +0000
**URL:** https://neonewstoday.com/week-in-review/neo-news-week-in-review-september-8-september-14/

General Updates

Neo announced it is hosting Seoul Pulse: Asian Coin Night afterparty for Korea Blockchain Week 2025 on Sep. 23. The event will feature a performance from Korean rap artist Camo, and is co-hosted alongside Assembly Labs and SpoonOS.

Neo BD manager Denis Suslov will speak at Inno Block 2025 in Singapore on Sep. 30.

Flamingo Finance announced that its DeFi platform was not impacted by the NPM supply chain attack that affected various open-source projects. The team’s review confirmed that no compromised packages were used, and Flamingo’s cross-chain flows are not exposed to the exploit. Users should continue standard precautions when signing wallet transactions. Additionally, Flamingo Finance noted that Epoch 23 concluded, that the total payout was US $4,347.71, and 119,624 FLM were burned.

Centre Point’s hosting committee released an overview of the second Neo community gathering, which will take place in Singapore beginning Sep. 27. The three-day event is designed to strengthen collaboration, beginning with connection and contributor-led discussions, followed by reflection on the Neo Council’s role and responsibilities, and concluding with action planning. Rather than producing a fixed roadmap, the focus is on building clarity, trust, and shared responsibility to help the ecosystem evolve.

AxLabs hosted the first session of Talk is Cheap, a series that seeks to glean insights into the development team’s mindset and how they view writing and shipping code on-chain. The broadcast featured AxLabs founder Guil. Sperb Machado, as well as the rest of the team, including Otniel Nicola, Michael Bucher, Daniel Mark, and Gabriel Lopes.

GrantShares witnessed the approval of Pixudi‘s Centre Point #2 travel grant proposal. Daniel Taiwo‘s travel grant proposal is on-chain and awaiting endorsement. Everstake Head of Staking Mariia Borodaieva submitted a travel grant proposal, but it has not been put on-chain. Lastly, the Vaulten grant proposal‘s funding was released on Sep. 11.

nDapp released a weekly N3 GAS check-in, noting that network users claimed approximately 94,867 GAS in the past week, and the on-chain activity burned about 885 GAS.

Frank Coin hosted a GasBot trivia contest in the FrankCoin channel of the NeoF1 Discord server and distributed 3 GAS in rewards to participants.

NNT Catch Up

NNT hosted Crypto Coffee & Blockchain Beer Space #72 on the official The Smart Economy Podcast X account, and was joined by Silicon Network co-founders Paul Hainsworth and Dylan Rose. Topics of discussion included the evolution of crypto and AI in the San Francisco Bay Area, small to large-scale GPU data centers, GPU NFTs as RWAs, owning underlying physical assets, the legal implications of offering NFTs that represent physical assets, building in the Chia and Base ecosystems, and much more.
