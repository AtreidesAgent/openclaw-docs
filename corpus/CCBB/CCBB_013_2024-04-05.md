## Analysis

### Summary
Episode 13 covers three major topics: Frank coin's integration into Pixudi as a utility token (challenging the "meme coin" label), Poly Network's suspension of cross-chain bridge services creating urgency around Neo's interoperability gaps, and the fallout from a failed GrantShares marketing proposal where community feedback drove the applicant to withdraw. Edge advocates strongly for third-party bridge solutions like Layer Zero alongside IBC protocol compatibility.

### Topics Covered
- Neo N3 statistics (100,808 addresses, block height 5,170,684, approaching 400 contracts)
- Frank coin added to Pixudi for mystery pack purchases — utility beyond meme status
- Meme coin staying power: cycles of hype, Dogecoin as OG example (Jackson Palmer rage-quit)
- Poly Network suspending Poly Bridge cross-chain services effective April 10
- Poly Network clarification: only frontend (Poly Bridge) suspended, not relay infrastructure
- $611M exploit history (#2 largest bridge hack); "Dear Hacker" open letter that worked
- Ghost Market building own bridge contracts on Poly Network relay infrastructure
- For The Win Network bridge to Polygon via NET tokens
- Edge advocates IBC protocol compatibility and third-party bridges (Layer Zero Labs)
- Tyler (COZ CEO) considering own Neon bridge solution due to Poly issues
- Edge warns against individual projects running long-term bridge infrastructure
- Fiat on-ramps and cross-chain solutions both severely lacking for Neo
- Failed GrantShares marketing proposal (~$3,800 NEO / ~$50K): agency withdrew citing "toxic community"
- Dean's constructive feedback on reimagining the proposal
- Neo's existing large-scale PR firm partnership (Da Hongfei in Cointelegraph, conference journalist access)
- Ricardo / Linked Academy as GrantShares recipient doing education outreach

### Dylan's Takes & Opinions
- Frank coin shouldn't be called a meme coin: "for a project that's trying to create so much utility, it belittles or diminishes the reach that it could have" `[10:40]`
- Meme coins "come and go" but never fully cool down; "we're all degenerates in this space" `[5:29]`
- Practical advice on Poly Network: if you need crypto for real-world bills (taxes, rent), "migrate those assets to a place where you can access them for sure" `[20:15]`

- Sees the positive in the failed proposal: community articulated what marketing actually means to them for the first time `[33:54]`
- Notes Neo has worked with a "large scale, high quality PR team" for years — not zero marketing `[35:09]`

### Guest Perspectives
- **Edge** (co-host): Explains Poly Network layered architecture — relay nodes pass messages between chains; bridge contracts lock/release assets on endpoints `[17:59]`.
