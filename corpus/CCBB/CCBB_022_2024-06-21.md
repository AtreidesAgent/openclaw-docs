## Analysis

### Summary
Episode 22 covers a wide range of topics including Edge's work on abstract wallets and the open-source vs. closed-source debate, market cycle normalization advice, Dylan's deep dive into the Hamster Kombat tap-to-earn Telegram game, the SEC permanently suspending its Ethereum/Consensys investigation, the CertiK-Kraken exploit saga, and Neo's ownership of the Oscar-qualifying short film Shadow Brother Sunday.

### Topics Covered
- Edge's abstract wallet research and open-source vs. closed-source considerations
- Market downturns as normal and cyclical — "sell in May and go away"
- Hamster Kombat: a Telegram-based tap-to-earn game launching on the TON network
- SEC permanently suspending its investigation into Consensys/Ethereum — implications for ETH ETFs
- Bitwise releasing its first ETH ETF commercial
- VanEck's mega bull ($140K) and mega bear ($300) ETH price scenarios by 2030
- Solana as the next potential ETF target after ETH
- The CertiK-Kraken saga: white hat rescue gone wrong with Tornado Cash usage
- Taylor Moynihan's on-chain sleuthing revealing the CertiK transactions
- Neo's rights to Shadow Brother Sunday, an Oscar-qualifying short film directed by Alden Ehrenreich

### Dylan's Takes & Opinions
- Dylan advised listeners experiencing market anxiety to "go out and touch grass" — emphasizing this is "normal, par for the course" and that summer months are traditionally quiet.
- He prefers building during bearish sentiment: "it feels like there's a lot less chatter at the water cooler."
- On Hamster Kombat, Dylan admitted "they got me" — appreciating the strategic passive-income mechanics, the crypto-education videos (first video teaches about private keys), and the global news coverage baked into the game.
- He found the SEC's Ethereum investigation suspension momentous, calling the Bitwise ETH ETF commercial "cute" and drawing parallels to old Apple vs. PC ads.
- Dylan expressed excitement about TradFi institutions creating ETF vehicles for digital assets: "when I first got into the space 2017... it felt improbable, impossible."
- On the CertiK/Kraken situation, Dylan questioned whether using Tornado Cash pointed to a rogue agent or "top down ineptitude" at CertiK, noting CertiK is US-based and Tornado Cash is "protocol non grata."
- He highlighted Neo's ownership of Shadow Brother Sunday as significant — "Neo owns rights to a movie" that qualified for Oscar consideration, directed by Alden Ehrenreich (Young Han Solo).

### Guest Perspectives
- **Edge** — Discussed abstract accounts as a way to make Web3 apps "indistinguishable from a Web2 app," removing the need for users to understand private keys. On open-source vs. closed-source, he suggested compromises: publishing code if a business fails, or sharing architecture without implementation details. Provided market cycle wisdom: "no matter where you came in, you can kind of always get a sense of where you'll be able to get out." On ETFs, Edge noted it's a "rising tide, lift all ships" moment. On Shadow Brother Sunday, he got excited about the possibility of the film appearing in the NFI registry explorer. Edge identified Cookie Clicker as the genre Hamster Kombat belongs to.
