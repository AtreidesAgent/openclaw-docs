# On the Brink: Stablecoin Regulation

## 1. OCC Conditional Charters
- Description: The Office of the Comptroller of the Currency (OCC) has issued conditional approvals for national trust bank charters, enabling banks to custody crypto assets and issue stablecoins under federal oversight.
- Evidence: OCC interpretive letters allowing activities like stablecoin issuance with nationwide preemption over state laws, providing a clear federal pathway.

## 2. GENIUS Act Framework
- Description: The proposed GENIUS Act aims to create a comprehensive federal regulatory framework specifically for stablecoins, setting minimum standards for reserves, redemption, and anti-money laundering (AML) compliance.
- Evidence: Bipartisan legislation introduced in Congress, outlining rulemaking processes for reserve composition and operational requirements.

## 3. Yield Prohibition Debate
- Description: Regulators are debating prohibitions on paying yield or interest on stablecoins to distinguish them from money market funds and prevent unintended monetary policy impacts.
- Evidence: Discussions in congressional hearings and regulatory comments emphasizing 'no yield solely for holding' to maintain stablecoin neutrality.

## 4. Reserve Composition Mandates
- Description: Strict mandates limit stablecoin reserves to highly liquid assets like cash, short-term U.S. Treasuries, and repurchase agreements to ensure 1:1 redeemability.
- Evidence: Proposed rules requiring daily attestation and audited reserves, mirroring banking liquidity standards.

## 5. Custody and Segregation Requirements
- Description: Reserves must be held in segregated, bankruptcy-remote accounts with qualified custodians to protect users in case of issuer insolvency.
- Evidence: OCC guidance on custody practices and emphasis on third-party custody models like those used by Circle and Paxos.

## 6. Redemption Rights Assurance
- Description: Issuers must guarantee prompt 1:1 redemption at par value upon demand, with mechanisms for same-day processing.
- Evidence: Regulatory focus on liquidity stress tests and historical runs like Silicon Valley Bank highlighting redemption risks.

## 7. AML/KYC Integration Mandates
- Description: Full compliance with Bank Secrecy Act (BSA) standards, including customer identification, transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting.
- Evidence: FinCEN rules applied to stablecoin issuers as money services businesses (MSBs).

## 8. State vs. Federal Jurisdiction Tension
- Description: Conflicts between state money transmitter licenses (MTLs) and emerging federal charters, with federal preemption gaining traction.
- Evidence: OCC letters overriding state restrictions for federally chartered entities.

## 9. MiCA as Global Model
- Description: The EU's Markets in Crypto-Assets (MiCA) regulation serves as a blueprint, categorizing stablecoins as e-money tokens with strict reserve and transparency rules.
- Evidence: U.S. policymakers referencing MiCA for balanced innovation and stability.

## 10. Circle's Regulated IPO Path
- Description: USDC issuer Circle is positioning for a public IPO by aligning with U.S. regulatory expectations for stablecoin issuers.
- Evidence: S-1 filings and compliance roadmap emphasizing federal charter pursuits.

## 11. Tether's Compliance Evolution
- Description: USDT issuer Tether has shifted from offshore opacity to regular attestations and reserve improvements amid regulatory pressure.
- Evidence: Quarterly transparency reports showing increased Treasury holdings.

## 12. PayPal USD (PYUSD) as Regulated Entry
- Description: PayPal's PYUSD, issued via Paxos, exemplifies fintech entry into regulated stablecoins with full U.S. compliance.
- Evidence: NYDFS oversight and integration into PayPal ecosystem.

## 13. FDIC Pass-Through Insurance Debate
- Description: Questions over whether segregated stablecoin reserves qualify for FDIC pass-through insurance, providing depositor protection.
- Evidence: FDIC letters clarifying crypto custody but excluding algorithmic models.

## 14. Cross-Border Regulatory Harmonization
- Description: Efforts to align U.S. rules with global standards for seamless international stablecoin usage.
- Evidence: G7 and FSB recommendations on global stablecoin frameworks.

## 15. Post-Terra Algorithmic Risks
- Description: Collapse of TerraUSD has led to heightened scrutiny and potential outright bans on uncollateralized algorithmic stablecoins.
- Evidence: SEC actions and calls for collateral-only models.

## 16. Traditional Banks Entering Stablecoins
- Description: Major banks like JPMorgan expanding proprietary stablecoins (JPM Coin) and exploring public ones via subsidiaries.
- Evidence: Pilot programs and regulatory approvals for interbank settlements.

## 17. Consumer Protection Prioritization
- Description: Emphasis on transparency, disclosure, and redeemability to safeguard retail users from issuer failures.
- Evidence: CFPB oversight and proposed consumer disclosure rules.

## 18. Innovation Sandboxes Proposed
- Description: Regulatory sandboxes to test stablecoin innovations under controlled conditions before full market entry.
- Evidence: State-level pilots influencing federal approaches.

## 19. Bipartisan Political Momentum
- Description: Growing bipartisan consensus accelerating stablecoin legislation passage.
- Evidence: House Financial Services Committee advancing multiple bills.

## 20. Institutional Adoption Unlock
- Description: Clear regulations expected to catalyze trillions in institutional capital into stablecoin-based DeFi and payments.
- Evidence: Projections from BlackRock and Fidelity on tokenized money markets.
