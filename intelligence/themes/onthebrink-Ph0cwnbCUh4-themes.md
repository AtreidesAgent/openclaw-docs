# EP.676 - Dave Balter (Flipside) - AI & Blockchain Analytics

**Date:** January 2026 (estimated)
**Video ID:** Ph0cwnbCUh4
**Format:** Guest interview

---

## Episode Summary
Dave Balter, CEO of Flipside Crypto (Castle Island's first investment in 2018), discusses the convergence of AI and blockchain data. The episode covers Flipside's pivot from traditional SQL-based analytics to AI-native workflows via MCP (Model Context Protocol), the death of "dashboards" in favor of conversational intelligence, and what real-vs-fake growth metrics mean for L1/L2 chains in the AI era.

---

## Themes

### #DeFi - Beyond Dashboards to Action
Flipside killed "Rio" (a Twitter-like real-time data feed product) after their COO demonstrated MCP integration allowing natural language queries with instant visualization. Balter argues the analytics industry is shifting from "look back" (historical dashboards) to "look forward" (AI-driven strategic action) — reducing analysis time from hours to minutes.

**Key claim:** "The new world order is we want to track TVL and momentum... you're moving into action stage, not look back, look forward stage."

### #institutional - Workflow Democratization
Flipside's pivot to MCP enables "vibe coding" — non-technical users (marketers, CEOs, strategists) can now perform analysis previously requiring SQL expertise. Analytics becomes accessible to "strategists or the marketers or the CEOs" rather than just analysts. This represents a leveling dynamic similar to the industrial revolution: "machines took the weakest person and the strongest person and made them equal."

**Notable:** Flipside's 600,000 SQL users (via Studio) were told to migrate to Snowflake — a bet that "within a year, every single one of the other data providers are going to be touting their AI system and realizing that their SQL interface is going away."

### #Bitcoin - Data Transparency vs TradFi
The asymmetry between onchain tick-by-tick data (available instantly for Uniswap) and TradFI reporting (quarterly Excel files 1–2 months delayed) creates an intelligence advantage. Balter argues tokenized securities onchain will obsolete legacy reporting infrastructure: "it's incomprehensible to me that we're going to have government agencies with Excel files three months late."

**Timeline:** Real-time onchain analytics is here; TradFI migration to chains "opens everything up — time is shorter, trust is easier."

### #policy - Regulatory Arbitrage & AI Wars
Balter notes that while OpenAI "suck[s] all the money" from the ecosystem, any stumble could "deflate a bunch of stuff" — comparing potential AI market correction to a "housing crisis." Simultaneously, fundraising is hard except for AI-native companies; entrepreneurs must prove traction ("$5M ARR without outside capital") before raising.

**Survival rule:** "Just don't run out of money."

### #macro - L1/L2 Dilemma: Bots vs Real Users
Blockchains face an existential choice: "falsify the real growth" with bot-driven vanity metrics (wash trading, "growth tactics") or accept slower "real human" metrics that "dashboard doesn't like." Flipside analyzes 700M addresses daily to identify "performant" vs bot activity. The second camp (real users) risks looking like failure to naive investors.

**Prediction:** Many premium L1/L2s built for "decentralized internet architecture" (file storage, decentralized compute) that aren't "store of money" or transaction-focused must "shift entirely" or face irrelevance.

### #VC - AI-Native Data Infrastructure
Flipside's thesis: "workflows are the things that matter" — not just data volume. Companies dumping data into LLMs without structured workflows will hit hallucination/errors. Flipside is betting on "the best in the world at AI systems integrating with big data workflows" specifically for blockchain.

**Key insight:** "You could build Google right now... the problem is you don't know the potholes they've stepped in already."

### #stablecoins - Intelligence Layer
Stablecoins are the bridge asset bringing TradFI onchain. Analysis must move beyond simple volume/TVL to behavioral intelligence: "who's using it, what is it indicating the change to traditional finance is happening."

---

## Notable Predictions
1. **SQL obsolescence:** Within 12 months, traditional SQL analytics interfaces will be abandoned for AI-native query systems across data providers
2. **Workflows > Data:** The competitive moat shifts from data access to structured AI workflows (pothole knowledge)
3. **Prediction markets evolution:** Current platforms are "one-dimensional"; AI systems will automate predictive betting at scale
4. **L1 consolidation:** Chains not achieving "store of money" or real business usage must pivot or fade; bot-driven metrics will be exposed
