## Analysis

### Summary
Episode 5 at ETH Boulder with Adam Dean Wozni, one of the event's emcees, discussing his journey from ConsenSys to the Cosmos ecosystem and his deep focus on community building. Adam shares his philosophy that a true community can self-organize and self-manage, compares online and offline community dynamics, and reflects on the strong 2017-2018 OG vibes he's getting at ETH Boulder — a sign that the event is attracting genuine believers rather than hype-chasers.

### Topics Covered
- Adam's experience emceeing ETH Boulder — public goods funding talks, strong attendance
- ETH Boulder's intimate, 2017-2018 OG vibes versus large-scale events
- Adam's career path from ConsenSys (late 2017) to the Cosmos ecosystem for decentralized infrastructure
- The Cosmos SDK and its app-chain architecture
- Decentralized cloud compute — motivation was that 70-80% of Ethereum nodes were on AWS
- Philosophy of community: not a real community until it can self-organize and self-manage
- Online vs. offline community building — Discord, Telegram, Twitter as starting points leading to IRL meetups
- The "first principles" buzzword debate (Adam's 2026 predictions post listed it as overused)
- Personal life balance — young child, wife also works in crypto

### Dylan's Takes & Opinions
- **Loves being the dumbest guy at the table**: "Awesome, yeah, I love being the dumbest guy at the table" — when Adam says 90% of conversations go over his head, Dylan relates.
- **Year in review vs. predictions**: "I'm more of a year in review kind of guy" — positions himself as analytical rather than speculative.
- **Community definition challenge**: Observes that community is "one of those words that's just thrown around so much no one knows what it is."
- **Online community is often just social media management**: Questions whether community is really just "telegram channels, or Discord servers, or like a Twitter presence."

### Guest Perspectives
- **Adam Dean Wozni** (Community builder, former ConsenSys): Got into crypto at ConsenSys in late 2017 ("Uncle Joe" Lubin still follows him). Defines community as something that can self-organize and self-manage — his role is to get the ball rolling and then enable members to host their own events and share ideas. Cites David Spinks (CMX founder) as an unconscious mentor. Getting strong 2017-2018 vibes at ETH Boulder. Has a 1.5-year-old child and his wife also works in crypto.
