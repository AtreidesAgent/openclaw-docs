## Analysis

### Summary
Ingo Ruebe, founder of Kilt Protocol, discusses building decentralized identity solutions for enterprises, governments, and consumers. The conversation covers how identity is fundamentally broken in web2, the critical difference between identity and identification, why Kilt chose Polkadot as its native chain, the four primary Kilt products, where ENS got it wrong, and how AI agents create urgent new identity requirements.

### Topics Covered
- Ingo's career spanning healthcare, media, energy, and ultimately blockchain identity
- The 2015 South by Southwest Ethereum presentation as the aha moment
- Why South by Southwest is preferred over crypto conferences for forward-looking insights
- GDPR as a catalyst: discovering 126 databases of personal data at a 15,000-employee company
- Identity vs identification: a crucial distinction (what others say about you vs proving you are you)
- The physical world's 500-year-old credential system and how the internet broke it
- Germany's historical sensitivity to data collection (80 years ago reference)
- Social KYC: internet-native identity using Twitter, GitHub, email verification
- W3N naming service: improving on ENS by linking to DIDs instead of crypto addresses
- Kilt's Polkadot roots: Gavin Wood answering questions in Element within 5 minutes
- Now supporting Ethereum and Base for institutional integration
- AI agents needing verifiable credentials, agency, and liability
- Bonding curves as upcoming Kilt feature for DAO building
- The Berlin blockchain scene in 2018 vs the "get rich" shift that followed

### Dylan's Takes & Opinions
- Called identity "one of the least sexy topics in crypto, but massively important in our day-to-day lives."
- Shared that at a recent Farcaster meetup at ETH Denver, the best conversations were about "everything but blockchain and crypto" — philosophy, medicine, relationships — supporting Ingo's point about attending non-crypto events.
- Noted that AI agents may mean "the first billion users might not even be human" — a mind-warping realization from a Consensus talk.

### Guest Perspectives
**Ingo Ruebe** drew a stark warning from German history: "80 years ago there were big problems in this country. They didn't have computers luckily, but if they had Facebook or Google... it would have been so easy to find out the people they don't like." He criticized ENS for being "so wrong in many ways" — fundamentally flawed because it links names to crypto addresses (like linking identity to a bank account) instead of abstracting to DIDs. He was candid that the 2018 Berlin blockchain scene devolved from "crazy people on dirty sofas building things" to people just wanting to get rich. On AI agents: he argued they need credentials, auditability, and liability — "you want to sue the agent" if something goes wrong.
