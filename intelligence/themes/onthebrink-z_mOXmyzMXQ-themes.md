# EP.680 - Gita Panchapakasan (Tesser) - Enabling FIs to Use Stablecoins

**Date:** January 2026 (estimated)
**Video ID:** z_mOXmyzMXQ
**Format:** Guest interview

## Episode Summary
Gita Panchapakasan, CEO of Tesser, discusses building infrastructure to enable traditional financial institutions (banks, PSPs, MSPs) to integrate stablecoin payments. With 20 years in payments (Mastercard, PayPal, MoneyGram, Visa, Circle), she explains the slow FI adoption journey, infrastructure fragmentation challenges, and how the GENIUS Act is accelerating conversations despite not solving all custody/accounting questions.

---

## Themes

### #stablecoins - FI-Ready Infrastructure
Tesser provides "one-stop shop" stablecoin infrastructure for traditional institutions that "move trillions of dollars today, but they don't move it very efficiently." The core offering packages custody (multiple options: custodial, MPC), on/off-ramps (matched to geography/risk), treasury/gas management, and reconciliation/reporting. FIs can go live in 2–4 weeks instead of 12+ months building internally.

**Key insight:** "The infrastructure exists but it's very fragmented" — custody, on-ramps, chains, tokens, gas are scattered. Tesser aggregates this into a single API/platform for regulated institutions.

### #payments - Settlement as the Killer Use Case
Panchapakasan identifies settlement (specifically nostro accounts elimination) as the "very big" stablecoin use case for banks. Traditional correspondent banking requires pre-funded accounts in multiple currencies with multiple partners — working capital headaches and treasury pain. Stablecoins enable "bearer instrument" settlement where value is transferred instantly without pre-funding, obsoleting the "accounts everywhere, friends in other banks, hold funds in all these places" model.

**Real-world tie:** At MoneyGram, spent 6 months with a Philippines bank partner figuring out working capital, interest, and cash-out timing — contrasted with stablecoin instant settlement.

### #institutional - The FI Adoption Spectrum
FIs range across the maturity curve:
- **Early movers:** WorldPay, Visa, Strike were looking 2–3 years ago
- **PSPs:** Stripe/Bridge acquisition accelerated partnerships; many "trying to figure out what makes sense"
- **Banks:** More cautious due to regulatory impact; "Standard Chartered got into the game early" but most still determining stablecoin strategy
- **Laggards:** "Still some that need a stablecoin 101"

**Barriers:** Banks specifically cautious about custody/accounting questions — "I don't know if I can hold stables, can you find me a way in which somebody can hold it on my behalf."

### #policy - The GENIUS Act Paradox
The GENIUS Act is "focused on issuance" (PPSI requirements) but doesn't address the operational questions banks face: "How does a bank account for stablecoins and what can they lend against it and how does it look on their balance sheet?" Despite this limitation, "mere fact of the existence of the GENIUS Act has actually opened up a lot of doors" — legitimacy signal for legal departments.

**Comparison:** MiCA is "much more comprehensive" but still focused differently than bank operational requirements. The US standard-setting effect means "other countries are also at least willing to engage and talk about it."

### #macro - Dollarization Demand
Beyond traditional dollarization narratives (Argentina), demand is emerging in developed markets: Wise announced a new hire to explore "digital holding strategy" — enabling customers to hold funds in dollars. "There's actually a lot of demand for holding dollars globally" — stablecoins as the simplest mechanism for this access.

### #DeFi - Web2 → Web3 Infrastructure Evolution
Technical maturity enabling FI readiness:
- **Scalability:** L2s now compete with Visa's 65,000 TPS; Ethereum's "15 TPS" days are over
- **Custody evolution:** Shift from Bitcoin-only "not-easy-to-move" Anchorage models to payment-optimized MPC/options
- **On/off-ramps:** Ecosystem growth means Tesser can credibly offer banks "we connect you to the ecosystem because it's there"

**Trust factor:** Enforcement of AML/KYC obligations on exchanges "has brought in a certain amount of trust into the ecosystem" — critical for FI comfort.

### #VC - Seed Stage Efficiency
Tesser raised $4.5M seed (Castle Island led, with Strobe, Anthemus, DCG). Currently 5.5–6 people. Engineering team "trying to see how much they can use AI to keep it tight" — lean operations philosophy.

## Notable Predictions
1. **Nostro obsolescence:** Banks will eliminate pre-funded correspondent accounts as stablecoin settlement becomes default
2. **FI infrastructure commoditization:** Packaging fragmented crypto infra into FI-ready platforms is the immediate opportunity; 12-month build cycles become 2–4 weeks
3. **Regulatory clarity asymmetry:** GENIUS Act issuance clarity ≠ bank operational clarity; custody/accounting standards still developing
4. **Global dollar access:** Stablecoins become the standard mechanism for global dollar savings beyond traditional banking rails
