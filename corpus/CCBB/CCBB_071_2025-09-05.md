## Analysis

### Summary
Solo episode 71 covers Erik Zhang's potential return to Neo (his "What about Neo4?" comment on GitHub), CenterPoint 2 planning for Singapore, GrantShares travel grant approvals for four community members, Vaultin receiving GrantShares funding approval, AxLabs launching its "Talk is Cheap" broadcast series, and the ZK DKG protocol integration into Neo X testnet v0.4.1.

### Topics Covered
- Neo X testnet v0.4.1: fully audited ZK DKG protocol integrated for anti-MEV
- Erik Zhang's return to Neo GitHub and his provocative "What about Neo4?" comment
- Dylan's extensive historical analysis of Neo N3's launch timing and its impact on the ecosystem
- CenterPoint 2 planning: expanded to 3 days, focus on empowering new attendees and addressing council free-loading
- GrantShares travel grants approved for Alvaro, Abdul Karim, Riley, and Robin
- Vaultin game received 7 votes and approved for GrantShares funding
- AxLabs launching "Talk is Cheap" broadcast series starting September 9
- Neo Legacy deprecation reminder: October 31
- NeoNS domain activity up 195%

### Dylan's Takes & Opinions
- **On Erik Zhang's return**: Welcomes it but has "mixed feelings" about Neo4 — has "PTSD flashbacks from Neo N3." Provides extensive historical analysis of N3's timeline failures.
- **On N3's failure**: "NEO's success in 2017 and 2018 was because it was ready at the right time. NEO's failure in 2021 and 2022 was because it was improperly positioned at the wrong time." Blames timing, not technology.
- **On Neo4 specifically**: Compares his reaction to "Chihuahua with the army helmet on stuck in the middle of a helicopter fight in Vietnam meme."
- **On CenterPoint 2 goals**: Focused on creating space for newcomers to feel empowered to participate, confronting council free-loaders who "just collecting a free paycheck," and walking away with "objective, measurable steps."
- **On council governance**: "The powers in the hands of the community and the developers" — wants the community to be able to replace defunct council members.
- **On pseudonymous builders**: Praised Vaultin's anonymous dev as "a nice kind of like nod to our past" referencing Satoshi Nakamoto.
- **On long-term survival**: "The way to survive in blockchain and crypto is to play long-term games with long-term people."
