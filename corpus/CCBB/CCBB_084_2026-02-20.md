## Analysis

### Summary
Recording live from ETH Denver week, Dylan shares his on-the-ground experience at both ETH Boulder (an intimate 200-person unconference) and ETH Denver (in its ninth year). He highlights the value of bear-market conferences for finding long-term builders, debuts the "Crypto Talk & Blockchain Walk" interview series, and argues that despite bearish sentiment on crypto Twitter, the builders who show up during downturns are the signal worth following.

### Topics Covered
- ETH Boulder unconference format: 200 attendees, vote-on-talks, intimate setting
- Naomi Brockwell's privacy talk: "Privacy has been framed as guilt and compliance has been labeled as virtuous"
- App developers inserting third-party data-scraping SDKs for $3-5K/month passive income
- Signal as a privacy-preserving alternative to Telegram/WhatsApp/Messenger
- Debut of "Crypto Talk & Blockchain Walk" interview series — walk-and-talk format
- ETH Denver: ninth year, smaller than previous years, fewer sponsors, new single venue
- Bear market indicators: fewer sponsors, less side events, less party culture
- "Fair weather friends" — people who only attend crypto events during bull runs
- Interviews with Kent Barton (ShapeShift), Austin Griffith (EF), Alicia (Botanics), and a crypto OG woman who flipped rare Pepe NFTs
- Austin Griffith on X402 and EIP-8004: protocols for AI agent wallets and commerce
- Bear market thesis: we've been in one since early 2025 despite Bitcoin ATH
- Bitcoin and USDT as best-performing assets; Bitcoin Cash oddly strong
- "Liberation Day" tariffs referenced as market inflection
- Long-term builder signal vs. short-term tourist noise
- Advice: develop your thesis now for 1-3-5 year positioning
