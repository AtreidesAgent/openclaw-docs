## Analysis

### Summary
Dylan speaks with JP Thor, the pseudonymous founder and "general herder of cats" at THORChain, about cross-chain liquidity, the future of self-custodial wallets, and the philosophy driving decentralized infrastructure. The conversation covers THORChain's evolution from testnet to powering most L1-to-L1 swaps in the industry, JP's background as an Australian Air Force fighter pilot, his helicopter adventures, the upcoming Rira Alliance app layer (incorporating Kujira), and Vultisig's threshold signature wallet that aims to replace hardware wallets entirely.

### Topics Covered
- Dylan's experience as a 3.5-year THORChain user: from crypto-native testnet (BEPSwap) to near-custodial-exchange-level UX
- THORChain's near-monopoly on L1-to-L1 swap routes; wallets integrating THORChain-powered swaps
- Affiliate fee model: wallets earning revenue by integrating THORChain (e.g., Trust Wallet making ~$1M/month)
- JP's origin story: 12 years as Australian Air Force pilot (fighters and bombers), discovered Bitcoin at a tech startup pitch night in August 2013 at $150
- JP's early Bitcoin prediction: $1M by 2023 — "a factor of 10 out, still too optimistic"
- Trying to build a Bitcoin wallet iOS app in 2013; Apple blanket-banned all Bitcoin apps
- THORChain's censorship resistance: nodes on bare metal, no leverage points (no app stores, no hosting dependencies)
- Address screening will happen at the interface layer (centralized apps) but never at the protocol layer
- SwapKit: API for wallets to integrate THORChain, Maya, Chainflip swap capabilities
- Rira Alliance and THORChain app layer: coordinated deployment of DeFi protocols, not random apps
- Kujira migration to THORChain: JP personally backstopped Kujira during liquidity crisis; team brings 3 years of app-chain experience
- Rugi token: fee-switch real yield token for the app layer; designed to complement (not compete with) RUNE
- Fat protocol thesis: ensuring base layer (THORChain nodes) always gets paid through gas fees and liquidity fees
- Vultisig: threshold signature wallet replacing hardware wallets; no private keys, multiparty computation, two-factor cosigning
- Vultisig security: full transaction details on phone screen for cosigning (vs. blind signing on Ledger), no private keys to steal
- JP's helicopter collection: Aston Martin Edition AC130; flew from England to Australia via Egypt, Saudi Arabia, India, Thailand
- Planned polar circumnavigation flight in 2026 with two helicopters
- JP's philosophy: purpose is propagating useful memes (information) for humanity; THORChain and Vultisig are "memes" worth spreading

### Dylan's Takes & Opinions
- Dylan has been a THORChain user since BEPSwap testnet (~3.5 years) and considers the UX now "almost comparable to a custodial exchange."
- Dylan was introduced to THORChain by "Eric Voorhees and the ShapeShift boys" and has been "hooked since day one."
- Dylan attended Rare Evo in Vegas where he rode in the THORChain Cybertruck and met Eldorado DEX team.
- Dylan entered crypto in 2018 and relates to JP's non-CS background.
- Dylan sees THORChain's economic incentive alignment (affiliate fees, RUNE security model) as "very first principles."
- Dylan visited ~9 universities on a campus tour ~2 years ago and met students building products.

### Guest Perspectives
**JP Thor** (Founder, THORChain):
- THORChain has "won" — swap routes, SwapKit integrations, and wallet agreements confirm its position; "once these wallets plumb into these networks, they're not going to unplumb."
- Still only "50% there" — at least 4 more years of building to rival centralized exchanges.
- On motivation: "It's never finished. It's never the destination, it's always the journey." Purpose is propagating useful information ("memes") for humanity.
- On censorship resistance: to censor THORChain at the base layer you'd have to "censor the entire internet" — nodes run on bare metal with direct internet connections.
- Single-signature wallets are dangerous; threshold signatures (Vultisig) are the proper solution for self-custody.
- Vultisig eliminates private keys entirely via multiparty computation; cosigning on iPhone gives full transaction visibility vs. blind signing on Ledger.
- Fat protocol thesis for THORChain: apps must send revenue to the base layer to avoid parasitic dynamics.
- Kujira was a "perfect marriage" — a team that spent 3 years building apps on an app chain, now migrating to THORChain's base layer.
- Bitcoin will hit $1M by end of 2030; "I'm a factor of 10 out" from his 2013 prediction.
- Bitcoin white paper embedded in macOS was likely "a rogue Apple employee" giving a middle finger to Craig Wright's lawsuits.
