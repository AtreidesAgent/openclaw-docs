## Analysis

### Summary
Dylan interviews Fisher Yu, CTO and co-founder of Babylon Labs, about turning Bitcoin into a first-class staking asset through a non-custodial staking protocol. The conversation traces Babylon's multi-year R&D journey from merge mining to Bitcoin timestamping to Bitcoin staking, explains the three-phase mainnet rollout, and discusses how institutions and retail users are locking billions of dollars of BTC to provide proof-of-stake security to other networks.

### Topics Covered
- Fisher's academic background: PhD in Australia, Dolby Labs (decentralized media delivery), collaboration with Professor David Tse
- Babylon's R&D evolution: merge mining (abandoned due to nothing-at-stake), Bitcoin timestamping (solved long-range attacks but too slow), then Bitcoin staking
- Bitcoin staking as Bitcoin's "third native use case" beyond store of value and payments
- Three-phase mainnet rollout: Phase 1 (supply/stakers), Phase 2 (Babylon chain/marketplace), Phase 3 (multi-staking/demand)
- Cap system: Cap 1 (1,000 BTC, filled instantly), Cap 2 (>$2B in 1.5 hours), Cap 3 ongoing
- 10 block confirmation requirement for staking activation (vs. typical 6, erring on caution)
- Points system mimicking reward distribution while Phase 1 has no chains to secure
- Finality providers: validators backed by Bitcoin stake; liquid staking protocols create their own
- Target consumers: Cosmos chains, Ethereum rollups, Bitcoin rollups (BOB, Citrea, Chakra, NewBit, BitLayer)
- TVL of $3.36B with 66,800+ stakers at time of recording
- Custodial vs. non-custodial distinction: custody is the least risky counterparty; the real risks are intermediaries
- Bitcoin ETF institutions and non-US market dynamics
- Bitcoin decentralization: chain maintained by miners, asset centralization is secondary

### Dylan's Takes & Opinions
- Dylan has been following Babylon since the light paper was released in summer 2023, calling it a project that's been "on my radar for a long time."
- He asks a "larger scale existential question" about Bitcoin centralization by institutions (MicroStrategy, BlackRock, nation-state reserves) — showing concern about co-option.
- Dylan notes that as a US citizen he cannot stake on Babylon, lamenting regulatory exclusion.
- He calls Bitcoin staking "a layer zero social consensus for bridging Bitcoin into other blockchain ecosystems."

### Guest Perspectives
**Fisher Yu (CTO, Babylon Labs):** Fisher explains that Babylon went through three protocol iterations over three years before arriving at Bitcoin staking. He frames Bitcoin staking as extracting Bitcoin's economic security at the speed of proof-of-stake (seconds) rather than proof-of-work (10+ minutes). He emphasizes the phased, cautious approach — each cap followed by security audits and bug bounties. On centralization concerns, he argues the Bitcoin chain is what matters: "as long as the miners are not controlled by a country or a company, the Bitcoin chain is decentralized." He sees 2025 as the "genesis year of trustless Bitcoin DeFi" and predicts staking adoption will steadily increase as larger institutions complete their integration processes.

### People & Projects Mentioned
