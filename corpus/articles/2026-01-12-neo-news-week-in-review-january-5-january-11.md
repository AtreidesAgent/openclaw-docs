# Neo News: Week in Review – January 5 – January 11

**Published:** Mon, 12 Jan 2026 14:41:39 +0000
**URL:** https://neonewstoday.com/week-in-review/neo-news-week-in-review-january-5-january-11/
**Tags:** Week In Review, FRANK, ndapp, NDMEME, SpoonOS

General Updates

Neo issued a warning that an account on X was impersonating an employee of Neo Global Development. The individual in question is not affiliated with Neo in any capacity; they are neither an employee nor a partner.

SpoonOS released a bi-weekly report, which noted multi-chain signing across EVM, Solana, and Neo, as well as deeper agent reasoning and memory capabilities via DeepAgent and RAG integration. The report also highlighted offline tool calling with Ollama, ERC-8004 trustless agent support, and new visual multimodal capabilities.

SpoonOS core contributor Raymond participated in a panel titled “2026 Market Outlook: AI, Prediction Markets and Value Strategies.”

NeoDashboard bought and burned approximately 71,500 NDMEME tokens from the total supply.

NeoRedPill announced a Telegram Contribution Event, where the Top 3 winners will share from a pool of 25 GAS. To be eligible, participants must say “GM NRP” once a day to earn 2 points, chat in the group once a day, and invite friends with a link. The event is live until Jan. 21.

nDapp released a weekly N3 GAS check-in, noting that network users claimed approximately 62,801 GAS in the past week, and that on-chain activity burned about 307 GAS.

NNT hosted a GasBot Trivia round where participants had a chance to compete for a pool of 4 GAS rewards.

Frank Coin hosted a GasBot trivia contest on the FrankCoin channel of the NeoF1 Discord server and distributed 3 GAS in rewards to participants.

NNT Catch Up

NNT hosted Crypto Coffee and Blockchain Beer Spaces #79 on the official The Smart Economy Podcast X account. Topics of discussion included the history of Neo and the ideas behind its founding, the recent public battle between the Neo co-founders, trends that can be improved in Neo, and a trifecta for growth that includes RWAs, stablecoins, and DeFi.

In Case You Missed It

Neo and SpoonOS announced the winners of the Scoop AI Hackathon’s Seoul Bowl, a two‑day event held Dec. 20-21 in Seocho‑gu, Seoul. The Seoul Bowl brought together developers, researchers, and creators to build AI applications that utilize the SpoonOS framework. More than US $8,000 in prizes were awarded across grand, track, and special award categories.
