## Analysis

### Summary
Episode 34 is a crisis-focused episode addressing Poly Network's announcement that it will permanently cease all operations by the end of September 2024, leaving the Neo ecosystem without its primary bridge. Dylan and Edge review all three Poly Network hacks, discuss the dire situation for US-based NEO holders who now have extremely limited on/off-ramp options, and look for silver linings including NGD's awareness of the problem and the upcoming Block Spirits activation at Token 2049.

### Topics Covered
- Poly Network announcing full termination of services by end of September
- Review of three Poly Network hacks: $600M (Aug 2021, returned), $10M (Jul 2023), ~$3M+ (Aug 2024, still at large)
- The isolation of US-based NEO holders — crypto.com as the only custodial on/off-ramp
- Astro Neo's bridge to BNB chain and For The Win Network's bridge as alternatives (both with liquidity/slippage issues)
- Ghost Market's dependence on Poly Network
- Neo X as a potential path to EVM bridges but still early (less than 100K gas migrated)
- Best bridging practices in the industry — Edge argues there are none yet, all popular bridges are "glorified multi-sigs"
- Privileged information that NGD leadership is aware and actively addressing the crisis
- Silver linings: Grind Hackathon, Token 2049 platinum sponsorship, Block Spirits activation
- The Block Spirits V2 activation at Token 2049 with lanyards for identification and battle rewards

### Dylan's Takes & Opinions
- Dylan called it "kind of a worst time for something like this to happen for Neo" — right on the precipice of building out Neo X.
- He publicly stated "shame on that team" regarding Poly Network's failure to acknowledge the third hack in their shutdown announcement.
- On US holders: "for US residents right now, if you want to get in or out of Neo, the only way you can do so is by using crypto.com" — describing their fees as "predatory" at 5-7%.
- Dylan emphasized he "raised the flag of concern" at the Singapore meeting and "bitched on our behalf" for American holders.
- He framed the crisis optimistically: "the worst that could have happened has already happened. And the only way forward right now is up."
- On the Block Spirits V2 activation at Token 2049: excited about the improved lanyard system enabling conference-wide identification and battles.

### Guest Perspectives
- **Edge** — Acknowledged Poly Network's shutdown forces Neo Foundation/NGD to "start conversations with teams that have been operating stable, trusted, non-hacked bridges." Noted that bridging via Neo X to EVM bridges is possible but adds an extra gas fee hop. On bridging standards: "I would argue that we still don't really have them" — every popular bridge is a glorified multi-sig. Suggested the best bridges are "the ones which have already been exploited several times because at least those teams have had the chance to learn." He noted Poly Network cited "changing market conditions" without mentioning the third hack. On the crisis: "These things will happen. It will just take a little bit of time. Diamond hands, guys."

### People & Projects Mentioned
- **Poly Network** — Permanently shutting down all operations by end of September after three major hacks
- **Flamingo Finance** — Neo's DeFi platform; completely caught off guard by Poly Network's announcement; created the Asset Support Initiative to compensate users
- **Astro Neo** — Has a bridge to BNB chain; tested by Dylan successfully but limited by liquidity/slippage
- **For The Win Network** — Has a bridge (bNeo to NAP on Polygon/Ethereum) independent of Poly Network
- **Ghost Market** — NFT marketplace dependent on Poly Network infrastructure

- **Nick Carter** — Released a stablecoin research paper "Stablecoins: The Emerging Market Story"
- **Ken Timsit / Kronos Labs** — Smart Economy Podcast episode just released about crypto.com's tri-chain ecosystem
