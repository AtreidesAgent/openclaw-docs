## Analysis

### Summary
Episode 4 features guest Michael Fabozzi, creator of Quirky Soul College and several Neo ecosystem tools, discussing what attracts and retains developers in the Neo ecosystem. The conversation centers on NeoX's potential to onboard EVM developers, the underappreciated value of Neo's native yield from the dual-token model (compared to Blast's hype), and practical challenges like rising GAS costs affecting user interactions. Michael shares his roadmap for expanding Quirky Soul College to NeoX and emphasizes that developer-first growth is the path to ecosystem health.

### Topics Covered
- Neo N3 network statistics (block height 4,834,561; 93,850 addresses; +1,200 this week)
- Quirky Soul College roadmap update: final mint wave and new game mode
- Geth client diversity follow-up (84% → mid-70s%, stalled)
- NeoX as a strategy to attract EVM developers
- NGD Eco Growth's rationale for building an EVM sidechain (developer onboarding friction)
- Neo's native yield (~20% APR from GAS generation) vs. Blast's native yield hype
- Blast's marketing spend and Paradigm backing as adoption drivers
- Berachain's dual-token model and proof-of-liquidity parallels to Neo
- Developer motivation and what attracted Michael to Neo (hackathon, tutorials, C# simplicity)
- NeoX's planned native features: built-in random number generation, native oracles, native bridge
- Neo's dual-token model as a potential strategic advantage (GAS for utility, NEO for governance/yield)
- APAC hackathon recap: roughly even split between NeoVM and NeoEVM projects
- Community awareness gap: ecosystem members unaware NeoX was used in the hackathon
- Question from CitizenK89: Will NEO token be irrelevant on NeoX?
- CarrotSwap as early NeoX builder
- Rising GAS costs affecting Quirky Soul College user interactions
