## Analysis

### Summary
Dylan speaks with Sarah Thiam, a developer advocate for the Filecoin Virtual Machine (FVM) at Protocol Labs, about the significance of combining smart contracts with decentralized storage. The conversation covers Sarah's background at Microsoft and GovTech Singapore, the architecture of FVM and its relationship to IPFS and Filecoin, why programmable storage is a game-changer, the early builder cohort of 100+ teams, grant programs, and the March 2023 mainnet launch of the Ethereum-compatible version of FVM.

### Topics Covered
- Sarah's background: product marketing at Microsoft, devrel, GovTech Singapore international developer relations
- The difference between decentralized and centralized approaches to tech development
- Protocol Labs ecosystem: IPFS (peer-to-peer file system) vs. Filecoin (blockchain storage network with incentivization)
- FVM architecture: a new programmability layer for Filecoin, not a separate project but a feature of the network
- FEVM (Filecoin EVM): Ethereum-compatible version allowing Solidity developers to deploy on Filecoin
- Native FVM planned for later 2023 with more granular storage management
- VM-agnostic strategy: Ethereum first, then Polygon, potentially Solana
- Early builder cohort: 100-115 teams building for 5+ months, weekly touchpoints
- Example projects: Hot Storage (perpetual storage with access control), Guardian View (whistleblower platform), data DAOs
- Grant programs: rolling grants ($5K micro-grants), hackathons (250+ submissions), accelerator partnerships
- Saturn: decentralized data retrieval layer on top of Filecoin
- March 14, 2023 (Pi Day) mainnet launch for FEVM
- Goal: 100 dapps deployed to mainnet within first month

### Dylan's Takes & Opinions
- Dylan drew parallels between his urban planning background and smart cities: "one of the things we were really talking about six seven years ago were smart cities and ways in which we can integrate technology."
- He "buys into the multi-language thesis" for blockchain development: "why force them to learn a blockchain-specific language like Solidity?"
- Dylan noted that Filecoin has been around his entire time in crypto but "things are really starting to kick up" with FVM.
- He referenced the Neo ecosystem's John deVadoss (ex-Microsoft, built .NET from zero to $500M/year) as someone who champions polyglot development.

### Guest Perspectives
**Sarah Thiam (Developer Advocate, Protocol Labs/FVM):** Sarah described the transition from Microsoft's centralized developer relations to Protocol Labs' open-source, decentralized approach as fundamentally different: products are co-built with the community in rapid feedback loops rather than shipped as finished products. She emphasized that FVM will be a "game changer" because for the first time, programmability and storage exist in the same environment. She believes centralized and decentralized systems will need to work together rather than one replacing the other. She highlighted Guardian View (a secure whistleblowing platform) as an example of real-world impact being built on FVM.

### People & Projects Mentioned
- **Protocol Labs** - Open source R&D lab behind IPFS and Filecoin
- **IPFS** - Interplanetary File System for peer-to-peer file sharing
- **Filecoin** - Blockchain-based decentralized storage network
- **FVM / FEVM** - Filecoin Virtual Machine and its Ethereum-compatible version
- **Juan Benet** - Protocol Labs CEO/founder
- **Hot Storage** - Perpetual storage platform with encryption and access control
- **Guardian View** - Whistleblower platform built on FVM
- **Saturn** - Decentralized data retrieval layer (L2 on Filecoin)
- **BNB Greenfield** - Referenced as building similar programmability on storage
- **John deVadoss** - Neo ecosystem thought leader, ex-Microsoft .NET

### Recurring Themes
- Adding programmability to decentralized storage as a paradigm shift
- Meeting developers where they are with familiar tooling (EVM compatibility first)
- Open source community co-development vs. centralized product shipping
- Real-world use cases for decentralized storage beyond just "storing files"
- Grant programs and hackathons as developer ecosystem growth engines
- The complementary nature of centralized and decentralized systems
- Multi-chain, VM-agnostic strategies for maximum developer reach

### Content Ideas & Future Topics
- Dylan expressed interest in following the FVM mainnet launch and the 100-dapp deployment goal.
- The native FVM with more granular storage control could warrant a follow-up.
